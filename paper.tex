\begin{abstract}
We evaluate a continuous hidden Markov model (CHMM) trained by Baum--Welch as a synthetic-data generator for daily US-equity returns, on SPY 2014--2026 (body window) and 1994--2006 (cross-decade validation via CRSP), a sector-balanced 30-ticker panel, and a six-asset US-equity copula extension. The empirical scope is the three symmetric Cont (2001) stylized facts (heavy-tailed marginals, negligible linear ACF, slow $|G_t|$ ACF); the leverage effect and gain-loss asymmetry are out of scope under the body's symmetric per-state emissions and partially captured via state-mixing only on the IS window (Appendix~\ref{sec:leverage_effect}). A unified scaffold supports four interchangeable emission families (Gaussian, Student-$t$, Laplace, Generalised Error Distribution) sharing identical forward-backward recursions and quantile-based initialisation; the M-step is the only architectural difference.

At the state-resolution-robust held-out operating point $K^\star = 3$ (selected by held-out per-observation log-likelihood and held-out KS on the strictly pre-2020 slice under both single-fold and four-fold / six-fold rolling-origin CV), the four CHMM variants attain $79.8$--$90.6\%$ IS / $63.1$--$83.2\%$ OoS Kolmogorov--Smirnov pass rates on the single SPY OoS window, with CHMM-N at $89.7\%$ IS / $80.5\%$ OoS and the penalised CHMM-t at $\lambda = 20$ providing the cleanest IS / OoS heavy-tail match at simulated excess kurtosis $14.91$ / $8.50$ against observed $7.68$ / $5.29$. A $K^\star = 6$ block is reported as a sensitivity reference (single-fold pre-2020 selection; not preferred over $K^\star = 3$ outside sampling error). The OoS window sits at the upper end of a six-fold rolling-origin walk-forward whose CHMM-N median OoS KS is $67.7\%$ at $K = 18$ and $62.1\%$ at $K^\star = 3$ (two stress folds, COVID and 2022 rate-hike, below $10\%$); single-window panel rankings are walk-forward-fragile and the walk-forward median is the operational summary. CHMM and ML HSMM are complementary scaffolds: ML HSMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ leads single-window OoS KS at $91.0\%$ but its $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE matches the i.i.d.\ baseline.

A regime-conditional Value-at-Risk that propagates the one-step-ahead state forecast through the CHMM mixture passes the Christoffersen joint conditional-coverage test at $\alpha = 0.05$ on the headline OoS window and on $19/24$ walk-forward folds; the three persistent walk-forward rejections concentrate on the W2 (COVID) and W4 (2022 rate-hike onset) stress folds at $p < 10^{-3}$ in each case, so the conditional-VaR value proposition is differentiating only under stationary OoS conditions. The textbook bilinear ACF identity recasts the SPY instance of the Ryd\'{e}n et al.\ (1998) low-$K$ failure as an effective-rank statement on the deflated transition matrix: one non-unit eigenvalue carries $93.6\%$ of the lag-1 ACF on SPY, but the cross-ticker dominant-mode share has median $75.6\%$ and minimum $32.6\%$ (NEM). Cross-ticker generalisation gives an OoS KS median of $69.1\%$ at the body headline $K^\star = 3$ ($75.1\%$ at the $K^\star = 6$ sensitivity reference, $73.4\%$ at $K = 18$; identical $11/30$ failure count and the same regime-introduction tickers driving the failures across all three operating points), lifted to $83.0\%$ by quarterly refit at $K = 18$; a non-equity GLD/SLV stress test collapses to $0\%$ OoS KS under static IS-fitted CHMM.
\end{abstract}

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} Continuous Hidden Markov Model, Baum-Welch, Student-$t$ Emissions, Stylized Facts, Volatility Clustering, Value-at-Risk, Synthetic-Data Generator.
