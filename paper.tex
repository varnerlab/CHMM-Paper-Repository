\begin{abstract}
We develop a family of regime-switching continuous hidden Markov models (CHMMs) as a reference synthetic-data generator for daily equity returns and benchmark them against non-parametric, discrete-state, and deep-generative alternatives. Three variants share a log-space Expectation--Maximization scaffold and differ only in the per-state emission law: Gaussian (CHMM-N), Student-t with per-state degrees of freedom (CHMM-t), and Laplace (CHMM-L). At a single moderate state resolution they reproduce the canonical stylized facts of daily equity returns within a standard CHMM framework. The contribution is the integrative evaluation built on this unified scaffold rather than a new estimator, with each operational claim grounded in a stated proposition: a closed-form spectral expression for the absolute-return ACF that recasts the Ryd\'{e}n et al.\ low-$K$ limitation as a $K$-rank statement on $\mathbf{T}$, ECM monotonicity for CHMM-t, identifiability and MLE consistency, marginal preservation under rank reordering, and structural inequalities that explain filter-VaR Kupiec failures. On ten years of daily SPDR S\&P~500 ETF (SPY) data, flat CHMM-t attains the smallest Maximum Mean Discrepancy in the panel and the discriminator AUC closest to $0.5$ among parametric and Markov-switching rows. On unconditional VaR, a semi-Markov CHMM and a two-regime MS-GARCH both reach the Kupiec floor at $1\%$, with MS-GARCH leading at $5\%$. On regime-conditional VaR, the Viterbi smoother diagnostic clears Kupiec and Christoffersen at $1\%$ and $5\%$ on the 2024 to 2026 holdout, but the filter-based one-step-ahead backtest fails Kupiec at both levels because the heavy-tailed mixture under the filter posterior is systematically over-conservative, an honest negative finding. We extend to multi-asset synthesis via a Single Index Model and Gaussian and Student-t copulas, with the flat Student-t copula the most competitive dependence layer.

\bigskip\noindent
\textbf{Keywords:} Continuous Hidden Markov Model, Baum-Welch, Student-t Emissions, Semi-Markov, Stylized Facts, Volatility Clustering, Copula Dependence, Cross-Asset Simulation, Value-at-Risk, Kupiec, Christoffersen, Maximum Mean Discrepancy, Synthetic-Data Generator.
\end{abstract}
