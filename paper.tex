\begin{abstract}
Synthetic equity-return generators support stress testing and scenario design, yet reproducing the three Cont stylized facts simultaneously, heavy-tailed marginals, negligible linear autocorrelation, and slow decay of the absolute-return autocorrelation function (ACF), remains difficult. We show that a continuous hidden Markov model (CHMM) trained by Baum--Welch reproduces all three on ten years of daily SPY returns with a 2.5-year held-out window, under a single scaffold that supports four interchangeable emission families (Gaussian, Student-$t$, Laplace, and Generalized Error Distribution).

The held-out-clean operating point $K^\star = 6$, selected by held-out per-observation log-likelihood and held-out KS on the strictly pre-2020 slice, is the headline default for synthetic-data consumers: the four CHMM emission variants attain $88.3$--$92.6\%$ IS and $76.3$--$79.0\%$ OoS Kolmogorov--Smirnov pass rates with absolute-return ACF MAE within $0.003$ of GARCH(1,1), and CHMM-N's simulated kurtosis $5.26$ matches observed OoS $5.29$ within $0.03$. A second held-out-clean operating point $K^\star = 3$ (held-out-likelihood and BIC) is the recommended default for risk-management consumers (CHMM-N: $89.7\%$ IS / $80.1\%$ OoS KS). An extended-state-resolution sensitivity reference at $K = 18$ adds $1.5$--$3.5$pp IS / OoS KS at the cost of moving from a strictly held-out criterion onto a multi-objective rule. The mechanism is a closed-form mixture-of-eigenvalues identity for the absolute-return ACF over the non-unit eigenvalues of the transition matrix (Hamilton 1994; Krolzig 1997; Timmermann 2000); we apply it to the SPY 2014--2024 instance of the Ryden et al.\ (1998) low-$K$ failure and show that the implied algebraic rank bound is non-binding at $K \ge 3$ on this instance, with a single non-unit eigenvalue carrying $\ge 93\%$ of the lag-1 ACF at every $K$ in the sweep.

A regime-conditional Value-at-Risk that propagates the one-step-ahead state forecast through the CHMM mixture passes the Christoffersen joint conditional-coverage test at every $(K, \alpha)$ on the headline OoS window and on 19 of 24 rolling-origin folds (median walk-forward OoS KS $67.7\%$, range $0$--$95\%$, with two stress folds at COVID 2020 and the 2022 rate-hike onset). Cross-ticker generalisation on a sector-balanced 30-ticker panel gives an IS KS median of $99.5\%$ and an OoS median of $73.4\%$, with single-name regime introductions driving the failure tail; a quarterly-refit version lifts the OoS median to $83.0\%$ and reduces failures from 11/30 to 7/30. Multi-asset synthesis on a US-equity universe composes through a rank-based Student-$t$ copula on CHMM marginals: profile MLE selects $\nu^\star = 6$ on six assets at IS off-diagonal correlation MAE $0.027$ (OoS off-diagonal MAE $0.209$ static, $0.185$ under quarterly rolling refit).

\bigskip\noindent
\textbf{Keywords:} Continuous Hidden Markov Model, Baum-Welch, Student-$t$ Emissions, Stylized Facts, Volatility Clustering, Spectral ACF, Value-at-Risk, Synthetic-Data Generator.
\end{abstract}
