\begin{abstract}
Synthetic equity-return generators support stress testing, regulatory backtesting, and scenario design, but existing approaches struggle to reproduce the three canonical stylized facts of daily returns simultaneously: heavy-tailed marginals, negligible linear autocorrelation, and slow decay of the absolute-return autocorrelation function (ACF). We show that a continuous hidden Markov model (CHMM) reproduces all three within a single EM scaffold, on ten years of daily SPDR S\&P~500 ETF (SPY) data with a $2.5$-year held-out window. At the held-out-likelihood and BIC operating point $K^\star = 3$, CHMM-N attains $89.7\%$ in-sample and $80.1\%$ out-of-sample Kolmogorov--Smirnov (KS) pass rates with an absolute-return ACF mean absolute error of $0.0467$ (Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison}); a tail-fidelity reference panel at $K = 18$ extends the four CHMM emission variants to $93.6$--$95.6\%$ IS and $80.8$--$85.7\%$ OoS KS, with CHMM-N's ACF-MAE of $0.0509$ within $0.003$ of GARCH(1,1) at $0.0485$ (the panel best is GARCH(1,1)-$t$ at $0.0316$). VaR and Expected-Shortfall envelopes bracket observed values at $1\%$ and $5\%$ on both windows under unconditional Kupiec coverage; conditional-coverage Christoffersen tests on the same pooled-archive breach series reject independence on OoS for every unconditional generator in the panel (CHMM, GARCH, bootstrap), driven structurally by volatility-clustering in $R_{\text{oos}}$ that any constant-across-$t$ VaR threshold cannot track. The mechanism behind the joint-fit reproduction is spectral: the absolute-return autocovariance admits the closed-form mixture-of-eigenvalues form $\rho_{|G|}(\tau) = \sum_{k \ge 2} c_k \lambda_k^{\tau} / \sigma_{|G|}^2$ over the non-unit eigenvalues of the transition matrix $\mathbf{T}$, a standard consequence of the Markov-chain spectral decomposition (Hamilton, 1994; Krolzig, 1997; Timmermann, 2000). We apply this identity to recast the low-$K$ limitation of \citet{ryden1998stylized} as a rank statement on the dominant-eigenmode-deflated transition matrix $\mathbf{T} - \mathbf{1}\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$ (rank at most $K - 1$ by construction), and show empirically that the rank constraint is binding only at $K = 2$--$3$ Gaussian-emission setups; once it is non-binding, the binding axis is the marginal mixture, which the four-family scaffold (Gaussian / Student-t / Laplace / GED, sharing forward-backward and quantile-based initialisation) addresses through a single M-step swap. CHMM-GED carries a per-state shape $p_k$ that nests Gaussian ($p = 2$) and Laplace ($p = 1$) as boundaries and adapts state by state. CHMM occupies the joint-fit corner of the metric panel where i.i.d.\ baselines win on marginal fidelity and GARCH wins on volatility clustering. Multi-asset synthesis composes through a rank-based copula on the CHMM marginals: profile MLE on a Student-t copula selects $\nu^\star = 6$ at IS off-diagonal correlation MAE $0.027$ on six SPY-member assets (Gaussian copula: $0.030$); on the OoS window the Gaussian and Student-t copulas are statistically indistinguishable (off-diagonal MAE $0.202$ and $0.209$), attributable to a regime shift in the JNJ-equity-factor relationship.

\bigskip\noindent
\textbf{Keywords:} Continuous Hidden Markov Model, Baum-Welch, Student-t Emissions, Stylized Facts, Volatility Clustering, Spectral ACF, Value-at-Risk, Synthetic-Data Generator.
\end{abstract}
