\begin{abstract}
We train a continuous hidden Markov model (CHMM) by Baum--Welch as a synthetic-data generator for daily US-equity returns. A unified ECM scaffold supports four emission families (Gaussian, Student-$t$, Laplace, Generalised Error Distribution) sharing identical forward-backward recursions and quantile-based initialisation; the M-step is the only architectural difference. The empirical study covers SPY 2014--2026, a sector-balanced 30-ticker panel, a CRSP cross-decade slice (1994--2006), and a six-asset US-equity copula extension. The empirical scope is the three symmetric Cont (2001) stylized facts (heavy-tailed marginals, negligible linear ACF, slow $|G_t|$ ACF); the leverage effect and gain-loss asymmetry are out of scope under symmetric per-state emissions.

The CHMM reproduces the three symmetric stylized facts at moderate state counts, on par with GARCH(1,1) on the absolute-return ACF axis. We apply the textbook bilinear-ACF identity over the non-unit eigenvalues of the transition matrix to the SPY instance of the Ryd\'{e}n et al.\ (1998) low-$K$ failure and recast it as an effective-rank statement on the deflated transition matrix; the rank constraint is non-binding at $K \ge 3$ at the cross-ticker median. A regime-conditional Value-at-Risk that propagates the one-step-ahead state forecast through the predictive mixture passes the Christoffersen joint conditional-coverage test on the headline window and on the bulk of a six-fold rolling-origin walk-forward, with rejections concentrated on out-of-distribution stress folds.

ML hidden semi-Markov models match or exceed the CHMM on raw distributional fidelity; the CHMM positioning is therefore on structural use cases (regime-conditional VaR, copula composition, parametric privacy) where multi-state regime-switching is required. The headline scope is daily US equities on stationary out-of-sample windows; a non-equity stress test (GLD, SLV) collapses under static fitting and periodic refit is the deployment recipe.
\end{abstract}

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} Continuous Hidden Markov Model, Baum-Welch, Student-$t$ Emissions, Stylized Facts, Volatility Clustering, Value-at-Risk, Synthetic-Data Generator.
