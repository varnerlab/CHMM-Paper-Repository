\begin{abstract}
Synthetic equity-return generators support stress testing and scenario design, yet reproducing the three Cont stylized facts simultaneously, heavy-tailed marginals, negligible linear autocorrelation, and slow decay of the absolute-return ACF, remains difficult. We show a continuous hidden Markov model (CHMM) reproduces all three within a single Baum-Welch scaffold, on ten years of daily SPY returns with a 2.5-year held-out window. At the held-out-likelihood operating point $K^\star = 3$, CHMM-N attains $89.7\%$ in-sample (IS) and $80.1\%$ out-of-sample (OoS) Kolmogorov--Smirnov pass rates at $|G|$ ACF mean absolute error (MAE) $0.0467$; at the held-out-clean synthetic-data-consumer reference $K^\star = 6$, the four emission variants (Gaussian, Student-$t$, Laplace, GED) attain $88.3$--$92.6\%$ IS and $76.3$--$79.0\%$ OoS KS with CHMM-N kurtosis $5.26$ on the IS window (observed $5.29$ OoS); an extended-state-resolution reference panel at $K = 18$ retains a $1.5$--$3.5$pp IS / OoS KS edge at the cost of relaxing the held-out criterion onto a multi-objective rule, and the penalised CHMM-t at $\lambda = 20$ on the extended panel provides the cleanest joint kurtosis match at $8.56$ IS / $7.07$ OoS against observed $7.68$ / $5.29$. Single-window pass rates sit at the upper end of a six-fold rolling-origin walk-forward distribution with median $67.7\%$ at $K = 18$ and $62.1\%$ at $K^\star = 3$ ($[Q_1, Q_3] = [8.2, 75.0]$ and $[7.2, 78.4]$), and CHMM-N's $|G|$ ACF-MAE sits within $0.003$ of GARCH(1,1). VaR and Expected-Shortfall envelopes bracket observed values at $1\%$ and $5\%$ on both windows; a regime-conditional VaR that propagates the one-step-ahead state forecast through the CHMM mixture passes Christoffersen-cc cleanly at every $(K,\alpha)$ on the headline OoS window and on $19$ of $24$ rows of a six-fold walk-forward extension (failures concentrate on the COVID and 2022-rate-hike-onset stress folds that are out-of-distribution by KS for every generator), while every constant-across-$t$ generator rejects breach independence on the unconditional variant. Cross-ticker generalisation on a sector-balanced 30-ticker panel ($10$ GICS sectors $\times 3$ large-cap representatives) gives a tight IS KS distribution (median $99.5\%$) and a sector-stratified OoS distribution with median $73.4\%$ and $11/30$ tickers below $60\%$, concentrated in single-name regime introductions (Health Care: LLY, UNH; OoS KS $\le 15\%$). The mechanism is a closed-form mixture-of-eigenvalues identity for the $|G|$ ACF over the non-unit eigenvalues of $\mathbf T$ (Hamilton 1994; Krolzig 1997; Timmermann 2000), recasting the Ryd\'{e}n et al.\ low-$K$ failure as a rank statement on the deflated transition matrix that binds only at $K \le 3$ Gaussian setups. Multi-asset synthesis composes through a rank-based copula on CHMM marginals: profile MLE selects $\nu^\star = 6$ on six SPY-member assets at IS off-diagonal correlation MAE $0.027$; on OoS Gaussian and Student-$t$ copulas are indistinguishable (MAE $0.202$ vs.\ $0.209$; $0.185$ under quarterly rolling refit).

\bigskip\noindent
\textbf{Keywords:} Continuous Hidden Markov Model, Baum-Welch, Student-$t$ Emissions, Stylized Facts, Volatility Clustering, Spectral ACF, Value-at-Risk, Synthetic-Data Generator.
\end{abstract}
