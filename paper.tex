\begin{abstract}
We evaluate a continuous hidden Markov model (CHMM) trained by Baum--Welch as a synthetic-data generator for daily US-equity returns on SPY 2014--2026 (body window), with cross-decade validation via CRSP 1994--2006, a sector-balanced 30-ticker panel, and a six-asset US-equity copula extension. The empirical scope is the three symmetric Cont (2001) stylized facts (heavy-tailed marginals, negligible linear ACF, slow $|G_t|$ ACF); the leverage effect and gain-loss asymmetry are out of scope under the body's symmetric per-state emissions. A unified ECM scaffold supports four emission families (Gaussian, Student-$t$, Laplace, Generalised Error Distribution) sharing identical forward-backward recursions and quantile-based initialisation; the M-step is the only architectural difference, and the four families are statistically indistinguishable on OoS sample-CRPS at $T_{\text{OoS}} = 572$.

At the state-resolution-robust held-out operating point $K^\star = 3$ (selected by held-out per-observation log-likelihood and held-out KS on the strictly pre-2020 slice under four-fold and six-fold rolling-origin CV), CHMM-N attains $89.7\%$ IS / $80.5\%$ OoS Kolmogorov--Smirnov pass rate at simulated excess kurtosis $3.83$ / $3.53$ against observed $7.68$ / $5.29$ and $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $0.0460$, on par with GARCH(1,1)'s $0.0485$. \textbf{Under a stationary-block-bootstrap null at mean block length $L = 20$ the OoS pass rate drops by ${\sim}25$pp}; cross-generator ordering is preserved. The single OoS window sits at the upper end of a six-fold rolling-origin walk-forward whose CHMM-N median OoS KS is $62.1\%$ at $K^\star = 3$, with two stress folds (W2 COVID, W4 2022 rate-hike onset) below $10\%$. A shared-$\nu$ Student-$t$ CHMM at $K = 18$ provides the cleanest single-row IS / OoS heavy-tail match in the panel without any penalty hyperparameter ($6.25$ / $5.00$ against observed $7.68$ / $5.29$); the per-state $\nu_k$ + $\lambda$-shrinkage construction is reported alongside it for comparability with the Peel--Liu (2000) tradition.

A regime-conditional Value-at-Risk that propagates the one-step-ahead state forecast through the CHMM mixture passes the Christoffersen joint conditional-coverage test at $\alpha = 0.05$ on the headline OoS window and on $19/24$ walk-forward folds. \textbf{At $\alpha = 0.01$ the higher-power Engle--Manganelli (2004) Dynamic Quantile test rejects conditional coverage at $K = 18$ on the same stationary OoS slice ($p = 0.017$)}, so the strict-tail conditional-VaR property is not supported at $T_{\text{OoS}} = 572$; tighter tail levels require either larger $T_{\text{OoS}}$ or a model class with explicit regime-introduction handling. The walk-forward W2 / W4 rejections are out-of-distribution by KS for every generator in the panel and are not closed by faster refit cadences.

ML HSMM dominates CHMM on raw OoS distributional fidelity at every operating point tried (Pareto-sojourn HSMM-N at $K^\star = 3$: $91.0\%$ OoS KS; Gamma-sojourn HSMM-N at $K = 18$: panel-best $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $0.0462$). The CHMM positioning is therefore on the structural use cases (regime-conditional VaR, copula composition, parametric privacy) rather than on raw stylized-fact reproduction. Cross-ticker generalisation gives an OoS KS median of $69.1\%$ at $K^\star = 3$, lifted to $83.0\%$ by quarterly refit at $K = 18$. \textbf{The headline scope is daily US equities on stationary OoS windows}: a non-equity GLD/SLV stress test collapses to $0\%$ OoS KS under static IS-fitted CHMM, the cross-decade 2004--2006 OoS slice collapses to $3$--$5\%$, and $11/30$ tickers in the cross-section fail OoS KS at the standard threshold under static IS fitting; periodic refit at deployment is the operational mitigation.
\end{abstract}

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} Continuous Hidden Markov Model, Baum-Welch, Student-$t$ Emissions, Stylized Facts, Volatility Clustering, Value-at-Risk, Synthetic-Data Generator.
