\begin{abstract}
We present a continuous hidden Markov model (CHMM) framework for generating synthetic equity excess growth rate paths that preserve the stylized facts of financial returns.
Unlike the discrete-state approach of \citet{alswaidan2026hybrid}, which discretizes observations into Laplace quantile bins and estimates transitions by frequency counting, we learn Gaussian emission parameters and transition probabilities directly from continuous observations via the Baum-Welch (Expectation-Maximization) algorithm, eliminating quantization error.
The Poisson jump-duration mechanism is preserved to enforce realistic tail-state persistence and partially reproduce volatility clustering.
Applied to ten years of daily SPDR S\&P~500 ETF (SPY) data, the framework achieves KS pass rates above 87\% across all state resolutions ($K \in \{3, 6, 9, 11, 13\}$) both in-sample and out-of-sample, with distributional fidelity improving monotonically with $K$.
Baseline comparisons confirm that Gaussian i.i.d.\ generators fail entirely (0\% KS), while the CHMM variants consistently outperform Laplace i.i.d.\ generators and match or exceed bootstrap resampling on distributional metrics.
Cross-asset generalization to NVDA, JNJ, and JPM confirms that the framework adapts to diverse volatility profiles.
The continuous emission structure offers a natural foundation for future extensions incorporating stochastic volatility dynamics within the HMM regime-switching framework.

\bigskip\noindent
\textbf{Keywords:} Continuous Hidden Markov Model, Baum-Welch Algorithm, Jump-Diffusion, Volatility Clustering, Synthetic Financial Data, Regime-Switching
\end{abstract}
