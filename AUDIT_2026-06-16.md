## Part 2 — Readability

### Diagnosis: density, not disorder

`style-check` returns **0 violations** across all eight files — no em dashes, no stray subsection
headings, no run-in paragraph headers, no soft lists, no section-ref-as-subject. Structurally the
paper is well organized and follows your conventions. What makes it hard to follow is that many
sentences carry too much at once: a finding + its mechanism + its provenance + a hedge + inline
notation, all in one 60–90-word span. Your own saved style rules already prescribe the fix —
*clean lead sentences* (state the action + point to the table; numbers go in the follow-up) and
*plain direct English* — the densest sentences simply violate those rules.

### Worst offenders + suggested rewrites

**(R1) Results — the CHMM-t penalised sentence** (`results.tex:33`). One sentence, a colon and two
semicolons, three different results.
> *Now:* "The penalised CHMM-t at λ=20 was reported as a sensitivity reference: the IS aggregate
> 14.91 was a 1/ν_k-shrinkage outcome with λ tuned at K=18 and re-used at K\*=3, where the state
> pinned at the lower ν bracket carried a larger share of the three-state mixture; CHMM-GED's
> per-state shape landed at 5.48 and 5.12, and CHMM-L at 5.28 and 4.88 (losing roughly 14pp on OoS
> KS at K=3 against CHMM-GED)."
> *Suggested:* "We report the penalised CHMM-t at λ=20 as a sensitivity reference. Its IS kurtosis of
> 14.91 is a shrinkage artifact: λ was tuned at K=18 and re-used at K\*=3, where the state pinned at
> the lower ν bound dominates the three-state mixture. CHMM-GED and CHMM-L sit in between, at IS/OoS
> kurtosis 5.48/5.12 and 5.28/4.88; CHMM-L loses about 14pp of OoS KS to CHMM-GED."

**(R2) Results — the cross-ticker failure sentence** (`results.tex:43`). Nested parentheticals that
forward-reference a K=18 table mid-clause.
> *Now:* "The deepest failures were driven by tickers that introduced a new regime in the OoS window
> (LLY and UNH in Health Care, NEM in Materials; full per-ticker rollup at the K=18 kurtosis-match
> check in Table~\ref{...}), and the remaining eight sub-60% tickers and the per-sector breakdown at
> the same K=18 check (with the same 11/30 failure count and overlapping ticker list) are reported in
> the appendix."
> *Suggested:* "The deepest failures were tickers that introduced a new regime out of sample — LLY and
> UNH (Health Care) and NEM (Materials). The per-ticker rollup, the remaining eight sub-60% tickers,
> and the per-sector breakdown are in the appendix; the failure count and ticker list are stable at
> the K=18 check."

**(R3) Methods — the model-definition paragraph** (`methods.tex:11`). One paragraph introduces ~10
symbols (S, s_t, T, T_ij, π, π_k, F, f_k, θ_k, F_k) back to back. This is the first wall a reader
hits and is probably the single biggest "overly technical" trigger.
> *Suggested:* split into short units — one for the state space and chain, one for the transition
> matrix, one for the initial distribution, one for the emissions — and **defer the per-state CDF
> F_k** to where the VaR construction first uses it (it isn't needed at the model definition). Same
> content, four readable beats instead of one block.

### Abstract

Structurally sound and the right length (~240 words), but it front-loads bare numbers and undefined
machinery, which is exactly the "too technical" feel. Two worst sentences:

- **Eigenvalue/rank sentence** (`paper.tex:146`): "A mixture-of-eigenvalues identity expresses the ACF
  as finitely many geometric modes; ... the associated rank bound was not active at the cross-ticker
  median once K≥3 ..." — four undefined ideas cold (mixture-of-eigenvalues identity, geometric modes,
  rank bound, cross-ticker median), and "ACF" before it's defined.
  > *Suggested:* "An eigenvalue decomposition writes the autocorrelation of absolute returns as a sum
  > of finitely many decaying components. For the typical ticker, three or more states sufficed to
  > lift the structural ceiling on that decay, and the choice of emission distribution mattered more
  > than the state count for the remaining gap."
- **Kurtosis sentence** (`paper.tex:146`): "shared-ν Student-t produced kurtosis 4.68 against observed
  7.68 without a tuning hyperparameter." Two bare numbers a reader can't yet judge.
  > *Suggested:* move 4.68/7.68 to the body; in the abstract say "recovered much of the heavy-tailed
  > kurtosis of daily returns with no tunable shape parameter."

General abstract levers: expand or replace cold acronyms on first use (ACF, MAE, CRSP), and move the
result numbers (4.68, 7.68, 19/24, 0.027, 0.077) into the body — your abstract convention keeps bare
numbers out anyway.

### Already good

Introduction (motivation → gap → motivated-approach → "In this study" closer), Related Work, and
Conclusion read cleanly and at an appropriate altitude. The density problem is concentrated in
Methods (definitions) and Results (multi-finding sentences).
