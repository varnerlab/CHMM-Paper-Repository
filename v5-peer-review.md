## Reviewer 1 (Moderate) — Senior Researcher, Regime-Switching Time-Series Models

### Summary
The manuscript fits a continuous Hidden Markov Model with $K$ regimes to daily SPY excess log returns under quantile-based initialisation, with four interchangeable emission families (Gaussian, Student-$t$, Laplace, GED) sharing a unified ECM scaffold; benchmarks against i.i.d., GARCH-family, MS-GARCH, semi-Markov (both Viterbi-AR(1) plug-in and an ML HSMM at $K^\star = 3$), and QuantGAN baselines; develops a regime-conditional VaR back-test that propagates the one-step-ahead state forecast through the CHMM mixture; and composes a multi-asset extension via a rank-based Student-$t$ copula on the per-asset CHMM marginals. The current revision delivers most V4 Tier-1 items cleanly and the empirical study is now genuinely thorough for the target venue. The remaining items are smaller framing issues plus one draft artefact in body Table~4.

### Strengths
1. **The four-emission scaffold is a substantive methodological contribution and the experimental panel supports it cleanly.** Section~3.2 ("Continuous Hidden Markov Model") and the M-step appendix (Section~A.1) document four emission families that share the forward-backward recursions and quantile-based initialisation; the M-step is the only architectural difference. Table~1 reports IS / OoS KS pass rates within $2$ pp IS and $5$ pp OoS across the four families, which is the right experimental signature of "the emission family swap moves tail weight cleanly without disturbing the central or temporal fit" (Section~4.3 closing prose). The CHMM-GED nesting of CHMM-N ($p_k = 2$) and CHMM-L ($p_k = 1$) as boundary cases under bracketed per-state shape is the right structural framing.

2. **The ML HSMM at $K^\star = 3$ in Table~1 plus the $K = 18$ collapse sentence in Section~4.3 closes the central V4 blocker honestly.** Yu's (2010) explicit-duration forward-backward over $(s_t, d_t)$ pairs with truncated discrete Pareto sojourns is the correct construction; the trade-off between KS gain ($98.4 / 91.0\%$) and ACF-MAE regression ($0.0629$, at the i.i.d.-baseline level versus CHMM-N's $0.0467$ at the same $K$) is now surfaced explicitly. The body framing "two complementary scaffolds at the same Markov backbone, with different trade-offs across the three Cont stylized facts" reads as the right calibration. The K=18 collapse note ("the joint $K^2 + KD_{\max}$ free-parameter count exceeds the effective IS sample size") converts what would have been a silent deferral into substantive evidence about HSMM scaling.

3. **The regime-conditional Christoffersen-cc result is the strongest substantive risk-management contribution.** Section~4.6 paragraph (iii) develops a one-step-ahead-filter VaR (equation~9) under IS-fixed parameters and shows it passes Kupiec, Christoffersen-ind, and Christoffersen-cc cleanly at every $(K, \alpha)$ on OoS, while every constant-across-$t$ generator (CHMM unconditional, GARCH, bootstrap) rejects independence ($\text{LR}_{\text{ind}} = 5.26$ at $K = 18, \alpha = 0.05$ unconditional vs $0.52$ conditional). The four-family extension in Appendix Table~`tab:cond_var_all_families` ($16/16$ panels pass with $p_{\text{cc}} \ge 0.089$ throughout) supports the reading that the regime-switching value proposition is intrinsic to the latent-state forecast and not specific to the Gaussian emission family.

4. **The CHMM-GED $\hat p_k$ partition figure** (Figure~`fig:p_hist`, Section~4.3) is the most distinctive empirical finding of the four-emission scaffold: thirteen states at the upper bracket $p_k = 3.0$, four states clustered in the Laplace regime $p_k \in [0.86, 1.24]$, one state at $p_k = 1.6$, with the Laplace-shape states concentrated at high-volatility ranks (panel b). The data-driven Gaussian-bulk / Laplace-tail partition replicates across 10 seeds and across the cross-asset universe; this is the right kind of empirical finding for a methodological paper and supports the "data chooses the kurtosis decomposition" reading.

5. **The reproducibility infrastructure is exceptional for the literature.** Single deterministic seed root ($20260420$), additive sub-seed rule documented in Appendix A.1, companion `CHMM-Model.jl` package exposed through `make_chmm_*`, `fit!`, `simulate`, and `simulate_copula` with `Manifest.toml` pinning the dependency graph, single-script rebuild via `run_full_rebuild.jl`. The walk-forward, profile-LL CI, four-family conditional-VaR, and per-ticker $\hat\lambda^\star$ runs are each in their own runner script. This is the right way to organise paper-scale empirical work and is among the better reproducibility practices in the regime-switching applied literature.

### Weaknesses

### Questions for Authors
1. **The ML HSMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ has $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $0.0629$, identical to the i.i.d.\ bootstrap and Laplace i.i.d.\ baselines** (both $0.0628$) within rounding. Is the explicit-duration sojourn at $K = 3$ collapsing the off-diagonal transition mass enough that $\hat{\mathbf T}_{\text{HSMM}}$ effectively behaves like the rank-one stationary projector for the absolute-return-ACF identity? A numerical full-rank check on $\hat{\mathbf T}_{\text{HSMM}}$ at $K = 3$ (smallest singular value, $|\lambda_2|$, deflated rank), parallel to the four-CHMM-family check in Table~`tab:t_singular_values`, would localise the cause. Also a state-occupancy diagnostic: what fraction of IS time does the ML HSMM at $K = 3$ spend in each state, against CHMM-N's analogous occupancy? If the HSMM concentrates on a single low-volatility state with long sojourns, the kurtosis regression to $3.46$ has a clean diagnosis.

2. **Profile-LL Wilks 95% CI for $\hat\nu^\star$ is $[6, 7]$** (Section~4.5) on a unit-spaced grid in $\nu \in [4, 12]$. The rolling-window quarterly refit (Appendix C.10, summarised in Table~`tab:cross_asset_supp_summary`) reports $\nu^\star$ wandering across $\{5, 6, 10, 15\}$ across nine quarters. Is the apparent wandering a small-sample artefact of the rolling profile-LL on $T \approx 63$-day windows, or a genuine non-stationarity in the tail-coupling? A formal test (e.g., a Quintos-Fan-Phillips-style structural-break statistic on the rolling $\hat\nu$ series, or a simple block-bootstrap CI on each rolling estimate) would let the reader interpret the rolling sequence as either stationary noise or a genuine non-stationarity.

3. **The walk-forward stress folds W2 (COVID) and W4 (2022 rate-hike onset) drop to KS $\le 8\%$ at both $K = 3$ and $K = 18$**, while the headline OoS window 2024-01 to 2026-04 (which covers the AI rally and rate-cycle reversal) attains $80$--$86\%$ KS. Why is the rate-cycle *onset* (W4 test slice 2022-01 to 2023-01) catastrophically harder than the rate-cycle *reversal* (in the headline OoS) for a model fit on the same kind of pre-period IS data? Is the volatility regime around the 2022 onset materially different from the 2024-2026 reversal in some way the IS-fitted CHMM cannot bridge?

4. **The CRPS column in Table~1 reports `--` for several rows** (GARCH(1,1)-$t$, MS-GARCH, CHMM-N at $K^\star = 3$, ML HSMM-N at $K^\star = 3$, CHMM-t penalised, CHMM-GED), with the caption note "rows not in the cached CRPS archive (no material expected difference from the reported CHMM rows, which cluster within $0.002$ DM)." The CRPS section is the proper-scoring-rule diagnostic that the body uses to rule out "the headline KS finding is a non-proper-metric artefact"; the missing rows leave the diagnosis on five rows out of twelve. Is there an obstacle to running the CRPS computation on the missing rows, or is it a queue/runtime issue? A complete CRPS column would tighten the proper-scoring-rule argument.

5. **The per-ticker $\hat\lambda^\star$ table** (Appendix Table~`tab:per_ticker_lambda`) reports six per-ticker optima clustered around $\lambda \in \{0, 10, 20\}$ on six tickers. Is there a principled rule (kurtosis-residual-at-$\lambda = 0$ threshold, or a per-state $\nu_k$ lower-bracket-pinning indicator at $\lambda = 0$) that would let a deployment decide $\hat\lambda^\star$ without a sweep, or is the per-ticker sweep the right operational protocol? The Discussion paragraph "per-ticker tuning is recommended whenever the ticker's residual kurtosis at $\lambda = 0$ is within $\sim 1$ unit of observed" hints at such a rule but does not commit.

## Reviewer 2 (Hard) — Constraint-Based Time-Series Modelling, Risk Backtesting, MCMC

### Summary
The mathematics is correct, the simulation infrastructure is reproducible, and the comparisons are now thorough. The principal V4 blockers (introduction reframing, ML HSMM K=18 collapse sentence, walk-forward median in the body, ML HSMM K=3 trade-off framing) are delivered. ### Strengths
1. **The spectral-mechanism attribution is honest and the contribution is correctly scoped.** Section~2.3 ("Spectral Mechanism for the Absolute-Return ACF") attributes the closed-form bilinear identity $\mathbf m^\top \mathrm{diag}(\bar{\boldsymbol\pi})\,\mathbf T^\tau\,\mathbf m$ and its eigendecomposition to Hamilton (1994), Krolzig (1997), and Timmermann (2000), with the Levin-Peres reference for the underlying Markov-chain spectral decomposition. The explicit framing "Our contribution is not the identity itself but its explicit application to recast the empirical Rydén et al. low-$K$ failure as a rank statement on the deflated transition matrix $\mathbf T - \mathbf 1\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$, and the empirical demonstration that the rank constraint binds only at $K \le 3$ on equity-return data" is exactly the right scoping. The effective-rank diagnostic in Appendix C.4 (a single non-unit eigenvalue carries $93.6\%$ of the lag-1 ACF at $K = 18$, $96.8\%$ at $K = 3$) supports the empirical claim.
2. **The introduction reframing** (V4 Tier-1 item 3, paragraph 3 of the introduction) is now correctly aligned with Appendix~C.4 and Section~5: the binding axis at $K \ge 3$ is the marginal mixture, the spectral identity is non-binding at this regime, and the $K = 2$ failure is on the distributional axis. This was the largest framing correction V4 asked for and it has been delivered cleanly.
3. **The four-family conditional-VaR extension** (Appendix Table~`tab:cond_var_all_families`, $16/16$ panels pass) supports the body's "intrinsic to the latent-state forecast and not specific to the Gaussian emission family" reading. This is the strongest substantive risk-management contribution and it now extends across CHMM-N, CHMM-t at $\lambda = 20$, CHMM-L, CHMM-GED at $K \in \{3, 18\}$ and $\alpha \in \{0.01, 0.05\}$.
4. **Penalised CHMM-t handling.** Appendix C.6 paragraph "Penalised-ECM rate sweep" reports the full $\lambda \in \{0, 5, 20, 50, 100, 200\}$ rate sweep with simulated kurtosis dropping monotonically from $14.30$ to $3.67$, and identifies the overshoot as a single-state lower-bracket-pinning artefact rather than a generic ECM failure mode. This is the right diagnostic stance and the body Discussion ("the overshoot is a single-state artefact rather than a lower-bracket pinning") is consistent with the appendix evidence.
5. **The $K = 18$ Table~`tab:t_singular_values` numerical full-rank check** across the four CHMM emission families confirms the rank statement underlying Section~2.3: the deflated matrix $\hat{\mathbf T} - \mathbf 1\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$ has numerical rank exactly $K - 1 = 17$ across all four families, and the smallest singular value of $\hat{\mathbf T}$ is in $[0.0007, 0.0170]$ (CHMM-t the least well-conditioned, consistent with the per-state $\nu_k$ lower-bracket-pinning making one or two heavy-tail states near-degenerate). This is the right kind of empirical check.
