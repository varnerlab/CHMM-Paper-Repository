Synthetic generators of equity-return time series support stress testing, regulatory backtesting, scenario design, and the training of downstream machine-learning models on data that cannot be released for privacy or licensing reasons \cite{jordon2022synthetic, assefa2020generating, alswaidan2026hybrid}. The bar for a useful generator is that it reproduce, simultaneously, the canonical stylized facts of daily returns: heavy-tailed marginals, negligible linear autocorrelation, and slow decay of the autocorrelation function (ACF) of absolute returns \cite{mandelbrot1963variation, cont2001empirical}. Generalized autoregressive conditional heteroskedasticity (GARCH) models \cite{engle1982autoregressive, bollerslev1986generalized} capture volatility clustering but not the multi-regime structure of the marginal; i.i.d.\ generators match the marginal but destroy temporal persistence; deep generators \cite{yoon2019timegan, wiese2020quantgan, rasul2021autoregressive} reproduce some stylized facts at the cost of interpretability and reproducibility.

An influential negative result has shaped two decades of work on the regime-switching route. \citet{ryden1998stylized} fitted Gaussian-emission hidden Markov models (HMMs) at $K = 2$--$3$ states and concluded that the geometric sojourn-time distribution of a standard Markov chain produces exponential ACF decay too fast to match the observed slow decay; \citet{bulla2006stylized} restored ACF fidelity by replacing the Markov chain with a hidden semi-Markov chain at the cost of substantially increased complexity.

We identify the binding axis. The empirical Ryd\'{e}n et al.\ low-$K$ failure compounds two constraints: a temporal one (the absolute-return ACF must admit slow-decay modes) and a distributional one (the marginal mixture must carry enough components to match observed kurtosis). The temporal axis is governed by the closed-form mixture-of-eigenvalues identity $\rho_{|G|}(\tau) = \sum_{k \ge 2} c_k \lambda_k^{\tau} / \sigma_{|G|}^2$ over the non-unit eigenvalues of $\mathbf T$ (Section~\ref{sec:theory}; the bilinear cross-moment identity and its eigendecomposition are textbook material in the regime-switching literature, \citealp{hamilton1994time, krolzig1997markov, timmermann2000moments}), which gives an algebraic upper bound of $K - 1$ on the number of decay modes through the rank of $\mathbf T - \mathbf 1\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$. An empirical effective-rank diagnostic on the fitted $\hat{\mathbf T}$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:spectral_rank}) shows this bound is non-binding at $K \ge 3$ on equity-return data: a single non-unit eigenvalue carries $93.6\%$ of the lag-1 absolute-return ACF at $K = 18$ and $96.8\%$ at $K = 3$. The binding axis at $K \ge 3$ is therefore the marginal mixture, whose representational capacity must match the empirical kurtosis target. At $K = 2$ both axes bind simultaneously (mono-exponential ACF that can be tuned, two-component marginal that cannot), and the empirical failure is on the distributional axis; at $K \ge 3$ the temporal axis decouples and the marginal-mixture component count drives the joint fit. The semi-Markov route adds an explicit sojourn distribution to relax the temporal axis, but on equity-return data the temporal axis is already non-binding under standard Baum-Welch at $K \ge 3$, so the operationally informative comparison between the two scaffolds runs on the distributional axis, where the four CHMM emission families and the explicit-duration HSMM at $K^\star = 3$ trade KS, kurtosis, and ACF fidelity differently (Section~\ref{sec:results}).

We instantiate this on ten years of daily SPY ETF data with a $2.5$-year held-out window, using a continuous HMM trained by Baum-Welch \cite{baum1970maximization, rabiner1986introduction} under quantile-based initialization in four emission variants sharing identical forward-backward recursions: CHMM-N (Gaussian), CHMM-t (per-state Student-$t$ with $\nu_k$ updated by ECM golden-section in the tradition of \citealp{peel2000robust, liu1995ml}), CHMM-L (Laplace, weighted median and weighted MAD in closed form), and CHMM-GED (Generalized Error Distribution with per-state shape $p_k$ via three-stage ECM, nesting CHMM-N at $p_k = 2$ and CHMM-L at $p_k = 1$). Against a benchmark panel (i.i.d.\ bootstrap of \citealp{politis1994stationary}; Gaussian and Laplace i.i.d.; GARCH(1,1) of \citealp{bollerslev1986generalized} and the heavy-tailed extension of \citealp{bollerslev1987conditionally}; MS-GARCH of \citealp{haas2004new}), the four CHMM variants at $K = 18$ attain $93.6$--$95.6\%$ IS and $80.8$--$85.7\%$ OoS KS, with CHMM-N's $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE of $0.0509$ matching GARCH(1,1)'s $0.0485$ to within $0.003$ (Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison}). The variants land at four kurtosis points (CHMM-N $5.0$, CHMM-L $6.6$, CHMM-GED $5.2$ with a bimodal Gaussian-bulk / Laplace-tail $\hat p_k$ partition, penalised CHMM-t at $\lambda = 20$ at $8.56$ IS / $7.07$ OoS as the cleanest single-row joint match to observed $7.68$ / $5.29$; the unpenalised CHMM-t IS kurtosis of $14.4$ is the per-state $\nu_k$ ECM lower-bracket pinning artefact discussed in Section~\ref{sec:discussion}). VaR and Expected-Shortfall envelopes bracket observed values at $1\%$ and $5\%$ on both windows. CHMM-N at the held-out-likelihood and BIC operating point $K^\star = 3$ delivers $89.7\%$ IS / $80.1\%$ OoS KS at $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $0.0467$ and is the recommended default for risk-management consumers; the held-out-clean $K^\star = 6$ block (selected by held-out per-observation log-likelihood and held-out KS on the strictly pre-2020 slice) extends the four emission variants to $88.3$--$92.6\%$ IS / $76.3$--$79.0\%$ OoS KS with simulated kurtosis $5.22$--$5.86$ (observed $7.68$ IS, $5.29$ OoS) and is the recommended default for synthetic-data consumers; an extended-state-resolution reference panel at $K = 18$ adds $1.5$--$3.5$pp IS / OoS KS at the cost of moving from a strictly held-out criterion onto a multi-objective rule. Multi-asset synthesis composes through a rank-based Student-$t$ copula on the CHMM marginals (profile MLE selects $\nu^\star = 6$; six-asset IS off-diagonal correlation MAE $0.027$). On the sector-balanced 30-ticker panel the IS distribution is tight (median KS $99.5\%$) and the OoS distribution is sector-stratified (Utilities, Information Technology, and Consumer Staples cluster above $90\%$ median; Health Care collapses to $14.5\%$ on weight-loss-drug regime introductions in LLY and 2024 healthcare-policy compression in UNH; $11/30$ tickers below $60\%$ OoS KS at the universe scale), reflecting the well-known stationarity-scope limit on financial time series \cite{pastorstambaugh2001equity, andreoughysels2002breaks, angtimmermann2012regime} and motivating periodic refit at deployment \citep{pesarantimmermann2007window}.

Our contributions are: (i) we apply the closed-form bilinear identity $\rho_{|G|}(\tau) = \sum_{k \ge 2} c_k \lambda_k^\tau / \sigma_{|G|}^2$ (folklore in the regime-switching literature, attributed above) to the empirical \citet{ryden1998stylized} low-$K$ failure and show empirically that the algebraic rank bound on $\mathbf T - \mathbf 1\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$ is non-binding at $K \ge 3$ on equity-return data, with a single non-unit eigenvalue carrying $\ge 93\%$ of the lag-1 absolute-return ACF at every $K$ in the sweep (Appendix~\ref{sec:spectral_rank}); the substantive content is the empirical effective-rank statement, not a re-derivation of the identity; (ii) four emission families (Gaussian, Student-t, Laplace, Generalized Error Distribution) under a unified ECM scaffold, where the M-step is the only architectural difference and CHMM-GED's per-state shape $p_k$ adapts each state on the Gaussian-to-Laplace axis; (iii) a single-window IS / OoS empirical study on SPY plus a sector-balanced 30-ticker generalisation panel (10 GICS sectors $\times$ 3 large-cap representatives) and a six-asset Student-t copula extension.
