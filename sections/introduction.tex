Synthetic generators of equity-return time series support stress testing, regulatory backtesting, scenario design, and the training of downstream machine-learning models on data that cannot be released for privacy or licensing reasons \cite{jordon2022synthetic, assefa2020generating, alswaidan2026hybrid}. The bar for a useful generator is that it reproduce, simultaneously, the canonical stylized facts of daily returns: heavy-tailed marginals, negligible linear autocorrelation, and slow decay of the autocorrelation function (ACF) of absolute returns \cite{mandelbrot1963variation, cont2001empirical}. Generalized autoregressive conditional heteroskedasticity (GARCH) models \cite{engle1982autoregressive, bollerslev1986generalized} capture volatility clustering but not the multi-regime structure of the marginal; i.i.d.\ generators match the marginal but destroy temporal persistence; deep generators \cite{yoon2019timegan, wiese2020quantgan, rasul2021autoregressive} reproduce some stylized facts at the cost of interpretability and reproducibility.

An influential negative result has shaped two decades of work on the regime-switching route. \citet{ryden1998stylized} fitted Gaussian-emission hidden Markov models (HMMs) at $K = 2$--$3$ states and concluded that the geometric sojourn-time distribution of a standard Markov chain produces exponential ACF decay too fast to match the observed slow decay; \citet{bulla2006stylized} restored ACF fidelity by replacing the Markov chain with a hidden semi-Markov chain at the cost of substantially increased complexity.

We identify the binding axis. The empirical Ryd\'{e}n et al.\ low-$K$ failure compounds two constraints: a temporal one (the absolute-return ACF must admit slow-decay modes) and a distributional one (the marginal mixture must carry enough components to match observed kurtosis). The closed-form mixture-of-eigenvalues identity $\rho_{|G|}(\tau) = \sum_{k \ge 2} c_k \lambda_k^{\tau} / \sigma_{|G|}^2$ over the non-unit eigenvalues of $\mathbf T$ \citep{hamilton1994time, krolzig1997markov, timmermann2000moments} (restated in Section~\ref{sec:theory}) gives an algebraic upper bound of $K - 1$ on the number of decay modes through the rank of $\mathbf T - \mathbf 1\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$. An empirical effective-rank diagnostic on the fitted $\hat{\mathbf T}$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:spectral_rank}) shows this bound is non-binding at $K \ge 3$ at the cross-ticker median on equity-return data: across a 30-ticker panel the dominant-mode share has median $75.6\%$ with interquartile range $[66\%, 86\%]$ and minimum $32.6\%$ (NEM); the SPY value at $K = 18$ is $93.6\%$, in the right tail of this distribution. The binding axis at $K \ge 3$ is therefore the marginal mixture, whose representational capacity must match the empirical kurtosis target.

We instantiate this on ten years of daily SPY ETF data with a $2.5$-year held-out window, using a continuous HMM trained by Baum-Welch \cite{baum1970maximization, rabiner1986introduction} under quantile-based initialization in four emission variants (Gaussian, per-state Student-$t$ in the tradition of \citealp{peel2000robust, liu1995ml}, Laplace, and GED) that share identical forward-backward recursions. The empirical scope is daily US equities under stationary OoS: a non-equity stress test on GLD and SLV ETFs (Appendix~\ref{sec:non_us_asset_supp}) collapses to $0\%$ OoS KS under static IS-fitted CHMM, a documented scope limit rather than a deferred follow-up.

The body headline is $K^\star = 3$, selected by held-out per-observation log-likelihood and held-out KS on the strictly pre-2020 slice under both single-fold and four-fold / six-fold rolling-origin CV (Appendix~\ref{sec:k_selection_kfold_pre2020}). At this operating point CHMM-N attains $89.7\%$ IS / $80.5\%$ OoS KS at $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $0.0460$ (Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison}), and the penalised CHMM-t at $\lambda = 20$ gives the cleanest IS / OoS heavy-tail match at $14.91 / 8.50$ against observed $7.68 / 5.29$. The single-window OoS sits at the upper end of a six-fold rolling-origin walk-forward whose median CHMM-N OoS KS is $62.1\%$ at $K^\star = 3$ and $67.7\%$ at $K = 18$ (Table~\ref{tab:walkforward}); the walk-forward distribution, not the single-window pair, is the operationally informative summary for production deployment. VaR and Expected-Shortfall envelopes bracket observed values at $1\%$ and $5\%$ on both windows.

Multi-asset synthesis on a US-equity universe composes through a rank-based Student-$t$ copula on the CHMM marginals; profile MLE selects $\nu^\star = 6$ at IS off-diagonal correlation MAE $0.027$, but on OoS the Gaussian and Student-$t$ copulas are statistically indistinguishable, so the dependence-family choice is an IS-calibration distinction. On the sector-balanced 30-ticker panel the IS distribution is tight (median KS $99.5\%$) and $11/30$ tickers fall below $60\%$ OoS KS, concentrated on single-name regime introductions; this reflects the well-known stationarity-scope limit on financial time series \cite{pastorstambaugh2001equity, andreoughysels2002breaks, angtimmermann2012regime} and motivates periodic refit at deployment \citep{pesarantimmermann2007window}.

Our contributions are empirical and architectural. \emph{(i)} We provide empirical evidence that the algebraic $K - 1$ rank bound on $\mathbf T - \mathbf 1\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$ from the bilinear identity is non-binding at $K \ge 3$ on equity-return data: a single non-unit eigenvalue carries $93.6\%$ of the lag-1 absolute-return ACF at $K = 18$ on SPY (Appendix~\ref{sec:spectral_rank}), and the cross-ticker median dominant-mode share is $0.76$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:spectral_rank_xticker}); this recasts the \citet{ryden1998stylized} failure as a marginal-mixture limit, not a temporal one. \emph{(ii)} A unified ECM scaffold for four emission families (Gaussian, Student-$t$, Laplace, GED) under shared forward-backward recursions where the M-step is the only architectural difference; CHMM-GED's per-state shape $p_k$ nests Gaussian and Laplace as boundary cases. \emph{(iii)} A regime-conditional Value-at-Risk that passes Christoffersen-cc cleanly across emission families on OoS, with Monte Carlo power calibration and an Engle--Manganelli DQ backstop; the use case that no exchangeable bootstrap or unconditional GARCH benchmark can serve. \emph{(iv)} An empirical scope along three orthogonal generalisation axes: a six-fold rolling-origin walk-forward on SPY 2014--2026, a sector-balanced 30-ticker panel, a CRSP cross-decade transfer, and a six-asset US-equity Student-$t$ copula extension.
