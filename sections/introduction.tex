A synthetic generator of financial time series is useful downstream only if it reproduces the canonical stylized facts of equity returns simultaneously: heavy-tailed marginals, negligible linear autocorrelation, and slow decay of the autocorrelation function (ACF) of absolute returns \cite{mandelbrot1963variation, cont2001empirical}.
GARCH models \cite{engle1982autoregressive, bollerslev1986generalized} capture volatility clustering but not the multi-regime structure of the marginal; i.i.d.\ generators match the marginal but destroy temporal persistence.
Hidden Markov models, popularized in macroeconometrics by \citet{hamilton1989new}, offer a natural middle path through a latent regime structure.

An influential negative result has shaped two decades of work on this problem.
\citet{ryden1998stylized} fitted Gaussian-emission HMMs at $K = 2$--$3$ states and concluded that the geometric sojourn-time distribution of a standard Markov chain produces exponential ACF decay too fast to match the observed slow decay.
\citet{bulla2006stylized} restored ACF fidelity with hidden semi-Markov models at the cost of substantially increased complexity.

We identify the spectral root cause.
The absolute-return autocovariance of a stationary CHMM admits a closed-form mixture-of-eigenvalues identity $\rho_{|G|}(\tau) = \sum_{k \ge 2} w_k \lambda_k^{\tau}$ over the non-unit eigenvalues of $\mathbf T$ (Section~\ref{sec:theory}); the empirical Ryd\'{e}n et al.\ result is then a $K$-rank statement on $\mathbf T$.
At $K = 2$ the ACF is mono-exponential and matching slow decay forces $|\lambda_2| \to 1$, which collapses the marginal mixture and fails distributional Kolmogorov--Smirnov; at $K \ge 3$ the constraints decouple.
The semi-Markov route injects the missing temporal flexibility through an explicit sojourn distribution; moderate-$K$ CHMM achieves the same end through the spectral richness of $\mathbf T$.

We instantiate this on ten years of daily SPDR S\&P~500 ETF (SPY) data with a continuous HMM trained by Baum-Welch \cite{baum1970maximization, rabiner1986introduction} under quantile-based initialization, in three emission variants sharing identical forward-backward recursions: CHMM-N with Gaussian emissions, CHMM-t with per-state Student-t emissions whose degrees of freedom $\nu_k$ are updated inside the M-step by a golden-section search on the ECM $Q$-function in the tradition of \citet{peel2000robust} and \citet{liu1995ml}, and CHMM-L with Laplace emissions whose location is the weighted median and whose scale is the weighted mean absolute deviation, both in closed form.
Against a six-generator panel (i.i.d.\ bootstrap, Gaussian and Laplace i.i.d., GARCH(1,1), and the three CHMM variants), the CHMM at moderate $K$ attains $93.8\%$ in-sample and $84.2\%$ out-of-sample Kolmogorov--Smirnov pass rates and an absolute-return ACF mean absolute error of $0.0507$, essentially matching GARCH's temporal fidelity without sacrificing distributional fit. The three emission variants land at three distinct points on the kurtosis axis: CHMM-N undershoots ($5.0$ simulated vs.\ $7.7$ observed), CHMM-t overshoots ($14.6$), and CHMM-L matches cleanly ($6.8$).
The unconditional Value-at-Risk and Expected-Shortfall envelopes bracket observed historical values at both $1\%$ and $5\%$ on the in-sample and out-of-sample windows.
Multi-asset synthesis composes cleanly through a rank-based Student-t copula on top of the CHMM marginals (profile MLE selects $\nu^\star = 6$, six-asset off-diagonal correlation MAE $0.027$).
Out-of-sample stationarity is honestly scoped: a five-window rolling-origin evaluation reproduces the headline pass rates on four of five windows, with the $2022$ rate-hike cycle as an explicit counter-example.
