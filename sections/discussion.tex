\paragraph{Replicating the slow-ACF behaviour at $K \ge 3$.}
Two issues compound in the \citet{ryden1998stylized} low-$K$ setup: a single self-transition rate produces a single exponential mode, and 1990s-standard random initialisations often converged to degenerate local optima. Our $K = 2$ replication on SPY (Appendix~\ref{sec:ryden_replication}) confirms the binding constraint is distributional (KS at $K = 2$ drops to $67$ to $72\%$, while ACF-MAE is essentially constant at $\approx 0.050$); with modern initialisation the ACF is reproduced at any $K \ge 2$ and distributional fidelity drives the choice of $K$.

\paragraph{Closing the kurtosis gap with heavy-tailed emissions.}
CHMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ produces simulated IS kurtosis $3.83$ against observed $7.68$: a three-component Gaussian mixture's between-component term is bounded and cannot tile the empirical heavy tail. The shared-$\nu$ Student-$t$ ablation is the headline heavy-tail recipe at $K^\star = 3$ (simulated IS kurtosis $4.68$, no penalty hyperparameter; Appendix~\ref{sec:chmm_t_shared_nu}). The penalised per-state CHMM-t at $\lambda = 20$ overshoots IS kurtosis to $14.91$ ($\lambda$ tuned at $K = 18$, re-used at $K^\star = 3$ as an upper bound; Appendix~\ref{sec:lambda_cv_pre2020}). CHMM-L sits between CHMM-N and CHMM-t at IS kurtosis $5.30$ without any shape parameter; CHMM-GED's bimodal $\hat p_k$ partition (Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_p_partition}) is the data-driven analog of a hand-classified Gaussian-bulk / Laplace-tail hybrid. The four-way variant decision is summarised in the variant decision guide of Appendix~\ref{sec:variant_decision_guide}.

\paragraph{Stationarity scope and operational deployment.}
The CHMM is trained on the assumption that returns are locally stationary across the IS window. Single-name equity returns are well known to carry sector-specific structural breaks (\citealp{pastorstambaugh2001equity, andreoughysels2002breaks, angtimmermann2012regime}). Table~\ref{tab:stationarity_scope} consolidates static-fit OoS performance across the four orthogonal stress sources tested in this paper. Periodic refit (\citealp{pesarantimmermann2007window, cappe2011online}) is the deployment recipe for non-stress equity slices; it does not save GLD or the walk-forward W2 / W4 stress folds, which are regime introductions the IS distribution does not span.

\paragraph{Stylized-fact scope and parameter parsimony.}
The Cont (2001) stylized-fact list also includes the leverage effect (negative $\mathrm{Corr}(G_t, |G_{t+1}|)$ on equity returns); the present paper's body evaluation covers only the three symmetric stylized facts and treats the leverage-axis behaviour of symmetric-emission CHMMs as a deferred topic (Appendix~\ref{sec:leverage_effect}). On parametric complexity, CHMM-N at $K = 18$ has $K(K-1) + 2K = 342$ free parameters (CHMM-t adds $K$ degrees-of-freedom, CHMM-GED adds $K$ shape parameters); the QuantGAN baseline of \citet{wiese2020quantgan} carries on the order of $10^4$ to $10^5$, three orders of magnitude higher.
