Subsections \ref{sec:descriptive}--\ref{sec:cross_asset_univariate} and \ref{sec:var_backtest} operate under \textbf{Pipeline~A} (single-asset CHMM fit per ticker, no cross-asset coupling); only \S\ref{sec:cross_asset} operates under \textbf{Pipeline~B} (Pipeline~A marginals plus a rank-based copula).

\subsection{Descriptive Statistics and Stylized Facts}
\label{sec:descriptive}

All three Cont stylized facts are present in both windows (Figure~\ref{fig:stylized_facts} in Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_stylized_facts}): heavy-tailed marginal (IS excess kurtosis $7.68$, Jarque--Bera $6{,}416.6$, $p < 0.001$), negligible linear autocorrelation in raw $G_t$ (Ljung--Box $\text{LB}_G \approx 5.5$ at lag $20$ vs.\ $\chi^2_{20}(0.05) = 31.4$), and persistent volatility clustering in $|G_t|$ ($\text{LB}_{|G|} = 2{,}959.3$, $p < 0.001$). The OoS window shares the same qualitative profile (excess kurtosis $5.29$).

At the body-headline operating point $K^\star = 6$ (held-out-clean), CHMM-N attains $92.6\%$ IS and $78.3\%$ OoS KS at simulated kurtosis $5.26$ (essentially the OoS observed $5.29$) and $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $0.0502$, on par with GARCH ($0.0485$). With up to $K - 1 = 5$ non-unit eigenvalues at $K^\star = 6$ and $17$ at $K = 18$, the mixture-of-eigenvalues sum~\eqref{eq:acf_normalised} reproduces the slow absolute-return ACF that the \citet{ryden1998stylized} $K = 2$--$3$ Gaussian setup could not recover on the SPY 2014--2024 instance of that experiment (Appendix~\ref{sec:ryden_replication}). The raw-return ACF-MAE column closes the third stylized fact: every CHMM row sits at $0.0234$--$0.0240$, indistinguishable from the i.i.d.\ baselines that have zero linear ACF by construction. The four CHMM emission variants land at distinct points on the kurtosis axis without retuning $K$ or the transition structure: CHMM-N at $K^\star = 6$ matches the OoS observed $5.29$ within $0.83$ units, CHMM-L sits at $5.86$ IS, CHMM-GED at $5.22$ IS, and (in the $K = 18$ sensitivity reference) the penalised CHMM-t with $1/\nu_k$ shrinkage at $\lambda = 20$ provides the cleanest IS / OoS heavy-tail match at $8.56 / 7.07$ against observed $7.68 / 5.29$. CHMM-GED's per-state shape $\hat p_k$ partitions bimodally into a Gaussian-bulk / Laplace-tail structure that replicates across seeds and tickers (Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_p_partition}). On the OoS sample-CRPS, the four CHMM variants are statistically indistinguishable on Diebold--Mariano two-sided tests ($p > 0.45$ within-CHMM); we read this as evidence that the four families are interchangeable on this evaluation rather than as a per-family strength claim, with the family choice driven instead by the per-row kurtosis match (Table~\ref{tab:variant_choice}, \S\ref{sec:discussion}). The MS-GARCH benchmark plateaus at $34$--$36\%$ IS KS across $K \in \{2, 3, 6\}$, so the multi-state benefit is specific to the CHMM scaffold rather than to multi-state regime-switching per se on this dataset (full higher-$K$ panel: Appendix~\ref{sec:extended_baselines}). A six-fold rolling-origin walk-forward (Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_misc}, Table~\ref{tab:walkforward}; body summary: Table~\ref{tab:walkforward_body}) attains median OoS KS $67.7\%$ at $K = 18$ and $62.1\%$ at $K^\star = 3$; the single-window OoS sits at the upper end, with two stress folds (W2 COVID, W4 2022 rate-hike onset) below $10\%$ and explicitly out-of-distribution by KS for every generator in the panel.

\paragraph{ML HSMM at $K^\star = 3$ as a co-headline result.}
Replacing the geometric sojourn distribution with an explicit truncated Pareto and refitting through the explicit-duration forward-backward of \citet{yu2010hidden} gives ML HSMM-N at $K^\star = 3$: $98.4\%$ IS / $\mathbf{91.0\%}$ OoS KS (the highest OoS KS in the panel), at simulated kurtosis $3.46$ and $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $0.0629$. The HSMM and CHMM scaffolds are best read as two complementary attacks on the same Markov backbone with different trade-offs: HSMM wins KS at $K = 3$ but the absolute-return ACF-MAE matches the i.i.d.\ baseline level ($0.0629$) rather than CHMM-N's $0.0467$, because the fitted Pareto sojourn concentrates probability mass on a single low-volatility state. The $K = 18$ extension of the same construction collapses to a near-degenerate optimum on $T_{\text{IS}} = 2{,}516$ and is a companion-paper direction. The numerical full-rank diagnostic on $\hat{\mathbf T}_{\text{HSMM}}$ at $K = 3$, the $\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}$ concentration mechanism, and the higher-$K$ HSMM degeneracy are in Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_hsmm_diagnostic}.

\subsection{Cross-Ticker Generalization (Pipeline~A)}
\label{sec:cross_asset_univariate}

Table~\ref{tab:cross_ticker} reports the penalised CHMM-t aggregate IS and OoS KS distribution across a sector-balanced 30-ticker panel (10 GICS sectors $\times$ 3 large-cap representatives) at $K = 18$, $\lambda = 20$. The IS distribution is tight (median $99.5\%$, $[Q_1, Q_3] = [99.3, 99.6]\%$); the OoS distribution is wider with median $73.4\%$, mean $66.8 \pm 29.5\%$, and $11/30 = 36.7\%$ of tickers below $60\%$, concentrated in single-name regime introductions (LLY $7.6\%$, UNH $14.5\%$, NEM $5.6\%$). A one-way ANOVA on OoS KS by sector returns $F(9, 20) = 0.44$ at $p = 0.90$ with $\eta^2 = 0.16$: the failures are ticker-specific rather than sector-specific (Appendix~\ref{sec:cross_ticker_anova}). A monthly-refit version (Appendix~\ref{sec:cross_ticker_monthly_refit}) interpolates the trade-off further: median OoS KS $\mathbf{86.7\%}$, mean $78.8\%$, and $5/30$ failures, so each shorter refit window yields a roughly proportional further reduction in failure count without saturating. A held-out-clean rebuild at $K^\star = 6$ produces an OoS KS distribution matching the $K = 18$ headline within sampling error (Appendix~\ref{sec:cross_ticker_k6_panel}); the body $K = 18$ panel is retained for kurtosis fidelity and comparability with Tables~\ref{tab:model_comparison} and~\ref{tab:cond_var}.

\subsection{Cross-Asset Dependence (Pipeline~B): Student-t Copula}
\label{sec:cross_asset}

Pipeline~B reuses the per-asset CHMM-N marginals from \S\ref{sec:cross_asset_univariate} and adds a rank-based Student-$t$ dependence layer (\S\ref{sec:cross_asset_methods}). The body cross-asset construction is scoped to a US-equity universe: profile MLE on six US-equity assets (SPY, NVDA, JNJ, JPM, AAPL, QQQ; $200$ paths) selects $\nu^\star = 6$ on the unit-spaced grid $\nu \in [4, 12]$ with Wilks $95\%$ profile-LL CI of $[6, 7]$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_cross_asset}); a half-unit-grid refinement plus $B = 200$ parametric bootstrap CI confirms $[6, 7]$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:copula_halfunit}), excluding the Gaussian limit. A non-equity stress test on GLD and SLV ETFs over the same windows (Appendix~\ref{sec:non_us_asset_supp}) shows OoS KS collapses to $0\%$ on both ETFs under a static IS-fitted CHMM, confirming that the cross-asset claim does not extend to non-equity asset classes without periodic refit; we report this as a documented limitation rather than a deferred follow-up. Table~\ref{tab:cross_asset} reports per-asset KS and the off-diagonal correlation MAE: $0.027$ on IS for the Student-$t$ copula, against $0.030$ Gaussian, $0.068$ truncated C-vine, and $0.076$ SIM (full panel in Appendix~\ref{sec:cross_asset_supp}). On OoS the off-diagonal MAE rises to $0.209$ for Student-$t$ and $0.202$ for Gaussian; the $0.007$ difference is below the simulation-noise floor at $N_{\text{paths}} = 200$. A quarterly-rolling refit on the six-asset universe reduces the OoS off-diagonal MAE to $0.185$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:rolling_copula_supp}).
