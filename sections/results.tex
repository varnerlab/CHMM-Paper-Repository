\subsection{Descriptive Statistics and Stylized Facts}
\label{sec:descriptive}

All three canonical stylized facts are present in both the IS ($T = 2{,}516$) and OoS ($T = 572$) windows.
The return series $G_t$ is the annualised excess log return computed VWAP-to-VWAP from daily SPY ETF data, $G_t = 252 \cdot (\log P^{\text{VWAP}}_t - \log P^{\text{VWAP}}_{t-1}) - r^{\text{rf}}_t$ (the VWAP-to-VWAP convention is robust to opening-auction noise; the $252$ annualisation matches the volatility convention used in the conditional VaR and copula sections; the i.i.d.\ and GARCH baselines below are fit on the same series).
The IS excess growth rates exhibit excess kurtosis of $7.678$, strong negative skewness ($-0.751$), and a Jarque--Bera statistic of $6{,}416.6$, decisively rejecting normality ($p < 0.001$).
The Ljung--Box test on absolute returns rejects no autocorrelation ($\text{LB} = 2{,}959.3$, $p < 0.001$), confirming pronounced volatility clustering.
The Ljung--Box test on raw returns is small at the same lag ($\text{LB}_{G} \approx 5.5$ at lag $20$, well below the $\chi^2_{20}$ critical value of $31.4$), confirming negligible linear autocorrelation.
The OoS window shares the same qualitative profile (excess kurtosis $5.294$, $\text{LB}_{|G|} = 250.6$).
Figure~\ref{fig:stylized_facts} summarises the three stylized facts visually on the IS window: a heavy-tailed marginal that fits the Laplace shape better than the Gaussian (panel a), the corresponding Q-Q deviation from normality (panel b), a raw-return ACF that sits inside the $99\%$ Bartlett band at every lag beyond a small lag-1 microstructure spike (panel c), and a slowly decaying absolute-return ACF that remains outside the band over many lags (panel d).

\subsection{State-Count Selection: Two Reference Operating Points}
\label{sec:k_selection_results}

We sweep $K \in \{3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21\}$ for CHMM-N on the SPY IS window and score each fit on (i) held-out log-likelihood on a clean pre-OoS validation slice (estimation 2014-01-03 to 2021-12-31, validation 2022-01-03 to 2024-01-03), (ii) the four information criteria of \citet{nguyen2018hidden} (Akaike information criterion (AIC), Bayesian information criterion (BIC), Hannan-Quinn criterion (HQC), consistent AIC (CAIC), $p = K^2 + 2K - 1$), and (iii) the IS and OoS two-sample Kolmogorov--Smirnov pass rates over $1{,}000$ simulated paths.

The held-out criterion picks $K^\star = 3$: validation log-likelihood per observation degrades monotonically from $-2.5345$ at $K = 3$ to $-2.5847$ at $K = 21$, a difference of $25.2$ nats over the $T_{\text{val}} = 502$-day validation slice. BIC on the estimation window agrees ($K^\star = 3$). Distributional fidelity, however, rises across the sweep before plateauing: IS KS rises from $89.7\%$ at $K = 3$ to $94.7\%$ at $K = 18$. The absolute-return ACF-MAE is remarkably stable across the entire sweep ($0.047$ to $0.053$), and the raw-return ACF-MAE is essentially flat at $\approx 0.024$ across the same sweep, consistent with the spectral identity~\eqref{eq:acf_normalised}: the absolute-return ACF is determined by the eigenvalue spectrum of $\mathbf T$, which the quantile-initialized Baum-Welch recovers at every $K \ge 3$, and the raw-return ACF is null in expectation under any HMM with i.i.d.\ within-state emissions.

We therefore report two held-out-clean reference operating points throughout this section, alongside an extended-state-resolution reference panel.
$K^\star = 3$ is the held-out-likelihood and BIC choice, the methodologically conservative default for risk-management consumers who require minimum model complexity.
$K^\star = 6$ is the synthetic-data-consumer reference operating point, supported by both held-out criteria on the strictly pre-2020 slice (held-out per-observation log-likelihood $-2.0606$ at $K = 6$ versus $-2.1921$ at $K = 18$, a 13-nat advantage on a 4.5-year window; held-out KS $96.8\%$; Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_misc}) and AIC on the estimation window. Both $K^\star = 3$ and $K^\star = 6$ are strictly held-out-clean.
The $K = 18$ panel is retained as an extended-state-resolution reference because it is the operating point at which the cross-ticker generalisation panel (Section~\ref{sec:cross_asset_univariate}, Table~\ref{tab:cross_ticker}) and the regime-conditional VaR back-test (Section~\ref{sec:var_backtest}, Table~\ref{tab:cond_var}) were constructed; we note up front that the $K = 18$ choice rests on a multi-objective rule combining IS KS plateau, IS / OoS distributional pass rate, and CAIC, that this rule uses the same $2024$--$2026$ window later employed for VaR evaluation, and that none of the six held-out criteria selects $K = 18$ on a $2022$--$2023$ validation slice that does not overlap the OoS window (BIC, HQC, CAIC, and held-out log-likelihood pick $K^\star = 3$; AIC picks $K^\star = 6$; held-out KS picks $K^\star = 9$; Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_misc}).
The cost of moving from $K^\star = 3$ to $K^\star = 6$ is the marginal IS / OoS KS gain reported in Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison}; the cost of moving further to $K = 18$ is the additional $\sim 13$ nats of held-out-likelihood penalty against $K^\star = 6$ on the pre-2020 slice. The complete per-$K$ panel ($K \in \{3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21\}$) and the full held-out-validation sweep are reported in Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_sensitivity} (held-out details in Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_misc}).

\paragraph{ML HSMM at $K^\star = 3$ as a semi-Markov reference.}
We replace the geometric sojourn distribution of the Markov chain with an explicit per-state truncated discrete Pareto distribution and re-fit by maximum likelihood through Yu's (2010) explicit-duration forward-backward over augmented $(s_t, d_t)$ pairs ($D_{\max} = 200$, off-diagonal transitions, Gaussian emissions; full derivation and the complementary plug-in semi-Markov foil in Appendix~\ref{sec:extended_baselines}). At $K^\star = 3$, ML HSMM-N attains $98.4\%$ IS KS and $91.0\%$ OoS KS (the highest OoS KS of any row in Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison}) at simulated kurtosis $3.46$ and $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $0.0629$. The result is a single-metric gain rather than a uniform improvement: ML HSMM-N wins on KS but undershoots simulated kurtosis below CHMM-N at the same $K$ ($3.46$ vs $3.86$, against observed $7.68$ IS), and the absolute-return ACF-MAE matches the i.i.d.\ baseline level ($0.0629$ vs $0.0628$ for the bootstrap and $0.0628$ for the Laplace i.i.d.) rather than CHMM-N's $0.0467$. The natural diagnosis is that the explicit-duration Pareto sojourn at $K = 3$ concentrates probability mass in long-sojourn states and reduces both tail-state realisation frequency (kurtosis down) and within-window absolute-return correlation (ACF-MAE up to baseline); under this reading, the explicit-duration scaffold buys distributional fidelity at $K = 3$ at the cost of the temporal mechanism that CHMM at $K \ge 3$ recovers through the non-unit spectrum of $\mathbf T$, and the body framing is best read as ``two complementary scaffolds at the same Markov backbone, with different trade-offs across the three Cont stylized facts'' rather than a strict comparison. The same Yu (2010) construction at $K = 18$ collapses to a near-degenerate local optimum on $T_{\text{IS}} = 2{,}516$ (IS KS $0.8\%$, simulated kurtosis $3.65$): the joint $K^2 + KD_{\max}$ free-parameter count exceeds the effective IS sample size, so the higher-$K$ ML HSMM is best read as a companion-paper direction at longer training windows or under a constrained sojourn parameterisation. Heavy-tailed emissions and higher-$K$ ML HSMM are companion-paper directions on this scaffold.

\paragraph{Numerical full-rank diagnostic on $\hat{\mathbf T}_{\text{HSMM}}$ at $K = 3$.}
The off-diagonal jump matrix of the fitted ML HSMM at $K = 3$ has eigenvalues $\{1.000, 0.823, 0.177\}$ and singular values $\{1.150, 0.762, 0.166\}$; the deflated jump matrix $\mathbf A - \mathbf 1 \bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$ is full rank at $K - 1 = 2$ with non-trivial eigenvalues $\{0.823, 0.177\}$ (numerical rank $2$ at tolerance $10^{-10}$). The dominant non-unit modulus $|\lambda_2| = 0.823$ is comparable to the CHMM-N at $K = 3$ value $|\lambda_2| = 0.953$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:spectral_rank}), so the spectral mechanism of the off-diagonal jump matrix is not collapsed and option~(a) of two candidate diagnoses (a near-zero $|\lambda_2|$) is not the cause of the $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE regression to the i.i.d.\ baseline level. The active mechanism is option~(b): the fitted Pareto sojourn distribution at $K = 3$ concentrates probability mass on a single low-volatility state (the stationary distribution of $\mathbf A$ is $\bar{\boldsymbol\pi} \approx (0,\,1,\,0)$ with $\sigma_2 = 0.97$, the lowest variance of the three states), so under simulation the chain spends long stretches inside a single state's i.i.d.\ emissions and the within-state i.i.d.\ baseline dominates the ACF integration over the IS window. The regression is therefore a sojourn-support mechanism rather than a spectral-collapse mechanism, consistent with the body framing in the preceding paragraph.

The CHMM emission variants land at distinct points on the kurtosis axis without retuning $K$ or the transition structure: CHMM-N undershoots ($5.0$ IS vs.\ observed $7.7$), unpenalised CHMM-t overshoots ($14.4$, the per-state $\nu_k$ ECM lower-bracket pinning artefact discussed in Section~\ref{sec:discussion}), CHMM-L matches cleanly ($6.6$, within $1.1$ units of observed), CHMM-GED sits adaptively between the closed-form anchors at $5.2$, and the penalised CHMM-t with $1/\nu_k$ shrinkage at $\lambda = 20$ (Section~\ref{sec:discussion}, Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_misc}) is the cleanest single-row match at $8.56$ IS / $7.07$ OoS while retaining the same KS pass rate as unpenalised CHMM-t to within $1$ percentage point. CHMM-GED also attains the highest IS Kolmogorov-Smirnov pass rate among the four variants ($95.2\%$) at the same parameter count as CHMM-t. All four variants match each other on KS pass rates to within $2$ percentage points IS and within $5$ percentage points OoS; on the absolute-return ACF-MAE they sit within $0.006$ of each other and on the raw-return ACF-MAE within $0.0003$. The emission-family swap moves tail weight cleanly without disturbing the central or temporal fit.

\paragraph{The CHMM-GED $\hat p_k$ partition: data chooses Gaussian-bulk / Laplace-tail.}
CHMM-GED carries a per-state shape $p_k \in [p_{\min}, p_{\max}] = [0.5, 3.0]$ that nests Gaussian ($p = 2$) and Laplace ($p = 1$) as boundary cases (Section~\ref{sec:mstep}). The empirical fit on SPY IS at $K = 18$ partitions states bimodally (Figure~\ref{fig:p_hist}): thirteen states attain the upper bracket at $p_k = 3.0$ (Gaussian-or-beyond shape), one state lands at $p_k = 1.6$, and four states cluster in the Laplace regime $p_k \in [0.86, 1.24]$. The smallest fitted $p_k$ is $0.86$, well clear of every $p_{\min}$ tested in the bracket sweep (Appendix~\ref{sec:nu_diagnostics}), so the lower bracket is non-binding. The four Laplace-shape states are concentrated in the high-volatility tail of the state ordering: ranks $13$, $15$, and $18$ by variance-equivalent $\sigma$ all sit near $\hat p_k = 1$. This is the data-driven analog of a hand-classified Gaussian-bulk / Laplace-tail hybrid, found by ECM rather than imposed a priori. The partition replicates across $10$ Monte Carlo seeds (every seed yields the same partition counts to within one state) and across the cross-asset universe (Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_robustness}); we read it as the most distinctive empirical finding of the four-emission scaffold.

On a proper scoring rule the CHMM family ties or beats every benchmark, ruling out the concern that the headline KS finding is a non-proper-metric artefact. No single benchmark dominates every metric: the i.i.d.\ bootstrap is the strongest distributional baseline but the worst on temporal fidelity ($|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $0.0628$); GARCH(1,1)-$t$ wins the volatility-clustering metric ($0.0316$) but fails KS ($57.3\%$ IS); the CHMM at $K = 18$ occupies the joint-fit corner. Headline pass rates are stable under multi-seed and KS-power-calibration controls (ten-seed std $\le 3.6$pp on OoS KS; KS-power positive control passes at $90\%$ asymptotic, $98$--$99\%$ block-bootstrap; Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_robustness}, \ref{sec:ks_power}, \ref{sec:ks_block_bootstrap}); the seed-to-seed standard deviation refers to ten alternative global seeds, not within-path SEs at the headline single-seed run.

\subsection{Cross-Ticker Generalization (Pipeline~A)}
\label{sec:cross_asset_univariate}

Still under Pipeline~A: each ticker is fit independently, no cross-asset coupling.
Table~\ref{tab:cross_ticker} reports the penalised CHMM-t aggregate IS and OoS KS distribution across a sector-balanced 30-ticker panel (ten GICS sectors, three large-cap representatives per sector; full universe: AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, JNJ, UNH, LLY, JPM, BAC, WFC, AMZN, HD, MCD, NFLX, VZ, DIS, CAT, BA, HON, PG, KO, WMT, XOM, CVX, COP, NEE, DUK, SO, FCX, NEM, APD), each fit independently at the extended-state-resolution reference $K = 18$ under the $1/\nu_k$ shrinkage prior at $\lambda = 20$.
The cross-ticker panel is reported at $K = 18$ rather than the held-out-clean $K^\star = 6$ to maintain comparability with the SPY extended-state-resolution panel in Section~\ref{sec:model_comparison} and the regime-conditional VaR construction in Section~\ref{sec:var_backtest}; the held-out-clean SPY-only Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison} block already establishes the $K^\star = 6$ headline.
CHMM-t is the natural generalisation vehicle here because it carries the strongest OoS KS in the SPY headline panel and supplies the per-state heavy tails that the outlier-prone single-name tickers require, and the penalised variant tames the per-state $\nu_k$ ECM lower-bracket pinning that drives the unpenalised cross-ticker kurtosis overshoots (Appendix~\ref{sec:cross_ticker_per_family_kurt}); per-family $K$-sensitivity for SPY across all four emission variants and per-ticker price-level metrics across all four families are in Appendix~\ref{sec:multi_emission_sensitivity}.

The aggregate IS KS distribution is tight: median $99.5\%$ with $[Q_1, Q_3] = [99.3, 99.6]\%$ across the 30 tickers; $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE has median $0.0416$ ($[Q_1, Q_3] = [0.0368, 0.0494]$) and the simulated-versus-observed kurtosis residual sits at median $0.61$ kurtosis units with $[Q_1, Q_3] = [-0.72, 3.37]$ (the residual is positive on heavy-tailed defensives where the penalised shrinkage is the binding constraint, negative on light-tailed broad-equity ETFs).
The OoS KS distribution is wider and exposes the stationarity-scope limit at the universe scale: median $73.4\%$ with $[Q_1, Q_3] = [49.1, 94.3]\%$, mean $66.8 \pm 29.5\%$, and $11/30 = 36.7\%$ of tickers below the $60\%$ pass threshold.
Per-sector rollup (median OoS KS across the three representatives, full per-ticker panel in Appendix~\ref{sec:cross_ticker_sector_panel}, Table~\ref{tab:sector_panel}): Utilities $96.2\%$, Information Technology $95.1\%$, Consumer Staples $92.7\%$, Industrials $78.9\%$, Materials $76.6\%$, Energy $70.3\%$, Consumer Discretionary $66.6\%$, Financials $57.6\%$, Communication Services $49.1\%$, Health Care $14.5\%$.
The Health Care collapse is the largest sector-level outlier and is driven by single-name regime introductions in the test slice (LLY: weight-loss-drug regime introduction, OoS KS $7.6\%$; UNH: 2024 healthcare-policy compression, OoS KS $14.5\%$) on top of JNJ which generalises cleanly (OoS KS $93.2\%$). Financials and Communication Services replicate the rate-repricing and streaming-platform-regime patterns that the original six-ticker spot-check flagged on JPM, NVDA.
Top three OoS-KS-failure tickers are NEM (Materials, $5.6\%$), LLY (Health Care, $7.6\%$), UNH (Health Care, $14.5\%$); the next two failures are NEE (Utilities, $24.0\%$) and WMT (Consumer Staples, $24.0\%$).
We acknowledge the OoS cliffs as stationarity-scope limitations rather than model-selection issues; the pattern persists at every $K$ in the sweep, and we discuss the operational implication (periodic refit at deployment) in Section~\ref{sec:discussion}.
