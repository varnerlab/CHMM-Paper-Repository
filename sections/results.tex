\subsection{Descriptive Statistics and Stylized Facts}
\label{sec:descriptive}

All three Cont stylized facts are present in both windows (Figure~\ref{fig:stylized_facts} in Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_stylized_facts}): heavy-tailed marginal (IS excess kurtosis $7.68$, OoS $5.29$), negligible linear autocorrelation in raw $G_t$, and persistent volatility clustering in $|G_t|$. A stationary block bootstrap at mean block length $L = 20$ gives broadly overlapping IS / OoS $95\%$ CIs ($[2.17, 12.40]$ and $[0.90, 8.26]$; Appendix~\ref{sec:kurtosis_bootstrap_ci}); kurtosis-fidelity targets should be read against the joint envelope rather than the point estimates.

\subsection{State-Count Selection}
\label{sec:k_selection_results}

We sweep $K \in \{3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21\}$ for CHMM-N on the IS window and select $K^\star$ by held-out per-observation log-likelihood and held-out KS under four-fold and six-fold rolling-origin CV on the strictly pre-2020 slice (Appendix~\ref{sec:k_selection_kfold_pre2020}). At both cadences the $K = 3$ vs $K = 6$ comparison sits inside sampling noise ($|z| \le 0.07$ on mean held-out log-likelihood with the sign flipping between fold designs); BIC also picks $K = 3$. Under parsimony we take $\mathbf{K^\star = 3}$ as the body operating point. Higher state counts are decisively worse on held-out criteria. Absolute-return ACF-MAE is essentially flat ($0.047$--$0.053$) across the entire sweep, consistent with the spectral identity~\eqref{eq:acf_normalised}.

The cross-generator ranking is robust to the distributional-metric choice (KS, Anderson--Darling, Wasserstein-1, Hellinger; Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_metrics}). The MS-GARCH benchmark plateaus at $34$--$36\%$ IS KS in our Nelder-Mead fit and at $0$--$0.1\%$ in the reference Bayesian re-run (Appendix~\ref{sec:extended_baselines}); the multi-state benefit is specific to the CHMM scaffold rather than to multi-state regime-switching per se. A six-fold rolling-origin walk-forward (Table~\ref{tab:walkforward_body}) attains median OoS KS $62.1\%$ at $K^\star = 3$, with two stress folds (W2 COVID, W4 2022 rate-hike onset) below $10\%$ that every generator in the panel rejects; the walk-forward median is the operationally informative summary for production deployment.

\paragraph{Per-state interpretability.}
The fitted CHMM-N states at $K^\star = 3$ partition cleanly along the conventional volatility axis (high-/low-/moderate-vol with the natural drift signs); the bimodal CHMM-GED $\hat p_k$ partition (Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_p_partition}) reflects the same axis at $K = 18$ with Laplace-like states in the high-volatility tail and Gaussian-like states in the bulk. The latent states map onto identifiable economic regimes at every $K$ examined.

\paragraph{ML HSMM as a co-headline result.}
Replacing the geometric sojourn distribution with an explicit truncated Pareto and refitting through the explicit-duration forward-backward of \citet{yu2010hidden} gives ML HSMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ at $98.4\%$ IS / $\mathbf{91.0\%}$ OoS KS, the strongest single-window OoS KS row in the panel and beating every CHMM operating point. CHMM and ML HSMM are best read as complementary scaffolds on the same Markov backbone, with KS / ACF / kurtosis trade-offs that select between them by use case. The HSMM advantage on KS is offset by an absolute-return ACF-MAE that matches the i.i.d.\ baseline level ($0.0629$) rather than CHMM-N's $0.0460$, because the fitted Pareto sojourn concentrates probability mass on a single low-volatility state and the regime-driven slow-ACF mechanism that the CHMM exploits at $K \ge 3$ is muted (per-state $\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}$ concentration in Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_hsmm_diagnostic}). Higher-$K$ HSMM variants (Pareto collapse at $K = 18$; discrete-Gamma-sojourn fix achieving $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $\mathbf{0.0462}$ on IS at $K = 18$) are reported in Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_hsmm_diagnostic} and \ref{sec:hsmm_gamma_sojourn}. Readers selecting a synthetic-data generator on raw OoS KS alone should prefer ML HSMM-N at $K^\star = 3$; CHMM at $K^\star = 3$ is the right choice when the structural use cases of \S\ref{sec:var_backtest}--\ref{sec:cross_asset} are in scope.

\paragraph{Bootstrap as a non-parametric synthetic-data benchmark.}
The stationary block bootstrap of \citet{politis1994stationary} attains $99.7\%$ IS / $92.1\%$ OoS KS in Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison}, above every CHMM operating point. CHMM differentiates on use cases the bootstrap cannot serve: regime-conditional VaR (\S\ref{sec:var_backtest}; the bootstrap is exchangeable and admits no forward state filter), multi-asset copula composition (\S\ref{sec:cross_asset}; needs parametric marginals), and privacy / licensing (every bootstrap path is a permutation of literal IS observations).

A block-bootstrap KS recalibration at $L = 20$ (the appropriate null under autocorrelated daily returns; \citealp{politis1994stationary}) preserves the cross-generator ordering on both windows, with the absolute level dropping $\sim 5$pp on IS and $20$--$30$pp on OoS; the cross-generator ranking is the robust comparison (Appendix~\ref{sec:ks_block_bootstrap}, Table~\ref{tab:ks_block_body}).

\subsection{Cross-Ticker Generalization (Pipeline~A)}
\label{sec:cross_asset_univariate}

Table~\ref{tab:cross_ticker} reports the penalised CHMM-t IS / OoS KS distribution across a sector-balanced 30-ticker panel ($10$ GICS sectors $\times 3$ large-cap representatives, $\lambda = 20$) at the body operating point $K^\star = 3$. The IS distribution is concentrated (median $96.8\%$); the OoS distribution is wider with median $69.1\%$, mean $66.2 \pm 28.2\%$, and $11/30$ tickers below $60\%$, concentrated in single-name regime introductions (LLY $8.7\%$, UNH $16.5\%$, NEM $5.0\%$). A one-way ANOVA on OoS KS by sector at the original $n = 3$ per-sector design is severely underpowered ($\eta^2 = 0.16$ at $F(9, 20) = 0.44$, $p = 0.90$); an $n = 6$ per-sector expansion to a 60-ticker panel (Appendix~\ref{sec:sector_panel_n6}) returns $F(9, 50) = 0.37$, $p = 0.946$, $\eta^2 = 0.062$, with the effect-size estimate dropping by more than half. The 60-ticker aggregate distribution is statistically indistinguishable from the 30-ticker version, so the visible concentration of failures on regime-introduction tickers is a per-ticker observation rather than evidence about sector-level structure. The operational recipe is periodic refit: at the $K = 18$ kurtosis-fidelity sensitivity reference, a quarterly-refit version (Appendix~\ref{sec:cross_ticker_quarterly_refit}) shifts the OoS KS median from $73.4\%$ to $\mathbf{83.0\%}$ and reduces failures from $11/30$ to $7/30$ (a monthly cadence interpolates to $86.7\%$ / $5/30$ at $3\times$ compute). Sensitivity panels at $K = 6$ and $K = 18$ and the SPY-level shared-$\nu$ Student-$t$ alternative are in Appendix~\ref{sec:cross_ticker_k6_panel} and \ref{sec:chmm_t_shared_nu}.

\paragraph{Cross-decade validation.}
A CRSP-sourced cross-decade split (1994--2004 IS / 2004--2006 OoS; full panel in Appendix~\ref{sec:cross_decade_validation}) tests whether the body's stylized-fact reproduction transfers across non-overlapping decades. The IS axis is decade-robust: at $K^\star = 3$ CHMM-N attains $84$--$90\%$ IS KS pass rate against the $90$--$94\%$ obtained on the body 2014--2024 IS window (full $K \in \{3, 18\}$ panel in Appendix~\ref{sec:cross_decade_validation}). The 2004--2006 OoS pass rate, however, collapses to $3$--$5\%$ because the calm post-dot-com bull-market slice has observed excess kurtosis $0.06$ (essentially Gaussian) against the 1994--2004 IS kurtosis of $3.05$. The cross-decade pattern matches the W2 (COVID 2020) and W4 (2022 rate-hike) walk-forward stress folds (Table~\ref{tab:walkforward_body}, both with OoS KS $< 10\%$): the IS axis transfers cleanly across decades, but a static-IS-fitted CHMM cannot bracket OoS slices whose marginal moves outside the IS regime library. The operational implication parallels the cross-ticker panel above: the static-fit OoS pass rate is OoS-slice-specific and the deployment recipe is the periodic-refit construction (the cross-ticker quarterly-refit panel in Appendix~\ref{sec:cross_ticker_quarterly_refit} and the rolling-copula construction of \S\ref{sec:cross_asset}), not the single-window static fit.

\subsection{Cross-Asset Dependence (Pipeline~B): Student-t Copula}
\label{sec:cross_asset}

Pipeline~B reuses the per-asset CHMM-N marginals from \S\ref{sec:cross_asset_univariate} and adds a rank-based Student-$t$ dependence layer (\S\ref{sec:cross_asset_methods}). The body cross-asset construction is scoped to a US-equity universe: profile MLE on six US-equity assets (SPY, NVDA, JNJ, JPM, AAPL, QQQ; $200$ paths) selects $\nu^\star = 6$ on the unit-spaced grid $\nu \in [4, 12]$ with Wilks $95\%$ profile-LL CI of $[6, 7]$ \emph{on the IS slice} (Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_cross_asset}); a full one-shot MLE jointly maximising over $(\Sigma, \nu)$ confirms the choice is robust to the two-step estimator (Appendix~\ref{sec:full_tcopula_mle}).
