Subsections \ref{sec:descriptive}--\ref{sec:cross_asset_univariate} and \ref{sec:var_backtest} operate under \textbf{Pipeline~A} (single-asset CHMM fit per ticker, no cross-asset coupling); only \S\ref{sec:cross_asset} operates under \textbf{Pipeline~B} (Pipeline~A marginals plus a rank-based copula).

\subsection{Descriptive Statistics and Stylized Facts}
\label{sec:descriptive}

All three Cont stylized facts are present in both windows (Figure~\ref{fig:stylized_facts} in Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_stylized_facts}): heavy-tailed marginal (IS excess kurtosis $7.68$, Jarque--Bera $6{,}416.6$, $p < 0.001$), negligible linear autocorrelation in raw $G_t$ (Ljung--Box $\text{LB}_G \approx 5.5$ at lag $20$ vs.\ $\chi^2_{20}(0.05) = 31.4$), and persistent volatility clustering in $|G_t|$ ($\text{LB}_{|G|} = 2{,}959.3$, $p < 0.001$). The OoS window shares the same qualitative profile (excess kurtosis $5.29$).

\subsection{State-Count Selection}
\label{sec:k_selection_results}

We sweep $K \in \{3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21\}$ for CHMM-N on the IS window and score each fit on held-out log-likelihood (estimation 2014--2021, validation 2022--2023), the four information criteria of \citet{nguyen2018hidden} (AIC, BIC, HQC, CAIC), and IS / OoS KS pass rates over $1{,}000$ simulated paths. Held-out log-likelihood and BIC pick $K^\star = 3$; AIC picks $K^\star = 6$; held-out KS picks $K^\star = 9$; absolute-return ACF-MAE is essentially flat ($0.047$--$0.053$) across the entire sweep, consistent with the spectral identity~\eqref{eq:acf_normalised}. We report two held-out-clean reference operating points throughout, $K^\star = 3$ and $K^\star = 6$, plus an extended-state-resolution reference $K = 18$ at which the cross-ticker (Table~\ref{tab:cross_ticker}) and regime-conditional VaR (Table~\ref{tab:cond_var}) panels are constructed. The $K = 18$ choice rests on a multi-objective rule that uses the OoS window and is therefore \emph{not} held-out-clean; full per-$K$ panel and held-out details are in Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_sensitivity} and~\ref{sec:supp_misc}.

At the held-out-clean $K^\star = 6$ operating point, CHMM-N attains $92.6\%$ IS and $78.3\%$ OoS KS at simulated kurtosis $5.26$ (essentially the OoS observed $5.29$) and $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $0.0502$, on par with GARCH ($0.0485$). With up to $K - 1 = 5$ non-unit eigenvalues at $K^\star = 6$ and $17$ at $K = 18$, the mixture-of-eigenvalues sum~\eqref{eq:acf_normalised} reproduces the slow absolute-return ACF that the \citet{ryden1998stylized} $K = 2$--$3$ Gaussian setup could not recover. The raw-return ACF-MAE column closes the third stylized fact: every CHMM row sits at $0.0234$--$0.0240$, statistically indistinguishable from the i.i.d.\ baselines that have zero linear ACF by construction. The four CHMM emission variants land at distinct points on the kurtosis axis without retuning $K$ or the transition structure: CHMM-N undershoots, unpenalised CHMM-t overshoots, CHMM-L matches at $6.6$, CHMM-GED at $5.2$, and the penalised CHMM-t with $1/\nu_k$ shrinkage at $\lambda = 20$ is the cleanest single-row IS match at $8.56$. CHMM-GED's per-state shape $\hat p_k$ partitions bimodally into a Gaussian-bulk / Laplace-tail structure that replicates across seeds and tickers (Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_p_partition}). On the OoS sample-CRPS, CHMM-N at $K = 18$ attains the lowest mean ($1.0384$) and the four CHMM variants tie or beat every benchmark on a proper scoring rule (Diebold-Mariano two-sided $p > 0.45$ for within-CHMM differences). A six-fold rolling-origin walk-forward (Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_misc}, Table~\ref{tab:walkforward}) attains median OoS KS $67.7\%$ at $K = 18$ and $62.1\%$ at $K^\star = 3$; the single-window OoS sits at the upper end, with two stress folds (W2 COVID, W4 2022 rate-hike onset) below $10\%$.

\paragraph{ML HSMM at $K^\star = 3$ as a co-headline result.}
Replacing the geometric sojourn distribution with an explicit truncated Pareto and refitting through Yu's (2010) explicit-duration forward-backward gives ML HSMM-N at $K^\star = 3$: $98.4\%$ IS / $\mathbf{91.0\%}$ OoS KS (the highest OoS KS in the panel), at simulated kurtosis $3.46$ and $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $0.0629$. The HSMM and CHMM scaffolds are best read as two complementary attacks on the same Markov backbone with different trade-offs: HSMM wins KS at $K = 3$ but the absolute-return ACF-MAE matches the i.i.d.\ baseline level ($0.0629$) rather than CHMM-N's $0.0467$, because the fitted Pareto sojourn concentrates probability mass on a single low-volatility state. The $K = 18$ extension of the same construction collapses to a near-degenerate optimum on $T_{\text{IS}} = 2{,}516$ and is a companion-paper direction. The numerical full-rank diagnostic on $\hat{\mathbf T}_{\text{HSMM}}$ at $K = 3$, the $\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}$ concentration mechanism, and the higher-$K$ HSMM degeneracy are in Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_hsmm_diagnostic}.

\subsection{Cross-Asset Dependence (Pipeline~B): Student-t Copula}
\label{sec:cross_asset}

Pipeline~B reuses the per-asset CHMM-N marginals from \S\ref{sec:cross_asset_univariate} and adds a rank-based Student-$t$ dependence layer (\S\ref{sec:cross_asset_methods}). Profile MLE on a six-asset universe (SPY, NVDA, JNJ, JPM, AAPL, QQQ; $200$ paths) selects $\nu^\star = 6$ on the unit-spaced grid $\nu \in [4, 12]$ with Wilks $95\%$ profile-LL CI of $[6, 7]$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_cross_asset}); a half-unit-grid refinement plus $B = 200$ parametric bootstrap CI confirms $[6, 7]$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:copula_halfunit}), excluding the Gaussian limit. Table~\ref{tab:cross_asset} reports per-asset KS and the off-diagonal correlation MAE: $0.027$ on IS for the Student-$t$ copula, against $0.030$ Gaussian, $0.068$ truncated C-vine, and $0.076$ SIM (full panel in Appendix~\ref{sec:cross_asset_supp}). On OoS the off-diagonal MAE rises to $0.209$ for Student-$t$ and $0.202$ for Gaussian; the $0.007$ difference is below the simulation-noise floor at $N_{\text{paths}} = 200$. A quarterly-rolling refit on the six-asset universe reduces the OoS off-diagonal MAE to $0.185$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:rolling_copula_supp}).
