This section derives the spectral mechanism behind the absolute-return ACF (the central technical contribution) and states the assumptions under which it holds.
Identifiability, MLE consistency, ECM monotonicity, rank-reordering marginal preservation, and the filter-VaR mixture-quantile inequality are each one-line specialisations of standard results; we collect them as cited propositions in Appendix~\ref{sec:supplementary} with one-sentence proof sketches.

\subsection{Assumptions}
\label{sec:assumptions}

\begin{assumption}[Irreducibility and aperiodicity]
\label{ass:irred}
The transition matrix $\mathbf T \in \R^{K \times K}$ is irreducible
and aperiodic on the state space $\{1, \ldots, K\}$.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Finite second moment and emission contrast]
\label{ass:moments}
Each per-state emission density $b_k(\cdot;\boldsymbol\theta_k)$ has
finite second moment, $\E_{b_k}[G^2] < \infty$, and at least two
states differ in either location $\mu_k$ or scale $\sigma_k$.
\end{assumption}

Under Assumption~\ref{ass:irred} the chain $\mathbf T$ has a unique
stationary distribution $\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}$ satisfying
$\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}\,\mathbf T = \bar{\boldsymbol\pi}$, and admits a
spectral decomposition with $\lambda_1 = 1$ a simple eigenvalue
and all other eigenvalues $\lambda_2, \ldots, \lambda_K$ strictly inside
the unit disk \citep{levinperes2017markov}.

\subsection{Spectral mechanism for the absolute-return ACF}
\label{sec:acf_mechanism}

Let $G_t$ be a stationary CHMM, $\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}$ the stationary
distribution of $\mathbf T$, and define the per-state absolute-return
moments
\begin{equation}
    m_k \;=\; \E\!\left[\,|G_t|\;\big|\; s_t = k\right],
    \qquad
    M_k \;=\; \E\!\left[\,G_t^2\;\big|\; s_t = k\right],
    \qquad k = 1, \ldots, K.
    \label{eq:per_state_moments}
\end{equation}
Conditioning on the latent state pair $(s_t, s_{t+\tau})$ and using that emissions are conditionally independent given the state sequence,
\begin{equation*}
    \E\!\left[|G_t|\,|G_{t+\tau}|\right]
    = \sum_{i,j} \bar{\boldsymbol\pi}_i\, (\mathbf T^\tau)_{ij}\, m_i\, m_j
    = \mathbf m^\top \mathrm{diag}(\bar{\boldsymbol\pi})\, \mathbf T^\tau\, \mathbf m,
\end{equation*}
with $\mathbf m = (m_1, \ldots, m_K)^\top$.
Under Assumption~\ref{ass:irred} the spectral decomposition of $\mathbf T$ reads $\mathbf T^\tau = \mathbf 1\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top + \sum_{k=2}^K \lambda_k^\tau\, \mathbf v_k \mathbf w_k^\top$ with $(\mathbf v_k, \mathbf w_k)$ the right and left eigenvectors of $\mathbf T$ associated with $\lambda_k$ (normalised so that $\mathbf w_k^\top \mathbf v_k = 1$). The first term contributes $(\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top \mathbf m)^2 = (\E|G_t|)^2$, and subtracting the product of marginals yields the autocovariance
\begin{equation}
    \mathrm{Cov}\!\left(|G_t|,\, |G_{t+\tau}|\right)
    \;=\; \sum_{k=2}^{K} c_k\, \lambda_k^{\tau},
    \qquad
    c_k = \big(\mathbf m^\top \mathrm{diag}(\bar{\boldsymbol\pi})\, \mathbf v_k\big)\big(\mathbf w_k^\top \mathbf m\big),
    \label{eq:acf_exact}
\end{equation}
and the autocorrelation
\begin{equation}
    \rho_{|G|}(\tau)
    \;=\; \sum_{k=2}^{K} w_k\, \lambda_k^{\tau},
    \qquad
    w_k = c_k / \sigma_{|G|}^2.
    \label{eq:acf_normalised}
\end{equation}
Equation~\eqref{eq:acf_normalised} is the closed-form mixture-of-eigenvalues identity for the absolute-return ACF: the autocorrelation at every lag is a weighted sum of geometric decays at the non-unit eigenvalues of $\mathbf T$.
The same derivation with $|G_t|$ replaced by $G_t^2$ and $\mathbf m$ by $(M_1, \ldots, M_K)^\top$ gives the analogous form for the squared-return ACF.

\paragraph{Ryd\'{e}n separation as a $K$-rank statement.}
At $K = 2$, equation~\eqref{eq:acf_normalised} reduces to the mono-exponential form $\rho_{|G|}(\tau) = w_2\, \lambda_2^\tau$, and matching a target slow-decay rate $\rho^*(\tau) = \exp(-\tau/\tau^*)$ on a horizon $\tau \in \{1, \ldots, L\}$ with $\tau^* \ge L$ forces $|\lambda_2| \ge \exp(-1/\tau^*)$, i.e.\ a near-unit second eigenvalue.
The eigenvalues of a $2 \times 2$ stochastic matrix are $\lambda_1 = 1$ and $\lambda_2 = T_{11} + T_{22} - 1$, so $|\lambda_2| \to 1$ requires both diagonal entries near unity; under Assumption~\ref{ass:moments} this concentrates $\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}$ near a vertex of the simplex and the marginal mixture collapses to a single emission density with the wrong tail, failing distributional KS.
At $K \ge 3$ the right-hand side admits at least two non-unit eigenvalues, the ACF can match $\rho^*(\tau)$ over the same horizon with each individual $|\lambda_k|$ bounded away from $1$, and the distributional-versus-temporal tension breaks.
This is the spectral content of the empirical Ryd\'{e}n et al.\ low-$K$ failure: at $K = 2$ the ACF and the marginal are coupled through a single eigenvalue, so the model cannot match both simultaneously; at $K \ge 3$ they decouple.
The empirical correlate is that ACF-MAE is essentially flat across $K \in \{3, 6, \ldots, 21\}$ while KS pass rates rise (Section~\ref{sec:results}).

The spectral identity~\eqref{eq:acf_normalised} is the mechanism behind the moderate-$K$ ACF claim. Under standard EM monotonicity \citep{wu1983convergence, mengrubin1993ecm}, the per-state $\nu_k$ ECM update of Section~\ref{sec:mstep} preserves the incomplete-data log-likelihood as non-decreasing on the compact bracket $[\nu_{\min}, \nu_{\max}]$; the closed-form weighted-median / weighted-MAD M-step for Laplace emissions \citep{rabiner1986introduction} preserves the same guarantee. Identifiability up to label permutation in all three emission families (CHMM-N, CHMM-t, CHMM-L) follows from \citet{allman2009identifiability} together with \citet{yakowitzspragins1968}, and MLE consistency from \citet{bickel1998asymptotic} and \citet{doucmoulinesryden2004} under Assumptions~\ref{ass:irred}--\ref{ass:moments}. Formal statements with proof sketches are collected in Appendix~\ref{sec:supplementary}.
