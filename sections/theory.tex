\subsection{Spectral Mechanism for the Absolute-Return ACF}
\label{sec:theory}

This subsection states the spectral mechanism behind the absolute-return ACF and the assumptions under which it holds. The closed-form bilinear identity $\mathbf m^\top \mathrm{diag}(\bar{\boldsymbol\pi})\,\mathbf T^\tau\,\mathbf m$ for the cross-moment of $|G_t|$ at lag $\tau$ and its eigendecomposition over the non-unit eigenvalues of $\mathbf T$ are textbook material in the regime-switching literature (\citealp{hamilton1994time, krolzig1997markov}; the moment-generating-function form for general Markov-switching specifications is given by \citealp{timmermann2000moments}). Our contribution is not the identity itself but its explicit application to recast the empirical \citet{ryden1998stylized} low-$K$ failure as a rank statement on the deflated transition matrix $\mathbf T - \mathbf 1\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$, and the empirical demonstration that the rank constraint binds only at $K \le 3$ on equity-return data (Section~\ref{sec:results}).
Identifiability, MLE consistency, ECM monotonicity, and rank-reordering marginal preservation are each one-line specialisations of standard results; we collect them as cited propositions in Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_propositions} with one-sentence proof sketches.

\paragraph{Assumptions.}
\label{sec:assumptions}

\begin{assumption}[Irreducibility and aperiodicity]
\label{ass:irred}
The transition matrix $\mathbf T \in \R^{K \times K}$ is irreducible
and aperiodic on the state space $\{1, \ldots, K\}$.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Finite second moment and emission contrast]
\label{ass:moments}
Each per-state emission density $b_k(\cdot;\boldsymbol\theta_k)$ has
finite second moment, $\E_{b_k}[G^2] < \infty$, and at least two
states differ in either location $\mu_k$ or scale $\sigma_k$.
\end{assumption}

Under Assumption~\ref{ass:irred} the chain $\mathbf T$ has a unique
stationary distribution $\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}$ satisfying
$\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}\,\mathbf T = \bar{\boldsymbol\pi}$, and admits a
spectral decomposition with $\lambda_1 = 1$ a simple eigenvalue
and all other eigenvalues $\lambda_2, \ldots, \lambda_K$ strictly inside
the unit disk \citep{levinperes2017markov}.

\paragraph{Spectral mechanism.}
\label{sec:acf_mechanism}

Let $G_t$ be a stationary CHMM and $\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}$ the stationary
distribution of $\mathbf T$. Define the per-state absolute-return moment $m_k$ and the per-state second moment $M_k$ jointly,
\begin{equation}
    m_k \;=\; \E\!\left[\,|G_t|\;\big|\; s_t = k\right],
    \qquad
    M_k \;=\; \E\!\left[\,G_t^2\;\big|\; s_t = k\right],
    \qquad k = 1, \ldots, K,
    \label{eq:per_state_moments}
\end{equation}
and stack them into vectors $\mathbf m = (m_1, \ldots, m_K)^\top$ and $\mathbf M = (M_1, \ldots, M_K)^\top$. The autocovariance derivation below uses $\mathbf m$ for $\rho_{|G|}(\tau)$ at $\tau \ge 1$; the lag-zero discussion at the end of the paragraph picks up $M_k$ where the conditional product is the per-state second moment rather than $m_k^2$.

Conditioning on the latent state pair $(s_t, s_{t+\tau})$ and using that emissions are conditionally independent given the state sequence,
\begin{equation*}
    \E\!\left[|G_t|\,|G_{t+\tau}|\right]
    = \sum_{i,j} \bar{\boldsymbol\pi}_i\, (\mathbf T^\tau)_{ij}\, m_i\, m_j
    = \mathbf m^\top \mathrm{diag}(\bar{\boldsymbol\pi})\, \mathbf T^\tau\, \mathbf m.
\end{equation*}
Under Assumption~\ref{ass:irred} the spectral decomposition of $\mathbf T$ reads $\mathbf T^\tau = \mathbf 1\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top + \sum_{k=2}^K \lambda_k^\tau\, \mathbf v_k \mathbf w_k^\top$ with $(\mathbf v_k, \mathbf w_k)$ the right and left eigenvectors of $\mathbf T$ associated with $\lambda_k$ (normalised so that $\mathbf w_k^\top \mathbf v_k = 1$). The first term contributes $(\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top \mathbf m)^2 = (\E|G_t|)^2$, and subtracting the product of marginals yields the autocovariance
\begin{equation}
    \mathrm{Cov}\!\left(|G_t|,\, |G_{t+\tau}|\right)
    \;=\; \sum_{k=2}^{K} c_k\, \lambda_k^{\tau},
    \qquad
    c_k = \big(\mathbf m^\top \mathrm{diag}(\bar{\boldsymbol\pi})\, \mathbf v_k\big)\big(\mathbf w_k^\top \mathbf m\big),
    \label{eq:acf_exact}
\end{equation}
and the autocorrelation
\begin{equation}
    \rho_{|G|}(\tau)
    \;=\; \sum_{k=2}^{K} w_k\, \lambda_k^{\tau},
    \qquad
    w_k = c_k / \sigma_{|G|}^2.
    \label{eq:acf_normalised}
\end{equation}
Equation~\eqref{eq:acf_normalised} is the closed-form mixture-of-eigenvalues identity for the absolute-return ACF: at every positive lag $\tau \ge 1$ the autocorrelation is a real-coefficient sum of geometric decays at the non-unit eigenvalues of $\mathbf T$. The $w_k$ are real coefficients (not non-negative weights), but their cumulative sum is non-negative because it equals the between-state variance ratio of $|G_t|$. Complex-conjugate $\lambda_k$ pairs combine into damped oscillatory modes of the form $|\lambda_k|^\tau \cos(\tau \arg \lambda_k + \phi_k)$; non-diagonalisable $\mathbf T$ adds polynomial-times-geometric prefactors via Jordan blocks. The lag-zero behaviour, the squared-return ACF, and a self-contained proof are in Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_spectral_acf}.

\paragraph{Ryd\'{e}n separation as a $K$-rank statement.}
At $K = 2$, equation~\eqref{eq:acf_normalised} reduces to the mono-exponential form $\rho_{|G|}(\tau) = \lambda_2^\tau$ with $\lambda_2 = T_{11} + T_{22} - 1$; slow decay is matched by tuning $T_{11} + T_{22}$. The temporal axis is therefore satisfiable; the binding axis is the two-component marginal, which cannot match the empirical kurtosis target. At $K \ge 3$ the matrix admits multiple non-unit eigenvalues and the marginal mixture has enough components to match the observed tail. The Ryd\'{e}n et al.\ low-$K$ failure is therefore a rank statement on two coupled axes: $\mathrm{rank}(\mathbf T - \mathbf 1 \bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top) \le K - 1$ bounds the decay modes available to $\rho_{|G|}(\tau)$, and $K$ simultaneously bounds the marginal mixture's component count. Empirically, ACF-MAE is essentially flat across $K \in \{3, 6, \ldots, 21\}$ while KS rises (Section~\ref{sec:results}); a direct effective-rank diagnostic (Appendix~\ref{sec:spectral_rank}) shows a single non-unit eigenvalue carries $93.6\%$ of the lag-1 ACF at $K = 18$ on SPY, so the $K$-rank upper bound is non-binding at $K \ge 3$ on this instance. The cumulative-contribution profile is monotone and concentrated: Table~\ref{tab:spectral_modes} reports the leading non-unit modes at $K = 18$; three modes carry $\ge 95\%$ of the lag-1 ACF and twelve modes carry $\ge 99\%$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:spectral_rank}, full per-mode panel). Repeating the diagnostic on the 30-ticker cross-section (Appendix~\ref{sec:spectral_rank_xticker}) gives a wider distribution: the cross-ticker median dominant-mode share is $0.76$, the interquartile range is $[0.66, 0.86]$, and the minimum is $0.33$ on NEM. The SPY value is therefore in the right tail of the cross-ticker distribution, and the rank-non-binding claim is supported at the cross-ticker median rather than as a universal property of equity-return data.
