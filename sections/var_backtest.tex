\subsection{Value-at-Risk and Expected-Shortfall Back-Test (Pipeline~A)}
\label{sec:var_backtest}
\label{sec:utility}

The headline of this subsection is the regime-conditional Christoffersen-cc result: a VaR threshold built from the CHMM one-step-ahead state forecast passes the joint conditional-coverage test cleanly at every $(K, \alpha)$ on OoS, where every constant-across-$t$ generator (CHMM unconditional, GARCH, bootstrap) rejects breach independence.

\paragraph{Envelope bracketing and Kupiec coverage.}
All four CHMM variants bracket observed VaR and ES at $1\%$ and $5\%$ within the $[5, 95]\%$ envelope on both windows; the IS CHMM-N median $\text{VaR}_{0.01}$ at $-6.64$ (observed $-6.38$) is the closest point estimate in the panel, with envelopes $\sim 1.7\times$ tighter than GARCH's (Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_var_envelope}). All four CHMM rows pass the unconditional Kupiec test on both windows ($\text{LR}_{\text{uc}} \in [3.03, 3.83]$ at $\alpha = 0.05$ OoS, against $\chi^2_1(0.05) = 3.841$); we treat Kupiec as a sanity check at this $T_{\text{OoS}}$ and the conditional construction as the substantive diagnostic.

\paragraph{Regime-conditional Christoffersen-cc \emph{(headline)}.}
At each OoS day $t$ we run the forward filter through $\mathcal F_{t-1} = R_{\text{IS}} \cup R_{\text{oos}}[1{:}t-1]$ to get the one-step-ahead state forecast
\begin{equation}
    \Prob(s_t = k \mid \mathcal F_{t-1}) = \sum_i \Prob(s_{t-1} = i \mid \mathcal F_{t-1})\, T_{ik},
    \label{eq:filter}
\end{equation}
and define $\widehat{\text{VaR}}_t(\alpha) = F_t^{-1}(\alpha)$ where $F_t(\cdot) = \sum_k \Prob(s_t = k \mid \mathcal F_{t-1})\, F_k(\,\cdot\,;\, \boldsymbol\theta_k)$ is the family-appropriate predictive mixture CDF under IS-fixed $(\mathbf T, \{\boldsymbol\theta_k\})$, with $F_k$ the per-state Gaussian, Student-$t$, Laplace, or GED CDF (the body Gaussian construction reduces to $F_k = \Phi(\,\cdot\,;\, \mu_k, \sigma_k)$). Table~\ref{tab:cond_var} reports the back-test for CHMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ and $K = 18$ and the penalised CHMM-t at $\lambda = 20$ on OoS; every row passes Kupiec, Christoffersen-ind, and Christoffersen-cc cleanly at $\alpha = 0.05$. The improvement in the independence statistic from the unconditional variant ($\text{LR}_{\text{ind}} = 5.26$ at $K = 18, \alpha = 0.05$) to the conditional variant ($\text{LR}_{\text{ind}} = 0.52$) is the regime-switching value proposition that the unconditional Kupiec headline did not exercise. The conditional pass extends to every emission family with the family-appropriate predictive density (CHMM-L, CHMM-GED) at $p_{\text{cc}} \ge 0.089$ throughout, so the regime-switching value proposition is intrinsic to the latent-state forecast and not specific to the Gaussian emission family (Appendix~\ref{sec:christoffersen_supp}, Table~\ref{tab:cond_var_all_families}).

\paragraph{Power caveat at $\alpha = 0.01$.}
The Christoffersen-cc test is power-bounded at $T_{\text{OoS}} = 572$, $\alpha = 0.01$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:christoffersen_power}: $\ge 80\%$ power only at clustering eigenvalue $\rho \ge 0.50$); the substantive risk-management diagnostic is therefore the $\alpha = 0.05$ row, where the Engle--Manganelli (2004) Dynamic Quantile test (Appendix~\ref{sec:engle_manganelli_dq}) also passes cleanly at both $K = 3$ ($p = 0.156$) and $K = 18$ ($p = 0.678$). \textbf{At $\alpha = 0.01$ the DQ test rejects conditional coverage at $K = 18$ ($p = 0.017$)} while Christoffersen-cc does not; the regime-conditional VaR over-couples high-volatility states at the strict tail at $K = 18$ on this OoS slice and the $\alpha = 0.01$ Christoffersen-cc pass is a power-driven non-rejection.

The state-filter VaR pipeline of Eq.~\eqref{eq:filter} applies to any multi-state regime-switching model; the CHMM-vs-MS-GARCH choice is dominated by the marginal-distribution column of Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison} (CHMM-N at $89.7\%$ IS KS vs MS-GARCH plateaus at $0$--$36\%$). The construction extends to four-family ablation, six-fold walk-forward, and quarterly refit (Appendices~\ref{sec:christoffersen_supp}, \ref{sec:supp_misc}, \ref{sec:quarterly_refit_cond_var}); the conditional Christoffersen-cc passes at $\alpha = 0.05$ on $19/24$ walk-forward rows, with failures concentrated on the W2 (COVID) and W4 (2022 rate-hike onset) stress folds that every generator rejects, and faster refit cadences do not close the W2 gap (Appendix~\ref{sec:walkforward_refit_cadence}). BH-FDR at $\text{FDR} = 0.05$ across the $40$-test combined panel retains $37/40$ rows on the full panel and $32/32$ on the non-stress subset; the three persistent rejections are all on the W2 fold.
