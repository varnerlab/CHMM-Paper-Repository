\subsection{Value-at-Risk and Expected-Shortfall Back-Test (Pipeline~A)}
\label{sec:var_backtest}
\label{sec:utility}

The headline of this subsection is the regime-conditional Christoffersen-cc result: a VaR threshold built from the CHMM one-step-ahead state forecast passes the joint conditional-coverage test cleanly at every $(K, \alpha)$ on OoS, where every constant-across-$t$ generator (CHMM unconditional, GARCH, bootstrap) rejects breach independence. We organise the diagnostics in three layers: (i) envelope bracketing, (ii) unconditional Kupiec coverage, (iii) the regime-conditional construction.

\paragraph{(i) Envelope bracketing.}
All four CHMM variants bracket observed historical VaR and ES at $1\%$ and $5\%$ within the $[5, 95]\%$ envelope on both windows; the IS CHMM-N median $\text{VaR}_{0.01}$ at $-6.64$ (observed $-6.38$) is the closest point estimate among the six benchmark generators, with envelopes roughly $1.7\times$ tighter than GARCH's. The full envelope panel for Bootstrap, GARCH, CHMM-N/-t/-L/-GED at $1\%$ and $5\%$ is in Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_var_envelope}, Table~\ref{tab:var_es}.

\paragraph{(ii) Unconditional Kupiec coverage.}
The four CHMM rows pass the unconditional Kupiec test on both windows: at $\alpha = 0.05$ on OoS, CHMM-N $\text{LR}_{\text{uc}} = 3.83$ against $\chi^2_1(0.05) = 3.841$, with the other three CHMM variants in $3.03$--$3.83$. The repeated $3.83$ across rows is a feature of $T_{\text{OoS}} = 572$: the Kupiec statistic is a function of the integer breach count and at $\alpha = 0.05$ the values $k \in \{14, 19, 20\}$ produce statistics that pile against the critical value rather than spreading continuously. We treat unconditional Kupiec as a sanity check at this $T_{\text{OoS}}$ and the conditional construction below as the substantive risk-management diagnostic.

\paragraph{(iii) Regime-conditional Christoffersen-cc \emph{(headline)}.}
At each OoS day $t$ we run the forward filter through $\mathcal F_{t-1} = R_{\text{IS}} \cup R_{\text{oos}}[1{:}t-1]$ to get the one-step-ahead state forecast
\begin{equation}
    \Prob(s_t = k \mid \mathcal F_{t-1}) = \sum_i \Prob(s_{t-1} = i \mid \mathcal F_{t-1})\, T_{ik},
    \label{eq:filter}
\end{equation}
and define $\widehat{\text{VaR}}_t(\alpha) = F_t^{-1}(\alpha)$ where $F_t(\cdot) = \sum_k \Prob(s_t = k \mid \mathcal F_{t-1})\, F_k(\,\cdot\,;\, \boldsymbol\theta_k)$ is the family-appropriate predictive mixture CDF under IS-fixed $(\mathbf T, \{\boldsymbol\theta_k\})$, with $F_k$ the per-state Gaussian, Student-$t$, Laplace, or GED CDF (the body Gaussian construction reduces to $F_k = \Phi(\,\cdot\,;\, \mu_k, \sigma_k)$). Table~\ref{tab:cond_var} reports the back-test for CHMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ and $K = 18$ and the penalised CHMM-t at $\lambda = 20$ on OoS; every row passes Kupiec, Christoffersen-ind, and Christoffersen-cc cleanly at $\alpha = 0.05$. The improvement in the independence statistic from the unconditional variant ($\text{LR}_{\text{ind}} = 5.26$ at $K = 18, \alpha = 0.05$) to the conditional variant ($\text{LR}_{\text{ind}} = 0.52$) is the regime-switching value proposition that the unconditional Kupiec headline did not exercise. The conditional pass extends to every emission family with the family-appropriate predictive density (CHMM-L, CHMM-GED) at $p_{\text{cc}} \ge 0.089$ throughout, so the regime-switching value proposition is intrinsic to the latent-state forecast and not specific to the Gaussian emission family (Appendix~\ref{sec:christoffersen_supp}, Table~\ref{tab:cond_var_all_families}).

\paragraph{Power caveat at $\alpha = 0.01$.}
A Monte Carlo power calibration at $T_{\text{OoS}} = 572$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:christoffersen_power}, Table~\ref{tab:christoffersen_power}, $B = 5{,}000$ replicates per cell) shows the Christoffersen-cc test is correctly sized at $4$--$5\%$ Type-I error under the i.i.d. null at both $\alpha$ levels, but the power profile is asymmetric. At $\alpha = 0.05$ ($\sim 28.6$ expected breaches) the test reaches $\ge 80\%$ power against breach-clustering eigenvalues $\rho \ge 0.20$; at $\alpha = 0.01$ ($\sim 5.7$ expected breaches) it reaches $\ge 80\%$ power only at $\rho \ge 0.50$, with rejection rate $42.6\%$ at $\rho = 0.20$. The body $\alpha = 0.05$ ``clean pass at every $(K, \alpha)$'' rows of Table~\ref{tab:cond_var} therefore carry strong power against moderate clustering; the $\alpha = 0.01$ rows should be read as ``not detectably worse than a strongly-clustered alternative'' rather than ``calibrated against any plausible alternative''. The substantive risk-management diagnostic in this paper is therefore the $\alpha = 0.05$ row at the $T_{\text{OoS}} = 572$ length used here; tighter $\alpha$ levels require either larger $T_{\text{OoS}}$ or a higher-power conditional-coverage test.

\paragraph{Multiple-testing correction across the panel.}
The combined conditional-coverage panel reports $40$ Christoffersen-cc tests across $(K, \alpha, \text{family})$ on the headline OoS window (16 rows in Table~\ref{tab:cond_var_all_families}) plus $(\text{fold}, K, \alpha)$ on the walk-forward (24 rows in Table~\ref{tab:walkforward_cond_var}). Reviewer feedback noted that ``$19/24$ pass'' and ``passes at every $(K, \alpha)$'' are panel-level rather than per-row claims and require correction for multiple testing. Applying the Benjamini--Hochberg false-discovery-rate procedure at $\text{FDR} = 0.05$ to the $40$ p-values: the headline single-OoS-window panel passes $16/16$ rows under BH (minimum $q$-value $0.376$, well above $0.05$); the walk-forward panel passes $21/24$ rows (BH actually accepts two more rows than the uncorrected test, because the $\text{W4}, K=18, \alpha=0.01$ marginal failure at raw $p = 0.022$ and the $\text{W2}, K=3, \alpha=0.05$ row at $p = 0.010$ no longer cross the BH ladder); and the combined panel passes $37/40$ rows under BH at FDR $0.05$. The three persistent rejections are all on the W2 COVID fold ($p < 10^{-3}$ in each case), which the univariate walk-forward already flags as out-of-distribution. Excluding W2 and W4, the conditional VaR passes $32/32$ tests under BH. The substantive read is: the FDR-corrected joint pass-rate is $37/40$ (full panel) and $32/32$ (non-stress subset), so the panel-level conditional-coverage claim survives multiple-testing correction. Under the more conservative Bonferroni rule (per-test threshold $0.05/40 = 0.00125$), the same three W2 rows reject and all others pass.
