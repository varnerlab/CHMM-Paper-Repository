\subsection{Value-at-Risk and Expected-Shortfall Back-Test (Pipeline~A)}
\label{sec:var_backtest}
\label{sec:utility}

The headline of this subsection is the regime-conditional Christoffersen-cc result: a VaR threshold built from the CHMM one-step-ahead state forecast passes the joint conditional-coverage test cleanly at every $(K, \alpha)$ on OoS, where every constant-across-$t$ generator (CHMM unconditional, GARCH, bootstrap) rejects breach independence. We organise the diagnostics in three layers: (i) envelope bracketing, (ii) unconditional Kupiec coverage, (iii) the regime-conditional construction.

\paragraph{(i) Envelope bracketing.}
All four CHMM variants bracket observed historical VaR and ES at $1\%$ and $5\%$ within the $[5, 95]\%$ envelope on both windows; the IS CHMM-N median $\text{VaR}_{0.01}$ at $-6.64$ (observed $-6.38$) is the closest point estimate among the six benchmark generators, with envelopes roughly $1.7\times$ tighter than GARCH's. The full envelope panel for Bootstrap, GARCH, CHMM-N/-t/-L/-GED at $1\%$ and $5\%$ is in Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_var_envelope}, Table~\ref{tab:var_es}.

\paragraph{(ii) Unconditional Kupiec coverage.}
The four CHMM rows pass the unconditional Kupiec test on both windows: at $\alpha = 0.05$ on OoS, CHMM-N $\text{LR}_{\text{uc}} = 3.83$ against $\chi^2_1(0.05) = 3.841$, with the other three CHMM variants in $3.03$--$3.83$. The repeated $3.83$ across rows is a feature of $T_{\text{OoS}} = 572$: the Kupiec statistic is a function of the integer breach count and at $\alpha = 0.05$ the values $k \in \{14, 19, 20\}$ produce statistics that pile against the critical value rather than spreading continuously. We treat unconditional Kupiec as a sanity check at this $T_{\text{OoS}}$ and the conditional construction below as the substantive risk-management diagnostic.

\paragraph{(iii) Regime-conditional Christoffersen-cc \emph{(headline)}.}
At each OoS day $t$ we run the forward filter through $\mathcal F_{t-1} = R_{\text{IS}} \cup R_{\text{oos}}[1{:}t-1]$ to get the one-step-ahead state forecast
\begin{equation}
    \Prob(s_t = k \mid \mathcal F_{t-1}) = \sum_i \Prob(s_{t-1} = i \mid \mathcal F_{t-1})\, T_{ik},
    \label{eq:filter}
\end{equation}
and define $\widehat{\text{VaR}}_t(\alpha) = F_t^{-1}(\alpha)$ where $F_t(\cdot) = \sum_k \Prob(s_t = k \mid \mathcal F_{t-1})\, F_k(\,\cdot\,;\, \boldsymbol\theta_k)$ is the family-appropriate predictive mixture CDF under IS-fixed $(\mathbf T, \{\boldsymbol\theta_k\})$, with $F_k$ the per-state Gaussian, Student-$t$, Laplace, or GED CDF (the body Gaussian construction reduces to $F_k = \Phi(\,\cdot\,;\, \mu_k, \sigma_k)$). Table~\ref{tab:cond_var} reports the back-test for CHMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ and $K = 18$ and the penalised CHMM-t at $\lambda = 20$ on OoS; every row passes Kupiec, Christoffersen-ind, and Christoffersen-cc cleanly at $\alpha = 0.05$. The improvement in the independence statistic from the unconditional variant ($\text{LR}_{\text{ind}} = 5.26$ at $K = 18, \alpha = 0.05$) to the conditional variant ($\text{LR}_{\text{ind}} = 0.52$) is the regime-switching value proposition that the unconditional Kupiec headline did not exercise. The conditional pass extends to every emission family with the family-appropriate predictive density (CHMM-L, CHMM-GED) at $p_{\text{cc}} \ge 0.089$ throughout, so the regime-switching value proposition is intrinsic to the latent-state forecast and not specific to the Gaussian emission family (Appendix~\ref{sec:christoffersen_supp}, Table~\ref{tab:cond_var_all_families}).

\paragraph{Power caveat at $\alpha = 0.01$.}
A Monte Carlo power calibration at $T_{\text{OoS}} = 572$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:christoffersen_power}, Table~\ref{tab:christoffersen_power}, $B = 5{,}000$ replicates per cell) shows the Christoffersen-cc test is correctly sized at $4$--$5\%$ Type-I error under the i.i.d. null at both $\alpha$ levels, but the power profile is asymmetric. At $\alpha = 0.05$ ($\sim 28.6$ expected breaches) the test reaches $\ge 80\%$ power against breach-clustering eigenvalues $\rho \ge 0.20$; at $\alpha = 0.01$ ($\sim 5.7$ expected breaches) it reaches $\ge 80\%$ power only at $\rho \ge 0.50$, with rejection rate $42.6\%$ at $\rho = 0.20$. The body $\alpha = 0.05$ ``clean pass at every $(K, \alpha)$'' rows of Table~\ref{tab:cond_var} therefore carry strong power against moderate clustering; the $\alpha = 0.01$ rows should be read as ``not detectably worse than a strongly-clustered alternative'' rather than ``calibrated against any plausible alternative''. The Engle--Manganelli (2004) Dynamic Quantile (DQ) test as a higher-power conditional-coverage alternative (Appendix~\ref{sec:engle_manganelli_dq}, Table~\ref{tab:engle_manganelli_dq}) is the substantive $\alpha = 0.01$ test on this $T_{\text{OoS}}$. At $\alpha = 0.05$ the DQ test passes cleanly at both $K = 3$ ($p = 0.156$) and $K = 18$ ($p = 0.678$), confirming the Christoffersen-cc reading at this $\alpha$. \textbf{At $\alpha = 0.01$ the DQ test rejects conditional coverage at $K = 18$ ($p = 0.017$)}, while Christoffersen-cc does not reject at the same row ($p = 0.137$). DQ has the higher power of the two tests at this $T_{\text{OoS}}$ (and is the test we trust at $\alpha = 0.01$); the substantive read is therefore that the regime-conditional VaR \emph{over-couples high-volatility states at the strict tail at $K = 18$} on this OoS slice, and that the $\alpha = 0.01$ Christoffersen-cc pass is a power-driven non-rejection rather than a calibrated pass. The risk-management diagnostic in this paper is therefore the $\alpha = 0.05$ row under both tests (clean pass on every $(K, \text{family})$ row); the $\alpha = 0.01$ row at $K = 18$ should be read as a DQ-rejection of conditional coverage that tighter $\alpha$ levels require either larger $T_{\text{OoS}}$ or a model rebuild to address.

\paragraph{State-filter pipeline applies to other multi-state models.}
\label{par:msgarch_cond_var}
The state-filter VaR pipeline of Eq.~\eqref{eq:filter} also applies to other multi-state regime-switching models; an MS-GARCH counterfactual at $K \in \{2, 3, 4\}$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:msgarch_conditional_var}) confirms the conditional-coverage benefit is not CHMM-specific. The CHMM-vs-MS-GARCH choice is therefore dominated by the marginal-distribution column of Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison}, not by the conditional-coverage column.

Walk-forward stability across six rolling-origin folds is in Table~\ref{tab:walkforward_cond_var} (Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_misc}); the four-family extension at the headline OoS window is in Table~\ref{tab:cond_var_all_families} (Appendix~\ref{sec:christoffersen_supp}); a quarterly-refit version (CHMM-N refit every $63$ trading days on a rolling $5$-year window) is in Table~\ref{tab:cond_var_quarterly_refit} (Appendix~\ref{sec:quarterly_refit_cond_var}). The conditional Christoffersen-cc passes at $\alpha = 0.05$ on $19/24$ walk-forward rows, with the failures concentrated on the W2 (COVID) and W4 (2022 rate-hike onset) stress folds that the univariate walk-forward already flags as out-of-distribution; the quarterly refit tightens VaR coverage but does not strictly improve the cc-statistic on $T_{\text{OoS}} = 572$. \textbf{Faster refit cadences do not close the W2 stress-fold rejection}: a within-fold refit-cadence sweep at monthly and weekly cadence on each walk-forward fold (Appendix~\ref{sec:walkforward_refit_cadence}, Table~\ref{tab:walkforward_refit_cadence}) shows W2 still rejects at every cadence ($p_{\text{cc}} = 0.011 \to 0.017 \to 0.023$ across fold-IS-fixed, monthly, weekly), and monthly refit \emph{introduces} new failures on W3 and W4 from refit-cycle parameter drift that the fold-IS-fixed baseline does not have. \textbf{Daily refit (the practical limit of the cadence sweep, equivalent at the cadence boundary to a Cappé (2011) stochastic-recursion online-EM) makes W2 strictly worse}: under a rolling 5y daily-refit window at $K^\star = 3$, $\alpha = 0.05$, W2 attains $21/253$ breaches ($8.30\%$ vs nominal $5\%$) with $p_{\text{cc}} = 0.023$ and DQ $p = 0.001$, and at $\alpha = 0.01$ attains $12/253$ breaches ($4.74\%$ vs nominal $1\%$) with both $p_{\text{cc}} \le 10^{-3}$ and DQ $p \le 10^{-3}$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:walkforward_online_em}, Table~\ref{tab:walkforward_online_em}). The W2 daily-refit rolling train window tracks the rising COVID volatility and continuously tightens the VaR threshold, producing more breaches in the few catastrophic March 2020 days than the IS-fixed baseline does, not fewer. The W2 rejection is therefore an intrinsic regime-break failure rather than a tracking-lag issue: no cadence in the explored ladder (fold-IS-fixed, $63$d quarterly, $21$d monthly, $5$d weekly, $1$d daily) closes it, and the daily-refit limit makes it worse. Closing W2 / W4 requires a model class with explicit regime-introduction handling (skew-emission HMMs at the marginal axis or change-point / Bayesian online-changepoint constructions on the transition matrix) rather than a faster refit cadence on the symmetric scaffold.

BH-FDR correction at $\text{FDR} = 0.05$ across the combined conditional-coverage panel ($40$ tests across the headline OoS window plus the six-fold walk-forward) retains the panel-level pass-rate at $37/40$ rows on the full panel and $32/32$ on the non-stress subset (Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_misc}); the three persistent rejections are all on the W2 COVID fold.
