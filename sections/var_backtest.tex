\subsection{Value-at-Risk and Expected-Shortfall Back-Test (Pipeline~A)}
\label{sec:var_backtest}
\label{sec:utility}

Still under Pipeline~A (Figure~\ref{fig:pipeline_schematic}): VaR and Expected-Shortfall are evaluated on the per-ticker SPY simulations, no cross-asset coupling.
A financial-risk consumer cares about (i) whether the simulated envelope brackets observed historical VaR / ES, (ii) whether the unconditional breach rate matches nominal, and (iii) whether breaches are conditionally independent across $t$. The headline contribution is (iii): a regime-conditional VaR built from the CHMM one-step-ahead state forecast passes the Christoffersen joint conditional-coverage test cleanly at every $(K, \alpha)$ on OoS, where every constant-across-$t$ generator (CHMM, GARCH, bootstrap) rejects independence.

\paragraph{(i) Envelope bracketing.}
We simulate $1{,}000$ independent paths from each of the six most relevant generators (Bootstrap, GARCH(1,1), CHMM-N/-t/-L/-GED) and report median and $[5\%, 95\%]$ envelope across paths in Table~\ref{tab:var_es}.
All four CHMM variants bracket observed historical VaR and ES at both $1\%$ and $5\%$ levels within the $[5\%, 95\%]$ envelopes on both windows; the IS CHMM-N median $\text{VaR}_{0.01}$ at $-6.64$ (observed $-6.38$) and $\text{ES}_{0.01}$ at $-8.80$ (observed $-9.00$) are the closest point estimates across the six generators. The CHMM envelopes are roughly $1.7\times$ tighter than GARCH's. CHMM-t's IS kurtosis overshoot does \emph{not} translate into a systematically more conservative VaR/ES point estimate; CHMM-L is mildly more conservative than CHMM-N, and CHMM-GED sits between the two, consistent with their kurtosis residuals.

\paragraph{(ii) Unconditional Kupiec coverage.}
The four CHMM rows pass the unconditional Kupiec test cleanly on both windows: at $\alpha = 0.05$ on OoS, CHMM-N $\text{LR}_{\text{uc}} = 3.83$ against $\chi^2_1(0.05) = 3.841$, CHMM-t $3.83$, CHMM-L $3.03$, CHMM-GED $3.83$. The repeated $3.83$ across rows is a feature of $T_{\text{OoS}} = 572$: the Kupiec statistic is a function of the integer breach count $k$, and at $\alpha = 0.05$ the values $k \in \{14, 19, 20\}$ produce $\text{LR}_{\text{uc}} \in \{3.03, 3.83, 3.83\}$ that pile against the $\chi^2_1$ critical value rather than spreading continuously. We treat the unconditional Kupiec as a sanity check rather than the primary diagnostic at this $T_{\text{OoS}}$; the substantive risk-management diagnostic is the conditional construction below.

\paragraph{(iii) Regime-conditional Christoffersen-cc \emph{(headline)}.}
The Christoffersen \cite{christoffersen1998evaluating} independence ($\text{LR}_{\text{ind}} \sim \chi^2_1$) and joint conditional-coverage ($\text{LR}_{\text{cc}} = \text{LR}_{\text{uc}} + \text{LR}_{\text{ind}} \sim \chi^2_2$) tests check whether breaches cluster in time. Under the unconditional pooled-archive variant (constant-across-$t$ VaR threshold from the IS-only simulated envelope), every panel generator rejects independence on the OoS window at $\alpha = 0.05$ for both VaR levels (CHMM-N at $5\%$: $\text{LR}_{\text{ind}} = 5.26$, $p = 0.022$; $\text{LR}_{\text{cc}} = 9.08$, $p = 0.011$; full panel in Appendix~\ref{sec:christoffersen_supp}, Table~\ref{tab:christoffersen_var}). The rejection is structurally driven by volatility-clustering in $R_{\text{oos}}$ that any unconditional generator cannot track.

The regime-conditional variant addresses this directly. At each OoS day $t$, run the forward filter through $\mathcal F_{t-1} = R_{\text{IS}} \cup R_{\text{oos}}[1{:}t-1]$ to get the one-step-ahead state forecast
\begin{equation}
    \Prob(s_t = k \mid \mathcal F_{t-1}) = \sum_i \Prob(s_{t-1} = i \mid \mathcal F_{t-1})\, T_{ik},
    \label{eq:filter}
\end{equation}
and define $\widehat{\text{VaR}}_t(\alpha) = F_t^{-1}(\alpha)$ where $F_t(\cdot) = \sum_k \Prob(s_t = k \mid \mathcal F_{t-1})\,\Phi(\,\cdot\,;\, \mu_k, \sigma_k)$ is the $K$-component Gaussian-mixture predictive CDF under IS-fixed $(\mathbf T, \{\mu_k, \sigma_k\})$. Table~\ref{tab:cond_var} reports the back-test for CHMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ and $K = 18$ on OoS.

The conditional construction passes all three tests at every $(K, \alpha)$, with breach rates $1.57\%$ and $4.55$--$6.12\%$ against nominal $1\%$ and $5\%$. The independence-test improvement from the unconditional variant ($\text{LR}_{\text{ind}} = 5.26$ at $K = 18, \alpha = 0.05$) to the conditional variant ($\text{LR}_{\text{ind}} = 0.52$) is the regime-switching value proposition that the unconditional Kupiec headline did not exercise: the latent-state forecast tightens the threshold in clustered-volatility windows so that breaches do not pile up at sub-runs of high volatility. The conditional Christoffersen-cc is the substantive risk-management contribution of the paper; the unconditional Kupiec serves as a sanity check at this $T_{\text{OoS}}$. The construction reuses IS-fixed CHMM-N parameters, requires no retuning of $K$ on a stress slice, and runs the forward filter on the OoS series under frozen parameters. The conditional pass extends to every emission family with the family-appropriate predictive density (CHMM-t at $\lambda = 20$, CHMM-L, CHMM-GED): all sixteen $(K, \alpha, \text{family})$ rows pass Kupiec, Christoffersen-ind, and Christoffersen-cc at $\alpha = 0.05$ with $p_{\text{cc}} \ge 0.089$ throughout (Appendix~\ref{sec:christoffersen_supp}, Table~\ref{tab:cond_var_all_families}). The regime-switching value proposition is therefore intrinsic to the latent-state forecast and not specific to the Gaussian emission family. The full unconditional Christoffersen panel across the six benchmark rows is in Appendix~\ref{sec:christoffersen_supp}.
