\subsection{Data and Excess Growth Rates}
\label{sec:data}

The single-asset analysis uses daily SPDR S\&P~500 ETF (SPY) prices from January~3, 2014 to January~3, 2024 (a ten-year training window, $T_{\text{IS}} = 2{,}516$), with the period from January~4, 2024 through April~20, 2026 ($T_{\text{OoS}} = 572$) held out.
Cross-ticker generalization additionally covers NVIDIA (NVDA), Johnson \& Johnson (JNJ), JPMorgan Chase (JPM), Apple (AAPL), and the Invesco QQQ Trust (QQQ).
Prices are split-adjusted but not dividend-adjusted; a full description of the data sources and the Polygon-to-Alpaca extension boundary is given in the Data and Software Acknowledgments at the end of the manuscript.
For ticker $i$ and trading day $j$, the annualized excess growth rate is
\begin{equation}
    G_{i,j} \equiv \frac{1}{\Delta t} \ln\!\left(\frac{P_{i,j}}{P_{i,j-1}}\right) - r_f,
    \qquad \Delta t = 1/252, \quad r_f = 0.
    \label{eq:growth_rate}
\end{equation}
The factor $1/\Delta t$ is a constant rescaling that puts the daily log-return on an annualised axis; all CHMM fits, simulations, and per-path metrics (KS, ACF, kurtosis, VaR / ES) are evaluated on the daily-scale series $G_{i,j}$ defined above, and the annualisation is a notational convenience that does not affect any reported metric. The $r_f = 0$ choice is a normalisation rather than a literal excess-return construction; using a non-zero risk-free rate would shift every reported VaR and ES point estimate by the same constant and leave the distributional and ACF metrics unchanged.
Throughout, $P_{i,j}$ is the daily session volume-weighted average price (VWAP) reported by the data provider, rather than the closing print; the choice is uniform across all tickers, all windows, and all runners, so every reported metric (Kolmogorov--Smirnov pass rate, $\text{ACF}(|G_t|)$ mean absolute error, kurtosis, Value-at-Risk and Expected-Shortfall envelopes, cross-asset off-diagonal MAE) is computed on VWAP-to-VWAP log returns.

The empirical study is organised along two pipelines, illustrated in Figure~\ref{fig:pipeline_schematic}. \emph{Pipeline~A} fits a CHMM to each ticker independently and evaluates single-asset marginal, tail, and temporal metrics; this is the scope of the SPY headline panel (Section~\ref{sec:model_comparison}), the cross-ticker generalisation (Section~\ref{sec:cross_asset_univariate}), and the unconditional VaR / ES envelope (Section~\ref{sec:utility}). \emph{Pipeline~B} reuses the per-asset Pipeline-A CHMM fits as \emph{marginals} and injects cross-asset \emph{dependence} through the rank-based Student-t copula of Section~\ref{sec:cross_asset_methods}, evaluated in Section~\ref{sec:cross_asset}.

\subsection{Continuous Hidden Markov Model}
\label{sec:chmm_def}

We model the continuous excess growth rate $G_t$ as a continuous HMM
$\mathcal{M} = (\mathcal{S}, \mathbf{T}, \mathbf{B}, \boldsymbol{\pi})$
with $K$ latent states, a per-state emission density $b_k(x; \boldsymbol\theta_k)$,
transition matrix $\mathbf{T} \in \mathbb{R}^{K \times K}$, and initial
distribution $\boldsymbol{\pi}$ \cite{rabiner1986introduction}.
The stationary marginal is the $K$-component mixture
\begin{equation}
    f(x) = \sum_{k=1}^{K} \bar{\pi}_k\, b_k(x; \boldsymbol\theta_k),
    \qquad \bar{\boldsymbol{\pi}} = \bar{\boldsymbol{\pi}} \mathbf{T},
    \label{eq:mixture}
\end{equation}
and supplies heavy tails either through the spread of component moments
(Gaussian components) or through the emission family itself (Student-t or Laplace).
Existence and uniqueness of $\bar{\boldsymbol{\pi}}$ require irreducibility and aperiodicity of $\mathbf T$, recorded as Assumption~\ref{ass:irred} in Section~\ref{sec:theory}; the spectral mechanism behind the absolute-return ACF that this mixture induces is derived in Section~\ref{sec:theory}.
We learn $\mathbf{T}$, $\boldsymbol\theta_{1:K}$, and $\boldsymbol{\pi}$ jointly from continuous observations via EM (Section~\ref{sec:estimation}).

\paragraph{CHMM-N: Gaussian emissions.}
$b_k(x) = \mathcal{N}(x; \mu_k, \sigma_k^2)$ with per-state location $\mu_k$ and variance $\sigma_k^2$.

\paragraph{CHMM-t: Student-t emissions.}
$b_k(x) = t_{\nu_k}(x; \mu_k, \sigma_k)$ with per-state location $\mu_k$, scale $\sigma_k$, and degrees of freedom $\nu_k \in [\nu_{\min}, \nu_{\max}]$.
Per-state degrees of freedom let heavy-tailed regimes coexist with near-Gaussian regimes in the same model.

\paragraph{CHMM-L: Laplace emissions.}
$b_k(x) = \mathrm{Laplace}(x; \mu_k, b_k)$ with per-state location $\mu_k$ and scale $b_k$. The heavier-than-Gaussian Laplace tail (per-component excess kurtosis $3$) provides an alternative route to tail fidelity that carries no shape parameter and is strictly cheaper than CHMM-t.

\paragraph{CHMM-GED: Generalized Error Distribution emissions.}
$b_k(x) = \mathrm{GED}(x; \mu_k, \alpha_k, p_k)$ with per-state location $\mu_k$, scale $\alpha_k$, and shape $p_k \in [p_{\min}, p_{\max}]$. The GED nests Gaussian at $p = 2$ and Laplace at $p = 1$, so per-state $p_k$ is the structural analog of CHMM-t's per-state $\nu_k$ on the Gaussian-to-Laplace shape axis: each state picks its own kurtosis adaptively, by ECM, on a continuous shape parameter. CHMM-GED therefore strictly nests CHMM-N (all $p_k = 2$) and CHMM-L (all $p_k = 1$) as boundary cases.

\subsection{Benchmark Generators}
\label{sec:benchmarks}

We benchmark the CHMM against representatives from each of the standard generator tiers, all fitted on the same IS window.
The \textit{i.i.d.\ bootstrap} \cite{politis1994stationary} samples $T$ returns uniformly with replacement, preserving the empirical marginal exactly and serving as the non-parametric distributional ceiling.
The \textit{Gaussian} i.i.d.\ generator draws $G_t \sim \mathcal{N}(\hat\mu, \hat\sigma^2)$ from the sample moments; the \textit{Laplace} i.i.d.\ generator draws $G_t \sim \text{Laplace}(\hat m, \hat b)$ from the sample median and mean absolute deviation.
\textit{GARCH(1,1)} \cite{bollerslev1986generalized} uses $G_t = \mu + \sigma_t z_t$, $\sigma_t^2 = \omega + \alpha(G_{t-1} - \mu)^2 + \beta \sigma_{t-1}^2$ with $z_t \overset{\text{i.i.d.}}{\sim} \mathcal{N}(0, 1)$, fitted by Nelder--Mead maximum likelihood; this is the parametric volatility-clustering workhorse.
\textit{GARCH(1,1)-$t$} \cite{bollerslev1987conditionally} replaces the Gaussian innovation with a Student-$t$, supplying a heavy-tailed volatility-clustering competitor with the same recursion.
\textit{MS-GARCH} (Markov-switching GARCH, \citealp{haas2004new}) is the regime-switching foil: a discrete two-state hidden chain modulates the GARCH parameters, providing the closest peer to the CHMM scaffold among classical baselines. The full extended GARCH-family panel (EGARCH, GJR-GARCH, HAR-RV, MS-GARCH $K=3$), the explicit semi-Markov CHMM foil, and a deep-generative QuantGAN \cite{wiese2020quantgan} row are reported in Appendix~\ref{sec:extended_baselines} (the QuantGAN architecture and training details in Appendix~\ref{sec:quantgan_supp}).

\subsection{Cross-Asset Dependence (Pipeline~B)}
\label{sec:cross_asset_methods}

The univariate analysis fits a separate CHMM to each ticker independently (Pipeline~A).
For multi-asset synthesis we extend Pipeline~A through the rank-based Student-t copula \cite{demarta2005tcopula, mcneil2015quantitative}, which preserves each asset's fitted CHMM marginal exactly while injecting cross-sectional tail-coupled dependence.
Of the three constructions evaluated in our larger panel (Single Index Model (SIM) \cite{sharpe1963simplified}, Gaussian copula, Student-t copula), the Student-t copula attains the strongest IS off-diagonal correlation MAE ($0.027$ vs $0.030$ Gaussian, $0.076$ SIM); on the OoS window the Gaussian and Student-t copulas are statistically indistinguishable on this metric ($0.202$ vs $0.209$), with both materially better than the SIM ($0.252$). We report the Student-t copula as Pipeline~B for its standard tail-dependence interpretation and the IS-fit advantage; the SIM and Gaussian copula constructions are reported in Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_cross_asset}.
By Sklar's theorem \cite{sklar1959fonctions, nelsen2006introduction}, any joint cumulative distribution function (CDF) with continuous marginals factors uniquely into a copula and the marginals; this lets us fix each marginal at its per-asset CHMM and estimate only the copula.
We use the nonparametric probability integral transform $u_{j,t} = \mathrm{rank}(g_{j,t}) / (T+1)$, estimate the copula correlation matrix $\Sigma$ by the analytic Kendall's-$\tau$ inversion $\rho_{ij} = \sin(\pi \tau_{ij}/2)$ \cite{embrechts2002correlation, mcneil2015quantitative}, and select the degrees-of-freedom parameter $\nu$ by profile maximum likelihood over $\nu \in \{2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 10, 15, 20, 30\}$.
Cross-asset simulation follows the rank-reordering construction of \citet{iman1982distribution}: (i) draw $T$ independent paths from each per-asset CHMM, (ii) sample $T$ copula variates, and (iii) reorder each column of the CHMM matrix to match the ranks of the copula sample.
Reordering only permutes simulated values, so every per-asset marginal is preserved \emph{exactly} while cross-asset dependence is injected through rank coupling; this is a standard property of the rank-reordering construction.

\subsection{Evaluation Protocol}
\label{sec:metrics}

For each model we simulate $P = 1{,}000$ independent paths of length $T$ and evaluate four core metrics, following the framework of \cite{stenger2024thinking}.
All experiments use a deterministic global random seed ($20260420$); per-experiment sub-seeds are derived additively so that each block is independently reproducible from a single seed root.
The two-sample Kolmogorov--Smirnov \cite{kolmogorov1933, smirnov1948} pass rate at $\alpha = 0.05$ over the $1{,}000$ paths scores distributional fidelity; the mean simulated excess kurtosis scores tail behaviour; and the mean absolute error of the absolute-return ACF over $L = 252$ lags,
\begin{equation}
    \text{ACF-MAE} = \frac{1}{L}\sum_{\tau=1}^{L} \left| \hat\rho^{\text{obs}}_{|G|}(\tau) - \bar{\hat\rho}^{\,\text{sim}}_{|G|}(\tau) \right|,
    \label{eq:acf_mae}
\end{equation}
is the direct diagnostic for the Ryd\'{e}n et al.\ limitation.
A separate Value-at-Risk panel (Section~\ref{sec:var_backtest}) carries the unconditional-Kupiec \cite{kupiec1995techniques} and Expected-Shortfall envelope diagnostics.
