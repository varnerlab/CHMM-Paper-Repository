A continuous HMM trained by Baum-Welch with quantile-based initialization reproduces the three symmetric Cont stylized facts on daily US-equity returns. The textbook mixture-of-eigenvalues identity for the absolute-return ACF (\citealp{hamilton1994time, krolzig1997markov, timmermann2000moments}) recasts the empirical \citet{ryden1998stylized} low-$K$ failure as an effective-rank statement on $\mathbf T - \mathbf 1\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$, non-binding at $K \ge 3$ on the SPY 2014--2024 instance. Four emission families share one ECM scaffold and land at four kurtosis points; a regime-conditional Value-at-Risk that propagates the one-step-ahead state forecast through the predictive mixture passes Christoffersen-cc cleanly across families on the headline OoS window and on $19/24$ rolling-origin walk-forward rows. ML HSMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ ($91.0\%$ OoS KS) is the strongest single-window row in the panel; CHMM and ML HSMM are best read as complementary scaffolds with different KS / ACF / kurtosis trade-offs.

The headline scope is daily US equities under stationary OoS. \textbf{The static IS-fitted CHMM does not transfer outside this asset class}: the GLD / SLV non-equity stress test of Appendix~\ref{sec:non_us_asset_supp} collapses to $0\%$ OoS KS. Walk-forward stress folds (W2 COVID, W4 2022 rate-hike) reject regardless of refit cadence; periodic refit is the deployment recipe for non-stress slices. Skew-heavy-tailed emissions, explicit-duration semi-Markov sojourns at higher $K$, and scaling to larger $d$ via untruncated regular vines or factor copulas are companion-paper directions. The spectral-rank diagnostic generalises beyond Rydén: any regime-switching ``failure'' attributing the limit to temporal structure can be re-litigated as a marginal-mixture vs.\ effective-rank decomposition on the fitted $\hat{\mathbf T}$.
