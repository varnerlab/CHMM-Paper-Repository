A continuous HMM trained by Baum-Welch with quantile-based initialization reproduces the three symmetric Cont stylized facts (heavy-tailed marginals, negligible linear ACF, slow $|G_t|$ ACF) on daily US-equity returns. At the state-resolution-robust body headline $K^\star = 3$ (selected by held-out per-observation log-likelihood and held-out KS on the strictly pre-2020 slice under both single-fold and four-fold / six-fold rolling-origin CV; Appendix~\ref{sec:k_selection_kfold_pre2020}) the four emission variants attain $79.8$--$90.6\%$ IS / $63.1$--$83.2\%$ OoS KS on the single SPY OoS window, with CHMM-N at $89.7\%$ IS / $80.5\%$ OoS KS, simulated kurtosis $3.83 / 3.53$, and $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $0.0460$; the penalised CHMM-t at $\lambda = 20$ provides the cleanest IS / OoS heavy-tail match in the headline block at $14.91 / 8.50$ against observed $7.68 / 5.29$. A $K^\star = 6$ block is reported as a sensitivity reference (single-fold pre-2020 selection; the four-fold and six-fold $k$-fold CV cannot distinguish $K^\star = 6$ from $K^\star = 3$ at conventional levels). The single-window OoS sits at the upper end of the six-fold rolling-origin walk-forward whose median CHMM-N OoS KS is $67.7\%$ at $K = 18$ and $62.1\%$ at $K^\star = 3$; the walk-forward median is the operationally informative summary for production deployment. An extended-state-resolution \emph{sensitivity reference} at $K = 18$, selected by a multi-objective rule that uses the OoS window, attains $93.6$--$95.6\%$ IS / $80.8$--$85.7\%$ OoS KS, with the penalised CHMM-t at $\lambda = 20$ providing the cleanest moderate-state-resolution IS / OoS kurtosis match at $8.56$ / $7.07$ against observed $7.68$ / $5.29$ (Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison}). The strongest single-window OoS KS row in the panel is ML HSMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ ($91.0\%$ OoS KS) but its $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE matches the i.i.d.\ baseline; CHMM and ML HSMM are best read as complementary scaffolds with different KS / ACF / kurtosis trade-offs. We apply the textbook closed-form mixture-of-eigenvalues identity for the absolute-return ACF (\citealp{hamilton1994time, krolzig1997markov, timmermann2000moments}) to the SPY 2014--2024 instance of the Ryd\'{e}n et al.\ low-$K$ setup and recast the empirical failure there as an effective-rank statement on $\mathbf T - \mathbf 1 \bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$, showing the rank constraint is non-binding at $K \ge 3$ on this instance; the substantive content is the empirical effective-rank application, not a re-derivation of the identity. The four emission variants share the same scaffold and land at four kurtosis points; CHMM-GED's per-state shape $p_k$ nests Gaussian and Laplace as boundary cases and produces a bulk/tail partition that replicates across seeds and tickers. VaR and ES envelopes bracket observed values at $1\%$ and $5\%$; a regime-conditional VaR that propagates the one-step-ahead state forecast through the CHMM mixture passes Kupiec, Christoffersen-ind, and Christoffersen-cc cleanly at $\alpha = 0.05$ on OoS (Table~\ref{tab:cond_var}; the $\alpha = 0.01$ rows are power-bounded at $T_{\text{OoS}} = 572$ and should be read with the calibration of Appendix~\ref{sec:christoffersen_power}). The conditional construction extends to a six-fold rolling-origin walk-forward with a $19/24$ aggregate pass-rate at the $5\%$ level (Table~\ref{tab:walkforward_cond_var}); the two failures concentrate on the COVID and 2022-rate-hike-onset stress folds that the univariate walk-forward already flags as out-of-distribution by KS.
Cross-ticker generalisation is exercised on a sector-balanced 30-ticker panel (Section~\ref{sec:cross_asset_univariate}, Table~\ref{tab:cross_ticker}); the body table reports the headline $K^\star = 3$ (OoS KS median $69.1\%$), the sensitivity reference $K^\star = 6$ (OoS KS median $75.1\%$), and the kurtosis-fidelity sensitivity $K = 18$ (OoS KS median $73.4\%$), all at identical $11/30$ failure count, with quarterly refit at $K = 18$ lifting the OoS median to $83.0\%$ and the largest gains concentrating on the regime-introduction tickers. Multi-asset synthesis composes through a rank-based Student-$t$ copula on the CHMM marginals at $\nu^\star = 6$ (six-asset IS off-diagonal correlation MAE $0.027$); on OoS the Gaussian and Student-$t$ copulas are statistically indistinguishable at $N_{\text{paths}} = 200$, so the dependence-family selection is an IS-calibration distinction. The headline scope is daily US equities; non-equity asset classes are not covered (the GLD / SLV stress test of Appendix~\ref{sec:non_us_asset_supp} collapses to $0\%$ OoS KS).

Skew-heavy-tailed emissions (the natural fix for the absent leverage-effect coverage in the present scope), explicit-duration semi-Markov sojourns at higher $K$, and a reference-implementation re-run of the original Wiese et al.\ (2020) QuantGAN architecture with Lambert-W input pre-processing are companion-paper directions; scaling to larger $d$ via untruncated regular vines or factor copulas is the natural multi-asset follow-up.
