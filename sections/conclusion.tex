A continuous-emission HMM trained by Baum-Welch with quantile-based initialisation matches the three symmetric Cont stylized-fact diagnostics on daily US-equity returns within stated, horizon-banded tolerances: it fits the heavy-tailed marginal, leaves the raw growth rate serially uncorrelated, and reduces the lag-252 absolute growth-rate ACF error relative to i.i.d.\ baselines at short and medium horizons, while its finite geometric memory leaves the far band (lags 64--252) at the i.i.d.\ floor. The standard mixture-of-eigenvalues identity for the absolute growth-rate ACF~\cite{hamilton1994time, krolzig1997markov, timmermann2000moments} expresses the \citet{ryden1998stylized} low-$K$ limitation as a rank condition on $\mathbf T - \mathbf 1\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$; that condition was not empirically active on the SPY fits at $K \in \{3, 18\}$ (a single dominant component carried the fitted ACF budget), and a cross-ticker diagnostic at $K = 18$ (conjugate pairs grouped into real components) placed the median dominant-component share at $0.785$; because that diagnostic was computed at $K = 18$ only, the low-$K$ statement is established on SPY rather than as a cross-ticker property, while marginal flexibility explained more of the remaining fit gap under the diagnostics used here. This result does not remove the finite-state restriction to finitely many geometric ACF modes. The unified EM framework shares the forward-backward recursion and transition update across variants and localises their differences to the emission density and its M-step update: Gaussian and Laplace use closed-form updates, while Student-$t$ and GED use ECM-style numerical block updates. The shared-$\nu$ Student-$t$ variant narrowed the excess kurtosis gap at $K^\star = 3$ to $4.68$ against observed $7.68$ without a tuning hyperparameter (Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison}); CHMM-L sat between the Gaussian and the penalised Student-$t$ rows without any shape parameter; and CHMM-GED's bimodal $\hat p_k$ partition recovered the Gaussian-bulk / Laplace-tail split directly from the data. The regime-conditional Value-at-Risk, built by averaging the family-appropriate predictive density over the one-step-ahead state forecast, was not rejected by the Christoffersen joint conditional-coverage test at $\alpha = 0.05$ on the main OoS window or on $19/24$ walk-forward rows (Tables~\ref{tab:cond_var_all_families} and~\ref{tab:walkforward_cond_var}). CHMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ was the only row of the full conditional-VaR panel (CHMM, filtered bootstrap, CAViaR, and the origin-aligned MS-GARCH block) not rejected by the asymptotic strict-tail Engle-Manganelli test at $\alpha = 0.01$ ($p = 0.06$; Table~\ref{tab:cond_var}), but a parametric-bootstrap calibration shows the asymptotic $\chi^2_6$ reference to be anticonservative at $T = 573$ (neither CHMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ nor MS-GARCH at $K = 4$ is rejected under finite-sample nulls), and a paired quantile-loss comparison finds no significant ranking among the panel's models at either level, so the strict-tail tier is exploratory and does not single out the CHMM among regime-switching risk models; the primary coverage evidence is the $5\%$ tier, where every panel model passes. Rejections concentrated on stress folds where the regime moved outside the training distribution: every tested walk-forward configuration (CHMM-N at $K \in \{3, 18\}$) was rejected on the W2 COVID fold, and W4 added a single tail-tier rejection at $K = 18$. The main result is bounded in scope. The i.i.d.\ bootstrap and a maximum-likelihood HSMM-N benchmark beat the CHMM on raw single-window OoS Kolmogorov-Smirnov, a marginal-axis result only (both rows sit at the i.i.d.\ $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE floor near $0.063$, against $0.0462$ for CHMM-N), and under the like-for-like per-path estimand the regime-switching MS-GARCH at $K = 3$ beats the CHMM on the volatility-clustering axis itself ($0.0408$ against $0.0462$; the one-regime GARCH rows sit slightly above CHMM-N at $0.0474$--$0.0490$) while losing badly on the marginal axis, while the CHMM directly supports the regime-conditional VaR and the cross-sectional multi-asset copula composition evaluated here (the composition preserves marginals and contemporaneous dependence, not per-asset temporal dynamics). No formal privacy guarantee is claimed for the synthetic output. The dataset scope is daily US equities under stationary OoS (Table~\ref{tab:stationarity_scope}); the GLD non-equity stress and the W2 and W4 walk-forward folds collapsed under static fitting, and we recommend periodic refit for production use.

The primary extension points outside the model class: the mixture-of-eigenvalues identity caps the absolute growth-rate ACF at finitely many geometric decay modes, and escaping that ceiling requires a different duration law rather than a larger state count --- a dedicated maximum-likelihood explicit-duration HSMM with heavy-tailed sojourns~\cite{yu2010hidden, bulla2006stylized} or a long-memory latent-volatility formulation~\cite{nystrup2017long}, directions our Gamma-sojourn sensitivity results at $K = 18$ (Table~\ref{tab:hsmm_gamma}) already indicate. Within the framework, the natural next steps are skewed emission families (targeting the leverage effect), covariate-dependent transitions or online change-point detection for the stress folds the static fit leaves open (untested here, not demonstrated remedies), factor or vine dependence structures at larger cross-sections, an innovation-copula construction that would preserve per-asset serial dependence in the composition, and an end-to-end portfolio-level test of the regime-conditional VaR. Each targets one stated limitation of the present scope; the sojourn-law extension is the one that leaves the finite geometric-memory ceiling behind.
