A continuous hidden Markov model trained by Baum-Welch with quantile-based initialization reproduces the three Cont stylized facts of daily equity returns simultaneously on SPY at two operating points: the held-out-likelihood and BIC choice $K^\star = 3$ for risk-management consumers (CHMM-N: $89.7\%$ IS KS, $80.1\%$ OoS KS, $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $0.0467$) and a tail-fidelity reference panel at $K = 18$ for synthetic-data consumers ($93.6$--$95.6\%$ IS KS and $80.8$--$85.7\%$ OoS KS across the four emission variants, Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison}). The closed-form bilinear identity behind the absolute-return ACF is folklore in the regime-switching literature (\citealp{hamilton1994time, krolzig1997markov, timmermann2000moments}); we apply it to recast the Ryd\'{e}n et al.\ low-$K$ failure as a rank statement on the dominant-eigenmode-deflated transition matrix $\mathbf T - \mathbf 1\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$ and demonstrate empirically that the rank constraint is binding only at $K = 2$--$3$ Gaussian-emission setups. Four emission variants share the same scaffold and land at four distinct points on the kurtosis axis: the Gaussian CHMM-N undershoots, the Student-t CHMM-t overshoots and is the per-state heavy-tailed choice for tail-conditional consumers (recommended with $1/\nu_k$ shrinkage at $\lambda = 20$), the Laplace CHMM-L matches observed kurtosis cleanly without a shape parameter, and the Generalized Error Distribution CHMM-GED carries a per-state shape $p_k$ that nests Gaussian ($p_k = 2$) and Laplace ($p_k = 1$) as boundary cases and lets each state pick its own kurtosis adaptively, on SPY producing a structural bulk/tail partition that replicates across seeds and tickers. The M-step swap is the only architectural difference across the four.

The headline quantitative findings on SPY are an absolute-return ACF mean absolute error within $0.003$ of GARCH(1,1) for CHMM-N at $K = 18$, a clean Kupiec pass on Value-at-Risk at both $1\%$ and $5\%$, and Value-at-Risk and Expected-Shortfall envelopes that bracket the observed historical values on both windows. Under the unconditional pooled-archive Christoffersen test every unconditional generator in the panel (CHMM, GARCH, bootstrap) rejects independence on the OoS window, structurally driven by volatility-clustering in $R_{\text{oos}}$ that any constant-across-$t$ VaR threshold cannot track. The regime-conditional variant that propagates the one-step-ahead latent-state forecast $\Prob(s_{t+1} \mid \mathcal F_t)$ through the CHMM emission mixture (Section~\ref{sec:var_backtest}, Table~\ref{tab:cond_var}) addresses this directly and passes Kupiec, Christoffersen-ind, and Christoffersen-cc cleanly at $\alpha \in \{0.01, 0.05\}$ for both $K^\star = 3$ and $K = 18$ on OoS, reusing the IS-fixed CHMM-N transitions and emissions. Substantial OoS degradation on NVDA and JPM reflects the well-known stationarity-scope limit on individual financial time series and motivates periodic refit in operational deployment.

Multi-asset synthesis composes cleanly with rank-based copula constructions on top of the CHMM-N marginals: the Student-t copula at the profile-MLE selection $\nu^\star = 6$ attains an IS off-diagonal correlation MAE of $0.027$, against $0.030$ for the Gaussian copula and $0.076$ for the Single Index Model factor baseline on the same marginals; on the OoS window the Student-t and Gaussian copulas are statistically indistinguishable ($0.209$ vs $0.202$), and a quarterly rolling-window refit reduces the OoS off-diagonal MAE further to $0.185$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:rolling_copula_supp}). The dominant cross-asset advantage of the copula constructions over SIM is the rank-reordering preservation of marginals; the Student-t-vs-Gaussian advantage is an IS-only property that does not survive the OoS regime shift in the JNJ-equity-factor relationship.

Skew-heavy-tailed emissions and explicit-duration semi-Markov sojourns are companion-paper directions; a truncated C-vine on the same six-asset universe does not improve over the elliptical copulas (Appendix~\ref{sec:cvine_supp}), so the natural multi-asset follow-up is scaling to larger $d$ via untruncated regular vines or factor copulas.
