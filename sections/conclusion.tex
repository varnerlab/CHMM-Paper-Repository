A continuous HMM trained by Baum-Welch with quantile-based initialization reproduces the three Cont stylized facts simultaneously on SPY at two held-out-clean operating points and an extended-state-resolution reference: the held-out-likelihood and BIC choice $K^\star = 3$ for risk-management consumers (CHMM-N: $89.7\%$ IS / $80.1\%$ OoS KS, $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $0.0467$); the held-out-likelihood / held-out-KS reference $K^\star = 6$ on the strictly pre-2020 slice for synthetic-data consumers (four-variant block at $88.3$--$92.6\%$ IS, $76.3$--$79.0\%$ OoS KS; CHMM-N kurtosis $5.26$ matches observed $5.29$ OoS within $0.03$); and an extended-state-resolution reference panel at $K = 18$ at the cost of relaxing the held-out criterion onto a multi-objective rule, with the four emission variants attaining $93.6$--$95.6\%$ IS, $80.8$--$85.7\%$ OoS KS and the penalised CHMM-t at $\lambda = 20$ providing the cleanest IS / OoS kurtosis match at $8.56$ / $7.07$ against observed $7.68$ / $5.29$ (Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison}). The mechanism is the closed-form mixture-of-eigenvalues identity for the absolute-return ACF (\citealp{hamilton1994time, krolzig1997markov, timmermann2000moments}); we apply it to recast the Ryd\'{e}n et al.\ low-$K$ failure as a rank statement on $\mathbf T - \mathbf 1 \bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$ and show the rank binds only at $K \le 3$ Gaussian setups. The four emission variants share the same scaffold and land at four kurtosis points; CHMM-GED's per-state shape $p_k$ nests Gaussian and Laplace as boundary cases and produces a bulk/tail partition that replicates across seeds and tickers. VaR and ES envelopes bracket observed values at $1\%$ and $5\%$; a regime-conditional VaR that propagates the one-step-ahead state forecast through the CHMM mixture passes Kupiec, Christoffersen-ind, and Christoffersen-cc cleanly at every $(K, \alpha)$ on OoS (Table~\ref{tab:cond_var}), and the conditional construction extends to a six-fold rolling-origin walk-forward with a $19/24$ aggregate pass-rate at the $5\%$ level (Table~\ref{tab:walkforward_cond_var}); the two failures concentrate on the COVID and 2022-rate-hike-onset stress folds that the univariate walk-forward already flags as out-of-distribution by KS, where every constant-across-$t$ generator rejects breach independence in any case.
Cross-ticker generalisation is exercised on a sector-balanced 30-ticker panel (10 GICS sectors $\times$ 3 large-cap representatives; Section~\ref{sec:cross_asset_univariate}, Table~\ref{tab:cross_ticker}): IS KS distribution is tight (median $99.5\%$), and OoS KS distribution is sector-stratified with $11/30 = 36.7\%$ of tickers below $60\%$, concentrated in single-name regime introductions (Health Care: LLY weight-loss-drug, UNH 2024 policy compression) and the rate-cycle / streaming-platform patterns previously identified on the original five-named-ticker spot-check. Multi-asset synthesis composes through a rank-based Student-$t$ copula on the CHMM marginals at $\nu^\star = 6$ (six-asset IS off-diagonal correlation MAE $0.027$); on OoS the Gaussian and Student-$t$ copulas are statistically indistinguishable, with a quarterly rolling refit reducing the off-diagonal MAE to $0.185$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:rolling_copula_supp}).

Skew-heavy-tailed emissions and explicit-duration semi-Markov sojourns are companion-paper directions; scaling to larger $d$ via untruncated regular vines or factor copulas is the natural multi-asset follow-up.
