### Questions for Authors
1. **The ML HSMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ is reported with simulated kurtosis $3.46$ IS / $3.38$ OoS** (Table~1), against observed $7.68$ IS / $5.29$ OoS. This is below CHMM-N at the same $K^\star = 3$ ($3.86$ IS) and well below the four-emission CHMM panel at $K = 18$ ($5.0$--$14.4$). Why does the explicit-duration HSMM, which adds a per-state Pareto sojourn distribution, *reduce* the simulated kurtosis below the geometric-sojourn CHMM at the same $K$? The natural intuition is the opposite (heavier tails when long sojourns are correctly modelled). Is this an artefact of the Gaussian emission, the Pareto sojourn truncation at $D_{\max} = 200$, or the EM landscape at $K = 3$ on $T_{\text{IS}} = 2516$? A diagnostic sentence would help.
2. **The walk-forward stress folds (W2 COVID at KS $7$--$8\%$; W4 2022 rate-hike start at KS $0$--$1\%$) drop sharply,** but the headline OoS window 2024-01 to 2026-04 (which covers the AI-rally and the rate-cycle reversal) attains $80$--$86\%$ KS across the four CHMM variants. What distinguishes the headline OoS window from W4? Is the stationarity scope of the IS-fitted CHMM somehow more compatible with the 2024--2026 regime than with the 2022 rate-hike onset? A short paragraph reading the difference would help readers calibrate the operational expectation.
3. **The conditional-VaR construction in body Section~4.6 reuses IS-fixed parameters and runs the forward filter on the OoS series under frozen parameters.** The two stress folds in the walk-forward panel (W2, W4) drop to KS $\le 8\%$ on distributional fidelity. A walk-forward extension of Table~4 (one row per fold for $K = 3$ and $K = 18$ at $\alpha \in \{0.01, 0.05\}$) would tell us whether the regime-conditional pass on the headline OoS window is window-specific.
4. **The pre-2020 held-out re-selection in Appendix C.6 selects $K^\star = 6$ on a strictly pre-COVID slice** by both held-out log-likelihood and held-out KS. Have you considered making $K = 6$ the synthetic-data-consumer reference operating point, replacing $K = 18$? The IS Wilks CI is tight; the rolling sequence is wide. Is the apparent wandering a small-sample property of the rolling profile-LL on quarterly windows ($T \approx 63$ trading days per refit), or a non-stationary signal in the tail-coupling? A formal test (e.g., Quintos-Fan-Phillips structural-break statistic on the rolling $\hat\nu$ series) would let the reader interpret the rolling sequence as either stationary noise or a genuine non-stationarity.
