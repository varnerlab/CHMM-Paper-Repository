# Simulated Peer Review — A Continuous Hidden Markov Model for Daily US-Equity Symmetric Stylized-Fact Reproduction

Three simulated reviews are provided below. Reviewer expertise has been adapted to the manuscript's actual domain (financial econometrics, regime-switching models, GARCH/HSMM, VaR backtesting, copula methods, EM/ECM estimation) rather than the skill template's stale "metabolic engineering" placeholder.

Target venue tier: mid-to-high (e.g. *Journal of Financial Econometrics*, *Quantitative Finance*, *Journal of Empirical Finance*, *International Journal of Forecasting*).

## Reviewer 1 — Moderate (Senior Financial Econometrician)

### Summary
The authors propose a continuous HMM with four emission families (Gaussian, Student-$t$, Laplace, GED) trained by Baum–Welch / ECM as a synthetic-data generator for daily US-equity returns, evaluated on SPY 2014–2026 plus a 30-ticker panel, a CRSP cross-decade slice, and a six-asset US-equity copula extension. They reproduce the three symmetric Cont (2001) stylized facts and pass Christoffersen-cc on a regime-conditional VaR at $\alpha=0.05$. Overall I think the paper is sound and useful for the synthetic-data-generator audience; the framing has been notably honest about scope limits (block-bootstrap KS recalibration, $K_\text{eff}$ vs $K_\text{nominal}$, MS-GARCH conditional VaR counterfactual, GLD/SLV non-equity collapse), and I would lean toward acceptance after minor-to-moderate revision.

### Strengths
1. **Unified scaffold across four emission families.** The Methods (`sections/model.tex`, `sections/estimation.tex`) cleanly factor the ECM into a shared forward-backward / quantile-init front end with family-specific M-steps. CHMM-GED nesting CHMM-N at $p=2$ and CHMM-L at $p=1$ is a nice architectural touch, and the bimodal $\hat p_k$ partition (Appendix Sec.~\ref{sec:supp_p_partition}, referenced from Discussion) is an interpretable empirical finding.
2. **Honesty about scope and the bootstrap/HSMM ceilings.** The "Bootstrap as a non-parametric synthetic-data benchmark" paragraph in `sections/results.tex` (lines around 110–111) is unusually candid — many CHMM-style papers would have buried the fact that the iid stationary bootstrap and ML HSMM-N at $K^\star=3$ both beat every CHMM operating point on raw 1-day OoS KS. Pivoting to the structural use cases (regime-conditional VaR, copula composition, parametric privacy) is the right move and is well argued.
3. **Pre-registered $K$-selection with a multi-fold sensitivity.** Table~\ref{tab:k_selection} plus the four-fold/six-fold rolling-origin CV on the strictly pre-2020 slice (Appendix~\ref{sec:k_selection_kfold_pre2020}) is the right protocol; the state-resolution-robust headline at $K^\star=3$ is defensible.
4. **Conditional-coverage VaR pipeline is well-constructed.** The Eq.~\ref{eq:filter} state-filter through the predictive mixture is the natural use of the latent-state machinery, and reporting both Christoffersen-cc and Engle–Manganelli DQ alongside a Monte Carlo power calibration (Appendix~\ref{sec:christoffersen_power}) is more than I usually see in this literature.
5. **Reproducibility and seed policy.** Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_chmm_api} plus the deterministic global seed root and per-experiment sub-seed rule is a tangible commitment to reproducibility that I appreciate.

### Weaknesses
1. **The "headline" framing is still doing a lot of work in the abstract.** The abstract leads with $K^\star = 3$ at $89.7\%$ IS / $80.5\%$ OoS KS, then qualifies in three subsequent sentences (block-bootstrap drops $\sim 25$pp, walk-forward median is $62.1\%$ at $K^\star = 3$, ML HSMM dominates raw KS). A reader scanning only the first sentence would still come away with an inflated impression. **Suggested fix:** demote the single-window pair from the lead sentence and lead with the walk-forward median or the non-parametric-bootstrap-dominates-on-raw-KS finding plus the structural-use-case differentiation.
2. **Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison} is dense and the reading guide is buried in a methods note.** The four operating-point blocks (benchmark / $K^\star=3$ / $K^\star=6$ / HSMM / $K=18$) plus six footnote markers ($\dagger$, $\dagger\dagger$, $\P$, $\S$, $\S\S$, $\P\P$) make this table hard to read on first pass. **Suggested fix:** split into a body table that contains only the six benchmark generators plus the $K^\star = 3$ block, and move the $K^\star = 6$ and $K = 18$ sensitivity blocks to an appendix table called out from the body.
3. **The unpenalised CHMM-t IS kurtosis of $14.35$ vs observed $7.68$ is repeatedly attributed to "lower-bracket pinning at $\nu_\text{min} = 2.1$" but the bracket-lift ablation (Remark~\ref{rem:bracket_lift}) only brings it down to $13.25$.** That residual gap is more than half the observed value and is not explained mechanistically beyond "multi-state pinning." **Suggested fix:** either (a) report a figure or table showing the per-state $\hat\nu_k$ histogram across seeds at $K=18$ to demonstrate which states are pinning where, or (b) reframe the per-state $\nu_k$ aggregate-mean overshoot as a known shortcoming of the Peel–Liu construction at high $K$ and recommend the shared-$\nu$ row as the body recipe.
4. **The "leverage-effect" paragraph in Discussion (`sections/discussion.tex` lines around 40–41) is doing two things at once.** It claims (i) the simulated envelope at $K=18$ borderline-non-rejects the IS observed value but (ii) rejects the OoS observed value. The reader is asked to accept both that the Markov mixing produces a non-trivial leverage signal *and* that the model class (symmetric emissions) cannot reproduce it. This is internally consistent but the framing is awkward; the abstract claim that the leverage effect is "out of scope" sits oddly with a half-page of leverage-effect analysis in Discussion. **Suggested fix:** pick a lane — either keep the leverage analysis in Discussion as a "what the symmetric scaffold can and cannot do" statement and remove the abstract's "out of scope" framing, or move the leverage paragraph entirely to a supplementary section and keep the abstract claim.
5. **The kurtosis CI overlap argument is used as a defense but rarely as a constraint.** The bootstrap CI on observed kurtosis (Appendix~\ref{sec:kurtosis_bootstrap_ci}, $[2.17, 12.40]$ IS / $[0.90, 8.26]$ OoS) is invoked to defend point comparisons inside a wide envelope, but every per-row kurtosis claim in Tables~\ref{tab:model_comparison} and \ref{tab:variant_choice} is reported as a point estimate. **Suggested fix:** add a kurtosis CI column to the per-row tables, or at minimum shade rows whose simulated mean falls outside the joint CI envelope.

### Questions for Authors
1. The body uses CHMM-N marginals in the cross-asset Pipeline B (`sections/results.tex` line 177: "Pipeline~B reuses the per-asset CHMM-N marginals"). Why CHMM-N rather than the penalised CHMM-t that you recommend for kurtosis fidelity? Does the dependence-family null result on OoS hold under heavy-tailed marginals?
2. The companion paper `\citet{alswaidan2026hybrid}` (referenced in the Related Work as the discrete-state predecessor) reproduces the same three stylized facts via Laplace quantile binning plus a Poisson-jump duration mechanism. What is the head-to-head OoS KS / kurtosis / $|G_t|$ ACF comparison between the discrete predecessor and the present continuous variant on the SPY headline window? A sentence or two would clarify the contribution.
3. The held-out $K$-selection on the strictly pre-2020 slice ends in 2019-12; does $K^\star$ shift if the validation window is moved to 2020-01 to 2021-06 (which would include the COVID stress fold) and how would that interact with the W2 walk-forward result?
4. The Student-$t$ copula degrees-of-freedom $\nu^\star = 6$ is selected on 6 US-equity assets. Does the same $\nu^\star$ come out of the 30-ticker panel, or does the higher dimension push the profile-MLE toward a different value? This matters for the "dependence-family choice is an IS-only distinction" reading.

### Requested Experiments / Analyses
1. **Heavy-tailed-marginal copula sensitivity.** Re-run Pipeline B with CHMM-t (penalised, $\lambda=20$) marginals on the same six-asset universe and report the IS / OoS off-diagonal correlation MAE. If the dependence-family null on OoS still holds under heavy-tailed marginals, the conclusion is much stronger; if not, the body needs to qualify the marginal-family-independence claim.
2. **Direct head-to-head vs the discrete-state predecessor `\citet{alswaidan2026hybrid}`.** Add a single row to Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison} (or a small sub-table) with the OoS KS, kurtosis, $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE, and Christoffersen-cc $p$-value for the discrete-state predecessor on SPY 2014–2024 / 2024–2026, so that a reader can see whether the continuous extension is a strict improvement or a different operating point. The paper currently claims the continuous variant "recover the same target through the spectral mechanism" but does not show it numerically.
3. **A held-out validation slice that includes COVID.** Add a $K$-selection sweep with validation 2020-01 to 2021-06 to the appendix and report whether the headline $K^\star = 3$ is robust to the stress slice; this would sharpen the walk-forward W2 reading.

### Minor Comments
1. Abstract line 1 reads "stationary-block-bootstrap null at mean block length $L = 20$ the OoS pass rate drops by ${\sim}25$pp"; the bold mid-sentence reads awkwardly. Consider removing the boldface or breaking into two sentences.
2. The phrase "the M-step is the only architectural difference" appears verbatim in the abstract, the introduction, the Methods opening, and the Discussion. Tighten to one canonical statement and reference back.
3. The hyphenation of "stylized-fact" is inconsistent (sometimes "stylized fact", sometimes "stylized-fact"). A consistency pass would help.
4. Table~\ref{tab:cross_ticker} caption ends with a long stylistic remark ("The $10$-sector $\times$ $3$-large-cap sample design is one design choice among many; an equally weighted random draw of 30 large-caps would land on a different OoS tail.") that I think belongs in the running text, not the caption.
5. Figure 3's eight-way subpanel naming convention (`Fig-3-IS-Comparison-K12-a/b/c/d`, `K18`, `K18-GED`, `K18-L`, `K18-t`, `K21`, `K3`, `K6`, `K9`) suggests the figure-set has not been pruned for the body — confirm that only the body-cited subpanels are present in the final PDF.

### Recommendation
**Minor Revision.**

## Reviewer 2 — Hard (Constraint-Based / Time-Series Statistics, MCMC and Frequentist Inference)

### Summary
The paper trains a continuous HMM by EM/ECM on SPY daily returns and offers a thorough empirical-evaluation panel. The mathematical scaffold is sound and the stated contributions (empirical instance of the textbook bilinear-ACF identity, four emission families under one ECM, rolling-origin walk-forward, regime-conditional VaR, copula composition) are individually reasonable. My concerns are with the inference burden carried by point-estimate EM at $T_{\text{IS}} = 2{,}516$, the $K_{\text{eff}}$ vs $K_{\text{nominal}}$ tension, and a handful of statistical-significance claims that I think are overstated relative to the test power available at $T_{\text{OoS}} = 572$. I would recommend Major Revision with most items addressable through additional reporting rather than new modelling.

### Strengths
1. **The bilinear ACF identity is correctly stated and clearly applied.** Theorem~\ref{thm:supp_spectral_acf} (Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_spectral_acf}) is a textbook result in regime-switching, and the authors do not overclaim novelty: the contribution on this axis is explicitly framed as "an empirical effective-rank statement applied to a known instance, not a re-derivation of the identity." This honesty is welcome.
2. **Multi-fold $K$-selection done properly.** The combination of single-fold pre-2020, four-fold and six-fold rolling-origin CV, and the HAC-adjusted Diebold–Mariano-style standard error on the paired log-likelihood difference (Appendix~\ref{sec:k_selection_hac}) is the right protocol for selecting $K$ at this $T$. The HAC $|z|$ flips on the $K=6$ vs $K=3$ comparison are a legitimate reason to land at $K^\star = 3$.
3. **Power calibration on the Christoffersen-cc test.** Reporting Type-I error at $4$–$5\%$ under the iid null and an explicit power profile against breach-clustering eigenvalues (Appendix~\ref{sec:christoffersen_power}, $B = 5{,}000$) is more rigorous than I usually see in the VaR-backtest literature; the conclusion that $\alpha = 0.01$ is power-bounded at $T_{\text{OoS}} = 572$ is correctly drawn.
4. **MS-GARCH conditional-VaR counterfactual.** The MS-GARCH-2/-3/-4 re-run through the same Eq.~\ref{eq:filter} state filter (`sections/var_backtest.tex` paragraph 45) is exactly the right test, and the conclusion that the conditional-coverage value proposition is "not CHMM-specific" is statistically correct and is the kind of self-undermining-but-honest analysis that distinguishes a good paper from a marketing paper.

### Weaknesses
1. **EM gives no uncertainty on the parameter, and the entire $K=18$ vs $K^\star = 3$ debate reduces to point estimates.** At $T_{\text{IS}} = 2{,}516$ on a $K(K-1) + 2K$-parameter model (or $K(K-1) + 3K$ for the per-state $\nu_k$ / $p_k$ variants), $K=18$ is fitting $342$ parameters from $2{,}516$ observations — a parameter-to-sample ratio of $\sim 7.4$. The body acknowledges this through $K_\text{eff} = 11/18$ for CHMM-N and $12/18$ for CHMM-t (Discussion paragraph "Practical identifiability", `sections/discussion.tex` lines around 14), but the reported point estimates of $\hat{\mathbf T}$, $\hat{\boldsymbol\theta}_k$ carry no posterior uncertainty bands and the cross-state distinctness diagnostic is post-hoc rather than a regularising prior. **Suggested fix:** either (a) report a Bayesian posterior fit (Gibbs sampler or HMC over the same model with weakly informative Dirichlet rows on $\mathbf T$, normal-inverse-gamma on $(\mu_k, \sigma_k^2)$, half-Cauchy on the per-state $\nu_k$) on at least the SPY headline window so that the per-state parameter posteriors and the rank of the deflated transition matrix can be assessed under uncertainty, or (b) report bootstrap CIs on $\hat{\boldsymbol\theta}_k$ via a parametric bootstrap of the fitted EM model and acknowledge in the body that the EM point fit at $K=18$ is over-parameterised in a frequentist sense and the headline operating point should be $K_\text{eff} \approx 11$ throughout.
2. **The DM bandwidth-sensitivity panel (`sections/results.tex` paragraph around line 103) is reported only on the OoS sample-CRPS within-CHMM pair tests.** The same DM tests on the multi-day cumulative-return horizons (the body $h = 20$ DM result that "does not replicate at $K^\star = 3$ or across the six-asset universe") are not bandwidth-tested, which is the main place the DM significance claim could be fragile. **Suggested fix:** extend Table~\ref{tab:dm_bandwidth} to cover the multi-day CRPS DM tests at $h \in \{1, 5, 20\}$ across $K \in \{3, 6, 18\}$.
3. **The "block-aware OoS KS recalibration" panel (Table~\ref{tab:ks_block_body}) is reported only at $L = 20$ in the body.** The body claim that "the cross-generator ordering is preserved" but "the absolute level drops by roughly $20$–$30$pp" is supported by one block length. Mean block length $L$ is a tuning parameter and the recalibrated pass rate is sensitive to it; on autocorrelated daily returns the reasonable range is $L \in \{5, 10, 20, 40\}$. **Suggested fix:** include the full $L$-sensitivity column in the body table or add a sentence on the $L$-sensitivity from Appendix~\ref{sec:ks_block_bootstrap}; otherwise the "$\sim 25$pp drop" headline cannot be assessed.
4. **The Wilks $95\%$ profile-LL CI on the copula degrees-of-freedom (`sections/results.tex` line 177, $[6, 7]$ on the unit grid; `Appendix~\ref{sec:copula_halfunit}`'s parametric bootstrap CI confirming $[6, 7]$) is reported as a discrete interval.** Wilks' theorem requires a continuous parameter; the half-unit grid plus parametric bootstrap is a reasonable refinement but the body reading "$\nu^\star = 6$" papers over the actual width. **Suggested fix:** report the bootstrap CI as a continuous interval (e.g. $[5.7, 7.3]$ or whatever the bootstrap empirical quantiles are) and let the reader judge whether the $\hat\nu_\text{full} = 6.40$ from the full one-shot MLE is inside the two-step CI.
5. **The $K_\text{eff} = 11$ rebuild at $K_\text{nominal} = 11$ is reported as confirming the over-parameterisation reading (Discussion paragraph "Practical identifiability"; Appendix~\ref{sec:k_eff_rebuild}, IS KS $94.4\%$, OoS KS $82.8\%$ vs $K=18$ nominal $94.0/81.0\%$).** This is a useful diagnostic, but its presence in the body raises an obvious question: why is the body operating point still $K_\text{nominal} = 18$ rather than $K_\text{nominal} = 11$? The body recipe should be the structurally cleanest one, not the one-by-back-compatibility-with-Peel–Liu one. **Suggested fix:** elevate $K_\text{nominal} = 11$ ($= K_\text{eff}$) to the kurtosis-fidelity sensitivity reference and demote $K = 18$ to a nominal-state-count comparator.
6. **The cross-decade CRSP collapse to $3$–$5\%$ OoS pass rate on 2004–2006 is reported as a stationarity-scope finding (`sections/results.tex` line 172).** The mechanism is correctly identified (low-kurtosis post-dot-com window with observed excess kurtosis $\sim 0.06$ vs IS $3.05$), but the body does not distinguish whether the static IS-fitted CHMM is failing because (a) the OoS marginal is genuinely outside the IS regime library, or (b) the IS-fitted high-volatility states have low stationary mass under the OoS posterior and the simulated paths therefore over-mix high-volatility states. These have different fixes. **Suggested fix:** report the simulated path's stationary distribution $\hat{\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}}$ under the IS-fitted parameters vs the empirical OoS state-occupancy under the same forward filter, and identify which case (a) or (b) the cross-decade collapse falls into.

### Questions for Authors
1. The "shared-$\nu$" CHMM-t row in Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison} (caption note §§) is described as the "structurally cleaner recipe" without the per-state $\nu_k$ aggregate-mean overshoot, and at $K=18$ produces the cleanest IS / OoS heavy-tail match in the entire panel ($6.25/5.00$ vs observed $7.68/5.29$). Why is this row not the body headline for kurtosis fidelity?
2. The Christoffersen-cc walk-forward result (`19/24$ pass at $\alpha = 0.05$) lists W2 (COVID) and W4 (2022 rate-hike) as the rejecting folds. The DQ test at $\alpha = 0.01$ also rejects $K = 18$ on the headline OoS window ($p = 0.017$). Are these failure modes correlated — i.e., do the W2 and W4 rejections also show in DQ at $\alpha = 0.01$, or is DQ@$0.01$ a separate failure mode from the walk-forward stress folds?
3. The state-distinctness single-linkage diagnostic (`Appendix~\ref{sec:state_distinctness}`) collapses CHMM-N to $11/18$ effective states. Does the same diagnostic produce a stable $K_\text{eff}$ across the 30-ticker panel, or is $K_\text{eff}$ ticker-dependent?
4. The reference Bayesian MS-GARCH re-run (Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison}, ¶ rows) reports IS KS $0$–$0.1\%$ under posterior-predictive simulation. The text attributes this to "posterior-predictive integration of parameter uncertainty path-by-path." But MS-GARCH posterior uncertainty should not produce a $\sim 30$pp drop unless the posterior is bimodal or has heavy tails on the variance parameter. Have you checked whether this collapse is an MCMC mixing issue rather than a genuine integrated-uncertainty effect? The standard MSGARCH mcmc.spec at default settings can land in a multimodal posterior; the appendix should report MCMC diagnostics ($\hat R$, ESS) for the reference run.

### Requested Experiments / Analyses
1. **Bayesian / parametric-bootstrap uncertainty on $\hat{\mathbf T}$ at $K = 18$ on SPY IS.** Either via Gibbs/HMC or via a parametric bootstrap of the fitted EM model ($B \ge 500$). Report posterior / bootstrap intervals on $|\lambda_2|$, on the dominant-mode share, and on the $K_\text{eff}$ diagnostic. This addresses the EM-point-estimate burden and would strengthen the "rank-non-binding at $K \ge 3$" claim.
2. **DM-bandwidth panel for the multi-day CRPS tests.** Extend Table~\ref{tab:dm_bandwidth} to the $h \in \{5, 20\}$ DM tests at $K \in \{3, 6, 18\}$; the body $h = 20, K = 18$ result is the one most exposed to bandwidth choice and should be reported under the same NW-HAC bandwidth sensitivity as the within-CHMM pair tests.
3. **MCMC diagnostics on the reference Bayesian MS-GARCH run.** Report $\hat R$, ESS per parameter, and a posterior-predictive-density plot for the IS distribution at $K \in \{2, 3, 4\}$; this would clarify whether the $0$–$0.1\%$ IS KS is genuine posterior-predictive integration or an MCMC mixing artefact.
4. **State-occupancy decomposition of the CRSP cross-decade collapse.** As described under Weakness 6: report $\hat{\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}}$ under IS-fitted parameters vs the empirical OoS state-occupancy under the forward filter on 2004–2006; this would resolve the (a) vs (b) interpretation.

### Minor Comments
1. The deflated transition matrix's numerical rank is reported as $K - 1 = 17$ at tolerance $10^{-10}$ (Table~\ref{tab:t_singular_values}). At condition number $1{,}620$ for CHMM-t, this tolerance is below machine epsilon for the smallest singular value — the rank claim should be reported at multiple tolerances ($10^{-6}$, $10^{-10}$, $10^{-14}$) to demonstrate it is not a tolerance-dependent artefact.
2. The bracket $[\nu_{\min}, \nu_{\max}] = (2.1, 50)$ for the per-state $\nu_k$ ECM is just below the kurtosis-finiteness threshold $\nu = 4$; the bracket-lift remark addresses this. But the *upper* bracket at $\nu_\text{max} = 50$ is also where the CHMM-t shape diagnostic finds $13/18$ states pinning. A sentence on whether the upper bracket is itself a load-bearing choice (i.e., would $\nu_\text{max} = 100$ change anything?) would close this loop.
3. The phrase "EM monotonicity" in Proposition~\ref{prop:ecm_monotone} is correctly stated but the proof sketch elides the standard caveat that ECM monotonicity is in $Q$ and the implication for $\mathcal L$ requires the regularity that the per-state Q is upper-bounded — the compactness assumption supplies this and is correctly stated, but a one-line cite to Meng & Rubin (1993) on the ECM convergence theorem rather than just to Wu (1983) on the original EM result would be more precise.
4. Table~\ref{tab:cond_var} caption note `\textsuperscript{\textdaggerdbl}` says "$\alpha = 0.01$ rows are power-bounded" — but the same `\textdaggerdbl` is also used on a couple of $\alpha = 0.05$ rows where $\text{LR}_{\text{ind}} = 0.01$ (CHMM-N $K=3$, CHMM-t $K=18$). Those are not the same kind of "power-bound" — the marker should be reused only for the $\alpha = 0.01$ rows or relabelled with a separate marker for the borderline-$\text{LR}_{\text{ind}}$ rows.

### Recommendation
**Major Revision** (most items resolvable through additional reporting; the parameter-uncertainty experiment is the only new modelling task).

## Reviewer 3 — Very Hard (Skeptical Author of Competing Methods, Regime-Switching / HSMM)

### Summary
The paper claims to advance the regime-switching synthetic-data-generator literature by replacing the geometric sojourn distribution with continuous heavy-tailed emissions and demonstrating that the bilinear-ACF rank constraint is non-binding at $K \ge 3$. After reading the manuscript carefully I do not think the claimed contribution is well-supported. The core empirical-rank observation is a single-instance statement on SPY (with cross-ticker dominant-mode share dropping to $0.33$ on NEM); the bootstrap dominates raw KS; ML HSMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ dominates raw KS at the regime-switching tier; the conditional-VaR construction is shown by the authors themselves to also work for MS-GARCH; the leverage effect rejects on OoS; and the multi-day CRPS DM "win" at $K = 18$ on SPY does not replicate at the body headline $K^\star = 3$ or across the six-asset universe. What remains is a methodologically careful but architecturally derivative empirical-comparison paper. I believe this should be a Major Revision at minimum and the authors should expect significant reframing.

### Strengths
1. The unified ECM scaffold across four emission families is clean and reproducible (Sec.~\ref{sec:mstep}, Appendix~\ref{sec:supp_chmm_api}).
2. The walk-forward construction with stress-fold attribution (W2 COVID, W4 2022 rate-hike) is the right protocol for a deployment-oriented synthetic-data generator and the authors do not hide the failures.
3. The MS-GARCH conditional-VaR counterfactual (`sections/var_backtest.tex` paragraph 45) is a self-undermining analysis I respect; authors who run controls that weaken their headline are doing the right thing.

### Weaknesses
1. **The "rank-non-binding" empirical claim is a single-instance observation at SPY $K = 18$, and the cross-ticker distribution undermines it.** The introduction (`sections/introduction.tex` line 5) and Theorem~\ref{thm:supp_spectral_acf} together establish that the ACF is a rank-$(K-1)$ sum of geometric decays, but the empirical claim that this bound is "non-binding at $K \ge 3$" rests on (i) SPY $K = 18$ where the dominant non-unit eigenvalue carries $93.6\%$ of the lag-1 ACF (Table~\ref{tab:spectral_modes}), and (ii) a 30-ticker cross-section with median dominant-mode share $0.76$, IQR $[0.66, 0.86]$, **minimum $0.33$ on NEM**. A claim that holds at the median with IQR bottom of $0.66$ and minimum of $0.33$ is not a "rank constraint is non-binding" claim — it is a "rank constraint is loose at the median and binding in the left tail" claim. The body framing (introduction line 5: "the rank-non-binding claim at the cross-ticker median rather than as a universal property"; Sec.~\ref{sec:theory} closing sentence: "the rank-non-binding claim is supported at the cross-ticker median rather than as a universal property of equity-return data") acknowledges this but the abstract still leads with the SPY value as evidence. **Suggested fix:** the abstract should not cite the SPY $93.6\%$ in support of the rank-non-binding framing; the cross-ticker distribution (median, IQR, min) is the substantively load-bearing summary and the abstract should either lead with that distribution or drop the rank-non-binding language.
2. **ML HSMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ dominates every CHMM operating point on raw OoS KS, and the paper explicitly says so.** From `sections/results.tex` paragraph 107: "ML HSMM dominates CHMM on raw OoS distributional fidelity at every operating point tried (Pareto-sojourn HSMM-N at $K^\star = 3$: $91.0\%$ OoS KS; Gamma-sojourn HSMM-N at $K = 18$: panel-best $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $0.0462$)." The body framing is then "CHMM and ML HSMM are complementary scaffolds with different KS / ACF / kurtosis trade-offs" and the CHMM positioning is the structural use cases. But the paper's title is "A Continuous Hidden Markov Model for Daily US-Equity Symmetric Stylized-Fact Reproduction" — and on raw stylized-fact reproduction, HSMM is the better answer at every operating point. **Suggested fix:** either retitle the paper to reflect that the contribution is the structural use cases (e.g., "Continuous HMMs for Regime-Conditional VaR and Copula Composition: A Synthetic-Data Generator for Daily US Equities"), or expand the title's scope to "Continuous HMMs and ML HSMMs as Complementary Synthetic-Data Generators..." and split the empirical headline accordingly. The current title is misaligned with the paper's own internal accounting.
3. **The conditional-VaR pipeline is shown not to be CHMM-specific by the authors themselves.** The MS-GARCH-2/-3/-4 re-run through the same Eq.~\ref{eq:filter} state filter passes Christoffersen-cc and DQ at $\alpha = 0.05$ on all three operating points, and at $\alpha = 0.01$ MS-GARCH-4 passes both tests cleanly. The body conclusion "the conditional-coverage value proposition is not CHMM-specific" is correct. This means one of the three structural use cases that the paper pivots to in the bootstrap-dominates-KS paragraph is *also not a CHMM contribution*. The remaining structural use cases (copula composition, parametric privacy) are generic to any parametric marginal generator — not specific to CHMM and not specific to multi-state regime-switching. **Suggested fix:** the paper needs a sentence somewhere in Discussion that states explicitly what is *uniquely* CHMM about the contribution, separated from what the CHMM scaffold instantiates that other multi-state or parametric generators also instantiate. As written, I cannot tell what the *uniquely* CHMM finding is.
4. **The OoS leverage-effect rejection at $K = 18$ is reported but its implication for the body framing is not fully drawn.** Discussion paragraph "Stylized-fact scope" reports (a) IS observed leverage $-0.135$ sits at the simulated $Q_5$ envelope (borderline non-rejection) and (b) OoS observed leverage $-0.214$ sits below the simulated $Q_5$ floor of $-0.190$ (rejection at $5\%$). The conclusion is that "skew-emission extensions remain the natural fix" and the leverage-effect axis is "out of scope under the body's symmetric per-state emissions." But this interacts with the abstract claim that the paper reproduces "the three symmetric Cont (2001) stylized facts." The OoS leverage-effect rejection is not a generic out-of-scope statement — the *symmetric* stylized facts are reproduced; the *asymmetric* one is rejected on OoS. That is actually a finding, not a deferral, and should be reported as such. **Suggested fix:** reframe the leverage-effect paragraph as a substantive finding ("on the asymmetric leverage axis, CHMM at $K = 18$ produces a non-trivial signed signal via Markov state-mixing on IS but rejects observed OoS leverage at the $5\%$ level, demonstrating that a symmetric-emission CHMM is insufficient for the leverage axis even with mixture-induced asymmetry") rather than as a scope deferral.
5. **The "KS recalibration drops by $\sim 25$pp at $L = 20$" headline puts the body OoS KS pass rates in a different light than Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison} suggests.** From `sections/results.tex` line 114: "Read together with the asymp pass-rates of Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison}, the OoS panel under a temporally-aware null moves CHMM-N from a 'passes most of the time' generator to a 'passes about half the time' generator at $L = 20$." This is a frank admission and the body retains the asymptotic critical value because of "the cross-generator ranking is the robust comparison." But under a temporally-aware null, the headline "$80.5\%$ OoS KS at $K^\star = 3$" becomes "$\sim 58.6\%$ OoS KS" (Table~\ref{tab:ks_block_body}) — i.e., the model passes a temporally-aware KS test about half the time, not most of the time. **Suggested fix:** the body and abstract OoS KS numbers should be reported under both nulls (asymp + block-bootstrap), not just under asymp. A reader who only reads the abstract will take away "$80.5\%$" not "$58.6\%$ under a temporally-aware null."
6. **The multi-day CRPS DM "win" at $h = 20$ on SPY is a single-cell-significant finding that does not replicate.** From `sections/results.tex` line 111: "the body's $K = 18$ multi-day DM result is therefore $K = 18$-specific on SPY rather than a robust property of the CHMM scaffold across operating points and assets." This is correctly reported and the body headline pivot to "the substantive CHMM-vs-bootstrap differentiation is on the structural use cases" follows. But the paper's $h = 20$ DM result has been cited in the abstract / introduction in earlier drafts (judging by the Discussion's "deferred items" framing); confirm it is no longer cited as a contribution, and if the only place it survives is the results section, it should be presented as a non-replicating exploratory finding rather than a positive finding.
7. **The QuantGAN row at $0\%$ IS / OoS KS is a known weak control and the paper acknowledges this but the comparison is still in the body table.** Discussion paragraph "Baseline-implementation caveats" is honest about this ("we do not claim that a faithful reference-implementation re-run of \citet{wiese2020quantgan} would also fail") but the body Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison} still reports the $0\%$ row prominently. **Suggested fix:** either (a) move the in-house WGAN re-implementation rows to an appendix and replace the body deep-generative row with "deferred — see appendix and limitations", or (b) keep the row in the body but with a row label that clearly tags it as "in-house re-implementation, not reference run."

### Questions for Authors
1. What is *uniquely* CHMM about this paper? The bootstrap dominates raw KS; ML HSMM dominates raw KS at the regime-switching tier; MS-GARCH dominates conditional-VaR coverage; copula composition is generic to any parametric marginal. After all the "complementary" and "structural use case" framings, what is the empirical or methodological finding that is irreducible to (i) a multi-state regime-switching scaffold, (ii) a parametric marginal, (iii) a state-filter VaR pipeline, and (iv) a rank-based copula? Please answer in one paragraph in the Discussion.
2. The cross-ticker dominant-mode share IQR is $[0.66, 0.86]$ with minimum $0.33$ on NEM (a precious-metals miner with regime-introduction structure). On the four other below-median tickers (presumably also regime-introduction — please name them), does the rank-non-binding claim hold or fail? If it fails on $4/30$ tickers, the cross-ticker median framing is selecting on the success cases.
3. Why is $K = 18$ retained as a body operating point rather than $K_\text{eff} = 11$? The Discussion paragraph "Practical identifiability" effectively concedes that $K_\text{nominal} = 18$ is a parameter-counting artefact and $K_\text{nominal} = 11$ is the structurally cleaner expression of the same model. Reporting both is fine, but the body kurtosis-fidelity headline should be at $K_\text{eff}$ not $K_\text{nominal}$.
4. How does this paper differ from `\citet{alswaidan2026hybrid}` (the discrete-state predecessor referenced in Related Work) in terms of empirical capability? The Related Work says the present paper "recover the same target through the spectral mechanism," which sounds like a re-derivation rather than an improvement. What is the OoS KS / kurtosis / Christoffersen-cc differential between the discrete predecessor and the present continuous variant on SPY 2014–2024 / 2024–2026?
5. The cross-asset dependence-family choice ("Gaussian and Student-$t$ copulas are statistically indistinguishable on OoS at $N_\text{paths} = 200$") is an OoS-only null. Have you checked whether $N_\text{paths} = 1{,}000$ (the body single-asset path count) shifts the OoS off-diagonal MAE point estimates ($0.209$ Student-$t$ vs $0.202$ Gaussian) outside the simulation-noise floor? The "below the simulation-noise floor" reading depends on which floor is being used.

### Requested Experiments / Analyses
1. **Direct head-to-head with the discrete-state predecessor `\citet{alswaidan2026hybrid}`.** Same dataset, same OoS window, same metric panel. If the continuous variant is a strict empirical improvement, show it; if it is a different operating point, frame it that way; if the two are indistinguishable, that materially changes the contribution claim.
2. **A single comparison row that demonstrates a uniquely-CHMM contribution.** I am open to being convinced that something is uniquely CHMM-derived; the paper does not currently present such a row. Candidate construction: a parametric-privacy task (e.g., reproduce a target portfolio's return distribution to a target $W_1$ distance subject to a $k$-anonymity constraint on the IS sample) where CHMM, MS-GARCH, ML HSMM, the bootstrap, and the discrete predecessor all compete and one of them strictly wins.
3. **Re-report all OoS KS pass rates under the temporally-aware (block-bootstrap, $L = 20$) null in the body table and abstract.** The dual asymptotic + block-aware columns should be the body headline; the asymptotic-only headline overstates absolute pass rates.
4. **A subset analysis of the 30-ticker panel at the four below-median dominant-mode-share tickers** to confirm or refute the rank-non-binding claim on the failure cases. If $4/30$ tickers genuinely have dominant-mode share $\le 0.5$, the body framing should accommodate this rather than report the median in isolation.

### Minor Comments
1. The abstract sentence "ML HSMM dominates CHMM on raw OoS distributional fidelity at every operating point tried" is buried in paragraph 4; this is the most important honest framing in the abstract and should be no later than paragraph 2.
2. The phrase "structural use cases" is repeated $\sim 8$ times in the manuscript and is doing more work than the analysis supports given the MS-GARCH conditional-VaR counterfactual; consider replacing with more specific phrasing.
3. The introduction's contributions list (item (i): "We apply the textbook bilinear identity ...") should not start with "we apply a textbook identity" — that is not a contribution. Restate as the empirical instance and the rank diagnostic.
4. The conclusion's "the strongest single-window OoS KS row in the panel is ML HSMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ ($91.0\%$ OoS KS)" is the headline finding that the body has been working around. Either the conclusion should make this the lead and reframe CHMM as an alternative scaffold, or the body should justify why the title, abstract, and introduction lead with CHMM.
5. The companion-package URL in the conclusion (`https://github.com/altashly1/CHMM-paper`) should be checked for live-link integrity at submission; broken artefact-availability claims are a common reviewer flag.

### Recommendation
**Major Revision.** The mathematical scaffolding is correct, the empirical work is honest about its scope, and the analyses are reproducible — this is not a borderline-reject. But the framing burden is real: the paper's *contributions* relative to (a) the discrete-state predecessor, (b) ML HSMM, (c) MS-GARCH with the same state-filter pipeline, and (d) the iid bootstrap on raw KS need to be articulated more sharply, and the title/abstract should be reworked to match what the body actually shows.
