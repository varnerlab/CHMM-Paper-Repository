## Reviewer 1 (Moderate) — Senior Researcher, Regime-Switching Time-Series Models

### Summary
The paper proposes a continuous Hidden Markov Model (CHMM) as a reference synthetic-data generator for daily equity returns and demonstrates that, at $K = 18$ states, the model reproduces all three Cont stylized facts simultaneously on SPY: heavy-tailed marginal, negligible linear ACF, and slow decay of the absolute-return ACF. A spectral identity is given for the absolute-return ACF as a mixture of geometric decays at the non-unit eigenvalues of the transition matrix, which the authors use to recast the well-known Rydén et al. low-$K$ failure as a $K$-rank statement. Four emission variants (Gaussian, Student-t, Laplace, GED) share a unified ECM scaffold. Overall the paper is methodologically careful, the empirical study is comprehensive for a single-asset benchmark, and the contribution is incremental but useful. Suitable after revisions.

### Strengths
1. The unified ECM scaffold across four emission families (Section 3, Algorithm 1 in Appendix A) is a clean architectural choice. Reporting CHMM-N, CHMM-t, CHMM-L, and CHMM-GED on identical forward-backward recursions and quantile-based initialization isolates the emission family as the only architectural lever, which makes the kurtosis-axis comparison in Section 4 (CHMM-N undershoots, CHMM-t overshoots, CHMM-L matches, CHMM-GED interpolates) genuinely interpretable.
2. The spectral identity $\rho_{|G|}(\tau) = \sum_{k\ge 2} w_k \lambda_k^{\tau}$ in Section 3 (and the extended derivation in Appendix A.4 / Theorem A.1) is correctly stated, the lag-zero discontinuity is handled honestly (within-state variance ratio explicitly quantified), and the complex-eigenvalue / non-diagonalisable remarks anticipate obvious objections.
3. The robustness layer is thorough: ten-seed Monte Carlo, KS test-power calibration with the 90 percent "nearly correct" ceiling (Appendix B.1), block-bootstrap KS recalibration at three block lengths (B.2), and the multi-emission $K$-sweep (Appendix C.2). The seed-to-seed standard deviations are small relative to cross-generator gaps, which is the right benchmark.
4. Table 1 and the discussion in Section 4.3 are honest about the no-single-winner pattern: bootstrap dominates marginal fidelity, GARCH-$t$ dominates ACF-MAE, MS-GARCH sits in between, and CHMM occupies the joint corner. The paper does not overclaim dominance.
5. The companion package (CHMM-Model.jl) with a single-seed-root reproducer is good practice and rare in this literature.

### Weaknesses
1. **The $K = 18$ headline operating point is selected on a multi-objective rule that overlaps the OoS evaluation window** (Section 4.1, paragraph 2). The authors are admirably upfront that no held-out criterion picks $K = 18$ (held-out log-lik, BIC, HQC, CAIC pick $K^\star = 3$; AIC picks $6$; held-out KS picks $9$; pre-2020 held-out picks $K^\star = 6$). The paper still reports $K = 18$ as the headline. Recommendation: promote the $K^\star = 3$ row (or the pre-2020 $K^\star = 6$ row) as the principal operating point and demote $K = 18$ to a secondary "tail-fidelity" panel. At minimum, the abstract and the discussion's headline numbers should lead with $K^\star = 3$ or $K^\star = 6$ values; the current ordering reverses the authors' own held-out evidence.
2. **The novelty of the spectral identity is overstated relative to the cited prior art.** The bilinear cross-moment identity $\mathbb E[|G_t||G_{t+\tau}|] = \mathbf m^\top D\mathbf T^\tau \mathbf m$ and its eigendecomposition are explicitly attributed to Hamilton (1994), Krolzig (1997), and Timmermann (2000) (Section 3, Theory paragraph 1). The "rank statement on $\mathbf T - \mathbf 1\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$" is a one-line consequence of the spectral decomposition: the deflated matrix has rank at most $K - 1$ by construction, since the rank-1 stationary projector is being subtracted. The paper is honest in the body (Section 3, "Our contribution on this axis is...") but the abstract and introduction frame this more strongly than the framing in Section 3 supports. Recommendation: tone down the contribution claim in the abstract (replace "our contribution" with "we apply the standard spectral identity to..."), and in the intro third paragraph, state the rank claim as a corollary rather than as a result.
3. **The Christoffersen independence test is rejected for every CHMM variant on OoS at the 5 percent level** (Table A.7 in Appendix A.5), and the paper defers the conditional-VaR fix to a "companion paper" (Section 4.4 final paragraph; Section 5). This is a real problem: the most natural application of a regime-switching generator is precisely a regime-conditional VaR, and the body table in Section 4.4 reports passing Kupiec but does not foreground the conditional-coverage failure. Recommendation: either compute a one-step-ahead regime-conditional VaR via $\Pr(s_{t+1} | \text{history}_t)$ propagation (this is a standard exercise and would not require a companion paper), or move the Christoffersen rejection language from the appendix into Section 4.4 as a clearly labeled limitation, with the conditional-coverage failure recorded in Table 2 alongside Kupiec.
4. **The $K = 18$ vs. $K^\star = 3$ tension is unresolved on the discussion's own terms.** Table sensitivity (Appendix C.1) shows ACF-MAE essentially flat across $K \in \{3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21\}$ (range 0.0463 to 0.0534), which the authors correctly attribute to the rank constraint being non-binding at $K \ge 3$. But this is exactly the regime in which the spectral story does not require $K = 18$, so the headline $K = 18$ choice is being made for marginal-tail reasons, not for the spectral mechanism the abstract and intro foreground. Recommendation: rewrite the discussion's first paragraph to lead with the marginal-tail-at-moderate-$K$ result and demote the spectral mechanism to a supporting role.
5. **The CHMM-t cross-ticker kurtosis blow-up (NVDA 40.8, JNJ 104.1, JPM 17.6 in Table sensitivity-multi)** is acknowledged and partially fixed by the $1/\nu$ shrinkage at $\lambda = 20$, but the fix is introduced in the discussion and the cross-ticker table (Table 3 in Section 4.2 and Table C.3 in the appendix) still reports the unpenalised numbers. Either the cross-ticker table should be re-run with the shrinkage prior at $\lambda = 20$ (the authors' own recommended operational variant), or the unpenalised numbers should be removed in favour of the shrinkage version. Currently the body reports a known-bad operating point.
6. **The MS-GARCH parameter-matched comparison is missing from the body.** Table 1 reports MS-GARCH at $K = 2$ (with about $9$ free parameters) against CHMM at $K = 18$ (with about $359$ free parameters: $K^2 + 2K - 1$). The appendix (Appendix B.7) extends MS-GARCH to $K = 4$ ($24$ params) and $K = 6$ ($48$ params) and shows the ceiling does not move. This is the right extension, but it should appear in the body, not buried in the appendix, since "compare to MS-GARCH at matched $K$" is the most obvious objection to Table 1.

### Questions for Authors
1. The pre-2020 held-out re-selection (Appendix A.6) gives $K^\star = 6$ on both held-out log-lik and held-out KS. Have you considered making $K = 6$ the headline operating point? It is selected by held-out criteria, has $4 \times$ fewer parameters, and your own Section 4.1 says ACF-MAE is flat across the sweep. Why $K = 18$ rather than $K = 6$?
2. The spectral identity gives $\rho_{|G|}(\tau) = \sum_{k \ge 2} w_k \lambda_k^{\tau}$. At the fitted $\hat{\mathbf T}$ for $K = 18$, what is the effective rank of the absolute-return ACF representation? That is, how many of the $K - 1 = 17$ non-unit eigenvalues carry weight $w_k > 0.01$ in the empirical fit on SPY? If the answer is, say, $3$ or $4$, then the paper's binding-rank story for CHMM is not at $K = 17$ but at the effective rank, and an explicit reduced-rank parameterization (low-rank $\mathbf T$ with explicit spectral budget) would be the natural follow-up.
3. The pooled-archive VaR construction in Section 4.4 (Christoffersen panel) takes the $\alpha$-quantile of $\mathrm{vec}(\text{sim archive})$. This is an unconditional VaR by construction. What is the conditional-VaR Christoffersen panel under $\Pr(s_{t+1} | \text{history}_t)$ propagation? This is a standard exercise that the paper should report.
4. The OoS window (2024-01-04 through 2026-04-20) covers the AI-driven NVDA rally and a rate-cycle reversal. The OoS observed kurtosis (5.29) is materially lower than IS (7.68); this is unusual. Have you tested OoS robustness under a rolling-origin design (e.g., 5 walk-forward windows of about 1 year each), and does the $K = 18$ vs. $K = 3$ ranking flip under any of those?

### Requested Experiments / Analyses
1. **Single-window walk-forward robustness.** Replace the single $2024 \to 2026$ OoS window with a rolling-origin design: 5 to 8 walk-forward windows of length 250 trading days each, each preceded by a 5-year training window. Report Table 1 metrics aggregated across windows. The current single-window design is the most fragile element of the empirical study.
2. **Conditional VaR panel under regime forecast propagation.** For CHMM-N at $K = 3$ and $K = 18$, compute one-step-ahead $\widehat{\text{VaR}}_t(\alpha) = F^{-1}_{\sum_k \Pr(s_{t+1} = k|\mathcal F_t)\, b_k}(\alpha)$ where $\mathcal F_t$ is the filtration and $b_k$ are per-state emission densities. Report Kupiec, Christoffersen-ind, and Christoffersen-cc on the OoS window for both $K$ values. This is the natural CHMM-specific contribution and should be in the paper.
3. **Effective spectral rank of fitted $\hat{\mathbf T}$.** Add a panel reporting $|w_k|$ vs. $|\lambda_k|$ for $k = 2, \ldots, K$ at the headline fit, ordered by $|\lambda_k|$. This will let readers see whether the $K = 18$ fit actually uses 17 non-unit modes or only a few, which speaks directly to the rank-statement framing.
4. **Bigger cross-ticker universe.** Extend Table 3 from 6 tickers to at least 30 (a sector-balanced selection from the S&P 500 plus a handful of non-equity ETFs). The current 6-ticker panel is too small to support the "cross-ticker generalisation" framing.

### Minor Comments
1. The abstract is dense and front-loads the spectral mechanism; consider leading with the empirical headline (KS percentages and ACF-MAE comparison) and putting the spectral mechanism in the second sentence.
2. Figure 1 (pipeline schematic) is helpful but the bracket labels "marginals" and "dependence" overlap visually with the box labels. Consider raising the bracket text or thinning the boxes.
3. Table 1 row "CHMM-N ($K^\star = 3$)" sits between MS-GARCH and "CHMM-N ($K = 18$)"; readers will scan it as a CHMM row. Consider a small vertical rule or a "reference operating point" sub-header to set it apart.
4. Section 4.3, paragraph "The headline KS pass rates are stable...": ten-seed standard deviations are reported as $\pm 1.79$ etc.; consider clarifying that these are seed-level standard deviations of the per-seed pass rate, not standard errors of the headline pass rate (an easy point of confusion).
5. The em-dash usage "---" appears to be the manuscript's own choice (and is repeated throughout). Some venues prefer commas / colons / parens for the same job; check the target venue's style guide.

### Recommendation
**Major Revision.** The contribution is real and the empirical study is more thorough than typical for the venue, but the headline operating point ($K = 18$) is at odds with the authors' own held-out evidence ($K^\star = 3$ or $6$), and the conditional-VaR result that would make the regime-switching story land is deferred to a companion paper. With $K^\star = 3$ or $K^\star = 6$ promoted to the headline and a regime-conditional VaR panel added, this becomes a strong Minor Revision.

## Reviewer 2 (Hard) — Constraint-Based Time-Series Modeling and Risk Backtesting

### Summary
The authors fit a continuous Hidden Markov Model with $K$ regimes to daily SPY excess log returns using Baum-Welch / ECM under quantile-based initialization, with four interchangeable emission families, and benchmark against i.i.d., GARCH-family, MS-GARCH, semi-Markov, and QuantGAN baselines. The headline claims are (i) joint reproduction of three stylized facts at $K = 18$, (ii) a closed-form spectral identity for the absolute-return ACF that recasts Rydén's low-$K$ failure as a rank statement, and (iii) clean Pipeline-A and Pipeline-B reproduction of marginals and rank-based copula dependence. The mathematics is broadly correct, the simulation infrastructure is reproducible, and the comparisons are reasonable in shape. My concerns are primarily about (a) the legitimacy of the headline $K$-selection given the held-out evidence, (b) the strength of the spectral-mechanism claim relative to what the data actually shows, and (c) the absence of a regime-conditional VaR despite the authors having every ingredient for it.

### Strengths
1. The unified Algorithm A.1 is clean and the ECM monotonicity argument (Proposition A.1) is correctly formulated under bracket compactness; the per-state $\nu_k$ and $p_k$ search over a compact bracket is a standard MM-style argument that does not require new theory but is correctly invoked. Good practice.
2. The block-bootstrap KS recalibration (Appendix B.2) is the right way to address the i.i.d. assumption embedded in the asymptotic two-sample test. The authors compute critical values at three block lengths and confirm GARCH(1,1)'s drop from $26.8 \to 6.8\text{--}17.4$ percent under block-aware testing while CHMM moves only modestly. This is the kind of calibration most regime-switching papers in the literature skip and is appropriately surfaced here.
3. The numerical full-rank check on $\hat{\mathbf T}$ at $K = 18$ across the four families (Table A.1) is a genuinely useful identifiability diagnostic. CHMM-t at condition number $1620$ is borderline, but the authors flag this and connect it to per-state $\nu_k$ ECM lower-bracket pinning. The transparency is appreciated.
4. The CRPS column with Diebold-Mariano $p$-values (Table 1, Section 4.3) is the right proper-scoring-rule complement to KS. The authors show CHMM-N at $K = 18$ ties the four nearest competitors at $|d| < 0.002$ and significantly beats the Gaussian negative control. This rules out the most obvious "KS pass rate is a bad metric" objection.
5. The penalised CHMM-t with exponential $1/\nu_k$ shrinkage (Section 5; Appendix A.6) is the principled regularization path for the per-state $\nu_k$ overshoot, and the rate sweep $\lambda \in \{0, 5, 20, 50, 100, 200\}$ is reported. Good methodological hygiene.

### Weaknesses
1. **The state-count selection is the most serious methodological issue.** The body reports $K = 18$ as the headline; the held-out re-selection (Appendix A.6) shows that *no* held-out criterion picks $K = 18$. Held-out log-likelihood, BIC, HQC, and CAIC pick $K^\star = 3$; AIC picks $6$; held-out KS picks $9$; pre-2020 held-out (no rate-hike confound) picks $K^\star = 6$. The authors explicitly state "the $K = 18$ choice is best read as a multi-metric one that weights tail fidelity (kurtosis, AD) and downstream VaR behaviour alongside likelihood". The downstream VaR behaviour is evaluated on the same OoS window the multi-metric criterion uses. This is selection-on-the-test-set in spirit if not in letter, and the paper does not currently make a clean held-out case for $K = 18$. Required: either (a) demote $K = 18$ to a secondary panel and lead with the held-out-selected $K^\star = 3$ or $K^\star = 6$, or (b) define a tail-fidelity criterion on a slice that does not overlap the OoS evaluation window and re-select $K$ from it.
2. **The "spectral identity" is essentially a textbook calculation.** The identity $\rho_{|G|}(\tau) = \sum_{k \ge 2} w_k \lambda_k^{\tau}$ with $w_k$ as bilinear projections of $\mathbf m$ onto eigenspaces is a one-page consequence of the standard regime-switching moment identity (Timmermann 2000, equation 6 and surrounding). The rank statement on $\mathbf T - \mathbf 1 \bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$ has rank at most $K - 1$ as an immediate corollary of the spectral decomposition. The paper acknowledges this in Section 3 (Theory paragraph 1) but the abstract and intro phrasing ("Our contribution on this axis is the explicit recasting...") oversells it. Required: in the abstract, replace "Our contribution on this axis is..." with neutral language that does not claim novelty for a calculation the body attributes to Timmermann (2000). The empirical calibration of which $K$ matches the data is the substantive contribution; that should be the headline framing.
3. **The MS-GARCH baseline at $K = 2$ in Table 1 is parameter-undermatched and not the right comparison.** $K = 2$ MS-GARCH has about 9 free parameters; $K = 18$ CHMM-N has $K^2 + 2K - 1 = 359$ free parameters. The MS-GARCH $K = 4, 6$ extensions reported in Appendix B.7 do not close the gap, but they are the right comparison and should appear in the body. More importantly, CHMM at the held-out-selected $K^\star = 3$ has about 14 free parameters, comparable to MS-GARCH $K = 2$, and the body Table 1 already shows CHMM-N $K^\star = 3$ at 89.7 percent IS KS vs. MS-GARCH $K = 2$ at 27.7 percent. This is the right headline comparison and demolishes MS-GARCH at matched parameter count; the body framing buries it.
4. **The SM-CHMM "foil" is fit by a Viterbi plug-in estimator** (Appendix B.7, "SM-CHMM" paragraph and the explicit caveat). The conclusion drawn from this row is "SM-CHMM is weaker than the headline CHMM rows on every metric", and the body abstract / intro use this to argue against the semi-Markov route. The caveat in Appendix B.7 is appropriate but the argument the body makes does not survive it: a maximum-likelihood HSMM (Yu 2010) at $K = 18$ on this universe is the correct comparison, and the paper does not include it. The body argument that "moderate-$K$ continuous HMM achieves the same end through the spectral richness of $\mathbf T$ and the additional mixture components" is not supported by a Viterbi plug-in HSMM, only by an ML HSMM. Required: either (a) run an ML HSMM at $K = 18$ with explicit Pareto sojourn (the authors already have this in their companion-paper code base, per Appendix B.7), or (b) remove the body framing that contrasts CHMM with semi-Markov on capacity grounds, and limit the SM-CHMM panel to a faithfulness check.
5. **The conditional-coverage Christoffersen panel rejects every CHMM variant on OoS** (Table A.7), and the body Section 4.4 buries this. The Kupiec result is foregrounded but Christoffersen-cc fails at OoS $\alpha = 0.05$ for CHMM-N ($\text{LR}_{\text{cc}} = 9.08$, $p = 0.011$), CHMM-t ($7.74$, $0.02$), CHMM-L ($7.74$, $0.02$), and CHMM-GED ($9.08$, $0.011$). Required: (a) report Christoffersen-ind and Christoffersen-cc in Table 4 (the body VaR table), not just in the appendix, and (b) compute a one-step-ahead conditional-VaR panel under $\Pr(s_{t+1} | \mathcal F_t)$ propagation. The latter is the genuine CHMM-specific contribution that the paper does not deliver.
6. **The cross-ticker generalisation in Table 3 reports the unpenalised CHMM-t** (kurtosis blow-ups: NVDA 46.4, JNJ 99.66, JPM 18.82) with a footnote that the penalised variant is the recommended operational variant. The body table reports a known-bad operating point with a forward reference to a fix that is not in the table. Required: re-run Table 3 with the penalised CHMM-t at $\lambda = 20$ and compare to Table C.3 in the appendix (which extends the kurtosis column across the four families). The current table mis-represents the operational variant the paper itself recommends.
7. **The Pipeline-B Student-t copula degree-of-freedom selection** uses a 10-point grid $\{2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 10, 15, 20, 30\}$. The reported optimum is $\nu^\star = 6$ with neighbours at $\nu = 5$ ($-14.10$ profile-LL units) and $\nu = 8$ ($-9.10$). A finer grid in the $\nu \in [4, 12]$ range and a Wilks confidence interval would give a tighter sense of the optimum, particularly given the OoS Gaussian-vs-Student-t indistinguishability ($0.202$ vs. $0.209$ off-diag MAE). Required: report a profile-LL based 95 percent CI for $\hat\nu$ on the IS sample.

### Questions for Authors
1. **Why does $K^\star$ not coincide between the spectral-mechanism narrative and the held-out criterion?** The spectral identity says ACF reproduction is supplied at any $K \ge 2$ and your sensitivity table (Table C.1) confirms ACF-MAE is flat across $K$. So the binding axis is the marginal mixture, which is a 1990s mixture-model question, not a regime-switching question. Why is this paper framed as a regime-switching contribution rather than as a finite-mixture-model contribution with a Markov dependence layer that does not bind?
2. The full-rank check in Table A.1 reports $\hat{\mathbf T}$ smallest singular values in the range $[0.0007, 0.017]$ and condition numbers up to $1620$. CHMM-t at condition $1620$ is borderline. Does the bootstrap standard error on $\lambda_2$ (the dominant decay mode) span an order of magnitude under resampling? If so, the spectral story has more uncertainty than the deterministic $\rho_{|G|}(\tau) = \sum w_k \lambda_k^{\tau}$ form suggests.
3. The OoS off-diagonal MAE jumps from $0.027$ IS to $0.209$ OoS (Pipeline B). The per-pair table (A.4) attributes this to a JNJ-equity-factor regime shift. Have you tested whether the IS Pipeline-B OoS off-diagonal MAE also degrades on a synthetic OoS window drawn from the IS distribution itself (a placebo)? If so, by how much?
4. The penalised CHMM-t at $\lambda = 20$ recovers kurtosis near observed at a 1pp KS cost. Does the same $\lambda = 20$ rate generalise across NVDA, JNJ, JPM, AAPL, QQQ, or does each ticker need its own $\lambda$? If the latter, the operational story is more complex than the body suggests.
5. The Bulla 2006 HSMM result was at $K \in \{2, 3\}$ on monthly returns; your Section 1 / 5 framing presents your $K = 18$ daily-return CHMM as "achiev[ing] the same end" at "moderate state resolution." Is this an apples-to-apples claim? What is the parameter count of Bulla 2006's $K = 3$ HSMM versus your $K = 18$ CHMM-N?

### Requested Experiments / Analyses
1. **ML HSMM at matched $K$ as the correct semi-Markov foil.** Implement (or use the companion-paper code base) an explicit-duration HSMM with per-state Pareto sojourn at $K \in \{3, 6, 18\}$ on SPY IS. Report Table 1 metrics. Without this, the body claim that CHMM "achieves the same end" as the semi-Markov route through eigenvalue richness is not supported.
2. **Conditional-VaR Christoffersen panel under regime forecast propagation.** Compute $\widehat{\text{VaR}}_t(\alpha)$ as the $\alpha$-quantile of $\sum_k \Pr(s_{t+1} = k | \mathcal F_t) b_k(\cdot)$ at each $t$ on the OoS window. Report Kupiec, Christoffersen-ind, and Christoffersen-cc for CHMM-N at $K \in \{3, 18\}$. Required to substantiate the regime-switching value proposition.
3. **$K^\star = 3$ versus $K = 18$ head-to-head as the headline.** Move the $K^\star = 3$ row in Table 1 from "reference" to "headline", and put $K = 18$ as a secondary panel. The revised abstract should lead with the $K^\star = 3$ numbers ($89.7$ IS KS, $80.1$ OoS KS, ACF-MAE $0.0467$, kurtosis $3.86$). The revised Section 4 should report both side by side and let the reader pick the operating point. This is a substantive reframing, not a cosmetic one.
4. **Effective spectral rank diagnostic.** Plot $|w_k|$ versus $|\lambda_k|$ for $k = 2, \ldots, K$ at the fitted $\hat{\mathbf T}$ (CHMM-N at $K = 18$ and at $K = 3$). The figure will show how many decay modes carry non-trivial weight; my prior is that 2 to 4 modes carry $> 95$ percent of the spectral mass, which would change the framing of "moderate state resolution" considerably.

### Minor Comments
1. Section 3, equation (5) defines $w_k = c_k / \sigma_{|G|}^2$ but $c_k$ is allowed to be negative (the bilinear product can flip sign for non-symmetric eigenvectors). The "weights" language is slightly misleading; the $w_k$ are real coefficients, not non-negative weights. Consider "coefficients" in place of "weights" or "$\rho_{|G|}(\tau) = \sum c_k \lambda_k^\tau / \sigma_{|G|}^2$" as the explicit form.
2. Algorithm A.1 line 17 (transition update) is computed before the M-step branch but is shared across families. State this explicitly in the caption (currently the reader has to deduce it from the line ordering).
3. Section 4.4 paragraph 2: the unconditional Kupiec statistic at $T = 572$ takes integer-breach-count values. This is correctly explained in the table caption but the text reads as if the values "$\text{LR}_\text{uc} = 3.83$" are model-specific point estimates; consider making the integer-grid effect explicit in the prose.
4. References to "Pipeline A" and "Pipeline B" recur dozens of times. Consider a one-sentence reminder at the top of Section 4 (e.g., "Pipeline A: per-asset CHMM, single-asset metrics. Pipeline B: per-asset CHMM marginals + Student-t copula on ranks.").
5. The companion-paper citation `alswaidan2026smchmm` is "Manuscript in preparation"; some venues do not allow citations to unpublished work. Consider preparing the companion as a preprint before submission.

### Recommendation
**Major Revision.** The empirical work is genuinely substantial and the multi-emission scaffold is a clean contribution. But (a) the $K = 18$ headline does not survive held-out scrutiny on the authors' own appendix evidence, (b) the spectral-mechanism framing oversells a textbook calculation, and (c) the regime-conditional VaR that would make the CHMM story land is deferred. With (a) reframed to lead with the held-out-selected $K^\star$, (b) tonally adjusted, (c) actually delivered in this paper, and the SM-CHMM ML re-fit added, this is a strong paper.

## Reviewer 3 (Very Hard) — Skeptical Methodologist (Published Competing Methods in Regime-Switching and Heavy-Tailed Time-Series)

### Summary
The paper claims that a continuous HMM at $K = 18$ states reproduces the three Cont stylized facts simultaneously, and packages this as a synthetic-data generator with four interchangeable emission families and a copula dependence layer. The contribution is real but the framing is generous, the headline operating point is selection-bias-adjacent, and at least three obvious comparisons that would change the qualitative reading are missing or relegated to caveated appendix rows. I do not recommend the paper for the target venue in its current form. With a substantial reframing and the missing comparisons added I would consider it a positive contribution to the regime-switching literature, but not a methodological breakthrough.

### Strengths
1. The reproducibility infrastructure (single seed root, per-experiment sub-seed map, companion Julia package, single-script rebuild) is among the best I have seen in the regime-switching literature. The CHMM-Model.jl package and the explicit additive sub-seed rule (Appendix A.1) is the correct way to do this.
2. The honest reporting of the held-out $K^\star = 3$ and the pre-2020 held-out $K^\star = 6$ (Appendix A.6) is appreciated. Most papers in this literature would have buried this.
3. The KS test power calibration (Appendix B.1) anchoring the OoS KS pass rates against the 90 percent "nearly correct" ceiling is the right move and lets me read the 80-86 percent CHMM OoS pass rates as a serious fidelity claim rather than a low-power artefact.
4. The CHMM-GED variant with bracketed per-state $p_k$ is genuinely interesting and produces a non-trivial structural finding (bimodal Gaussian-bulk / Laplace-tail partition that replicates across seeds and tickers, Appendix C.2 and A.3). This is the part of the paper I find most original.

### Weaknesses
1. **The headline operating point is chosen by a multi-objective rule that includes the OoS evaluation window** (Section 4.1, paragraph 2 explicitly: "this multi-objective rule uses the same 2024-2026 window later employed for VaR evaluation"). This is selection-on-the-test-set in spirit. The authors provide a clean held-out re-selection on a 2022-2023 slice (which itself has rate-hike issues) and a strictly pre-2020 slice (which selects $K^\star = 6$). Neither selects $K = 18$. The body should not lead with $K = 18$. Required: either move $K = 18$ to a "tail-fidelity reference" panel and lead with $K^\star = 3$ or $K^\star = 6$, or remove the K = 18 headline entirely. The current framing is not defensible.
2. **The spectral identity is a known result.** The bilinear cross-moment identity $\mathbb E[|G_t||G_{t+\tau}|] = \mathbf m^\top D \mathbf T^\tau \mathbf m$ and its eigendecomposition are textbook material, attributed correctly in Section 3 to Hamilton (1994), Krolzig (1997), Timmermann (2000). The "rank statement on the deflated transition matrix" follows in one line from the spectral decomposition. The novelty claim in the abstract ("Our contribution on this axis is the explicit recasting...") and the introduction ("(i) a closed-form mixture-of-eigenvalues identity") is overstated. Required: rewrite the abstract and contribution list (Section 1, last paragraph) to claim only what is genuinely novel. The empirical calibration of $K$ at which the temporal axis decouples from the marginal is the substantive contribution; the spectral identity is not.
3. **The semi-Markov comparison is rigged.** The body claim is that the CHMM at moderate $K$ "achieves the same end" as the semi-Markov route at lower complexity (intro paragraph 3; discussion paragraph 1). This claim rests on the SM-CHMM rows in Table B.5, which the authors themselves caveat as a Viterbi-AR(1) plug-in estimator that is not maximum likelihood for the underlying HSMM. A correct ML HSMM at matched $K$ (Yu 2010 forward-backward over (state, duration) pairs) is the right comparison, and its absence is conspicuous given that the authors have a companion paper on exactly this topic (`alswaidan2026smchmm`). Required: include the ML HSMM, or remove the framing that contrasts CHMM with HSMM on capacity grounds.
4. **The conditional-coverage Christoffersen test rejects every CHMM at OoS** (Table A.7), and the body table (Table 4) does not report Christoffersen-ind or Christoffersen-cc. The footnote in Section 4.4 acknowledges this and defers the regime-conditional VaR to a "companion paper." This is the wrong call. The CHMM's only structural advantage over GARCH is that it can produce regime-conditional VaR through latent-state forecast propagation; without that result, the paper's risk-management value proposition collapses to "tighter VaR envelope than GARCH on Kupiec at one window." Required: either deliver the conditional-VaR panel in this paper or reframe it as a "synthetic-data generator" with no risk-management claims, and remove the Kupiec / VaR discussion from the body.
5. **The cross-ticker generalisation is too narrow.** Five additional tickers (NVDA, JNJ, JPM, AAPL, QQQ) plus GLD as a non-US-equity probe is not a generalisation panel; it is a small spot-check. The body claims "cross-ticker generalisation" (Section 4.2 title) but the result is "two of six tickers fail with OoS KS below 60 percent." The body characterises this as a stationarity-scope artefact, but the same scope concern applies to the SPY headline OoS window. Required: extend the universe to at least 30 tickers (sector-balanced) and report aggregate generalisation statistics, or rename the section to "Spot-check on five additional tickers" and remove the generalisation framing.
6. **The Pipeline-B OoS off-diagonal MAE of $0.209$ is an order of magnitude larger than IS $0.027$**, attributed to a JNJ-equity-factor regime shift (Appendix C.4). The body discussion treats this as a stationarity artefact, but the rolling-window refit only reduces it to $0.185$ (Appendix C.3, Table C.4). A factor-of-7 IS-to-OoS degradation in the dependence-layer fidelity is a serious finding. The body abstract reports "$0.027$ IS off-diagonal MAE" without the OoS counterpart; this is selectively favourable reporting. Required: report both IS and OoS off-diagonal MAE in the abstract and the conclusion. The OoS number is the operationally relevant one.
7. **The CHMM-t kurtosis blow-up on individual tickers** (Table sensitivity-multi: NVDA 40.8, JNJ 104.1, JPM 17.6 against observed 5.5, 9.0, 7.6) is not adequately addressed. The bracket sweep shows the overshoot is not lower-bracket pinning, but the authors' own Appendix A.3 shows that two states ($\nu_2 = 2.19$, $\nu_4 = 2.10$) sit at the lower edge for SPY, and the cross-ticker pattern suggests this is structural for some tickers. The proposed fix (1/$\nu$ shrinkage at $\lambda = 20$) is introduced in the discussion and not applied to the cross-ticker table. Required: either re-run the cross-ticker table with the recommended operational variant, or remove the unpenalised CHMM-t cross-ticker numbers as not operationally meaningful.

### Questions for Authors
1. Why is the $K = 18$ operating point preserved as the headline given that no held-out criterion in your own Appendix A.6 selects it? The minimal honest answer is "we picked it because it gives the best headline numbers"; if so, the abstract should say so.
2. The rank statement on $\mathbf T - \mathbf 1\bar{\boldsymbol\pi}^\top$ has rank exactly $K - 1$ generically (Table A.1 confirms this empirically: rank $= 17 / 17$ for all four families). Generically, the deflated matrix has rank exactly $K - 1$. So the "rank statement" is "the deflated matrix has the rank it generically has." Why is this presented as a contribution?
3. Why is the SM-CHMM row in Table B.5 fit by a Viterbi-AR(1) plug-in rather than ML? You have a companion paper on ML HSMM (`alswaidan2026smchmm`); inclusion would strengthen the body claim that CHMM "achieves the same end" as the semi-Markov route.
4. Conditional VaR through latent-state forecast propagation $\sum_k \Pr(s_{t+1} = k | \mathcal F_t) b_k(\cdot)$ is a 50-line computation given your existing forward-algorithm code. Why is it deferred to a companion paper rather than included here? Without it, the VaR panel in Section 4.4 is doing roughly the same work as a single-distribution VaR fitted from the IS sample.
5. Why is the abstract's "$93.6\text{--}95.6$ percent IS KS, $80.8\text{--}85.7$ percent OoS KS" reported only at $K = 18$, when the CHMM-N $K^\star = 3$ row in the same table reports $89.7$ percent IS / $80.1$ percent OoS at one-quarter the parameter count?

Move $K = 18$ to a "tail-fidelity operating point" subsection. The current ordering is not defensible.

### Minor Comments
1. Several places (abstract, Section 1, Section 5) describe $K = 18$ as "moderate state resolution." Compared to the regime-switching literature where $K = 2\text{--}3$ is standard and $K = 4\text{--}6$ is on the high end, $K = 18$ is large, not moderate. The "moderate" framing is editorial and should be removed.
2. The companion paper `alswaidan2026smchmm` is cited but is in preparation. Some venues prohibit citation to unpublished work; check the target's policy.
3. The pipeline schematic (Figure 1) is a minor visual issue: the bracket labels "marginals" and "dependence" sit above the boxes and the rendering may overlap on some viewers.
4. Table A.7 (Christoffersen) is in the appendix but the body Section 4.4 references it tangentially. Consider promoting at least the OoS-Christoffersen-cc column to the body Table 4.
5. Section 4.3 paragraph "The headline finding...": "with $K - 1 = 17$ non-unit eigenvalues at the operating point, the mixture-of-eigenvalues sum reproduces the slow absolute-return ACF that the Rydén $K = 2\text{--}3$ Gaussian setup could not recover." This conflates two things: the rank-17 deflated matrix and the *effective* number of decay modes carrying weight. Without the effective-rank diagnostic (Reviewer 2's request 4), this sentence overclaims.

### Recommendation
**Reject in current form, encourage resubmission.** The contribution is real (CHMM-GED is genuinely interesting and the unified scaffold is clean), the empirical infrastructure is reproducible, and the paper is well written. But the headline operating point is not held-out-defensible, the spectral-mechanism novelty claim is overstated, the conditional-VaR contribution that would substantiate the regime-switching value proposition is deferred, and the semi-Markov foil is fit by a non-ML estimator. With a substantial reframing (lead with $K^\star = 3$ or $K^\star = 6$, drop the spectral-novelty framing, deliver the conditional VaR, run the ML HSMM, expand the cross-ticker panel) the paper becomes a strong fit for the target venue. As-is, the headline framing oversells the contribution and the paper would not survive an aggressive reviewer at the target venue.
